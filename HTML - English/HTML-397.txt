<p><b>Rances Barthelemy</b> (born 25 June 1986) is a Cuban <a href="/wiki/Professional_boxer" class="mw-redirect" title="Professional boxer">professional boxer</a>. He is a former two-weight world champion, having held the <a href="/wiki/IBF" class="mw-redirect" title="IBF">IBF</a> <a href="/wiki/Junior_lightweight" class="mw-redirect" title="Junior lightweight">junior lightweight</a> title in 2014, and the IBF <a href="/wiki/Lightweight" title="Lightweight">lightweight</a> title from 2015 to 2016. Rances has two brothers, <a href="/wiki/Yan_Bartelem%C3%AD" title="Yan Bartelemí">Yan Bartelemí</a> and Leduan Barthelemy, who are also  professional boxers.
</p><p>Barthelemy was a standout amateur, competing in more than 200 fights and winning a Cuban junior national championship.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Barthelemy defected to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> prior to the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He left Cuba over frustration with how his brother Yan’s career was developing and the implications that it could have for his own future in the sport.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rances_Barthelemy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Barthelemy defeated <a href="/wiki/Arash_Usmanee" title="Arash Usmanee">Arash Usmanee</a> on 4 January 2013 by a controversial twelve-round <a href="/wiki/Unanimous_decision" title="Unanimous decision">unanimous decision</a> which many observers thought Usmanee had won and became the #2 contender for the <a href="/wiki/IBF" class="mw-redirect" title="IBF">IBF</a> <a href="/wiki/Junior_lightweight" class="mw-redirect" title="Junior lightweight">junior lightweight</a> title.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Barthelemy would later become the #1 contender by defeating Fahsai Sakkreerin on 21 June 2013 by second-round <a href="/wiki/Knockout" title="Knockout">knockout</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Barthelemy originally defeated <a href="/wiki/Argenis_Mendez" title="Argenis Mendez">Argenis Mendez</a> on 3 January 2014 to win the title,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> but the win was considered controversial, as Barthelemy knocked Mendez out seconds after the bell to signal the end of the second round came. Mendez and his team filled an appeal to the Minnesota Combative Sports Commission and IBF shortly after the fight,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and after the appeal was successful on 30 January 2014, the result was changed to a no decision and Mendez was reinstated as the IBF junior lightweight champion.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Barthelemy later defeated Mendez in a rematch on 10 July 2014 via unanimous decision to win the title.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He vacated his title in February 2015 in anticipation of moving up in weight category.
</p><p>In his next contest, fighting in the super lightweight division against <a href="/wiki/Antonio_DeMarco" title="Antonio DeMarco">Antonio DeMarco</a> at the MGM Grand Garden Arena in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>, on 21 June 2015, Barthelemy gained a convincing victory. Barthelemy showed his versatility early, coming out orthodox before switching up to southpaw midway throughout the first round. Barthelemy scored a knockdown with a huge left in the fourth round, but DeMarco survived the round. Barthelemy lost a point in the ninth round for excessive low blows, but it wasn’t enough to affect the outcome as he won 99–89 on all three cards.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
