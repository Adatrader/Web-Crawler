	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Copyright law of Poland</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">&#160;&#160;(Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Polish_copyright_law&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="Polish copyright law">Polish copyright law</a>)</span></div>
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="box-No_footnotes plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-style ambox-No_footnotes" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Text_document_with_red_question_mark.svg/40px-Text_document_with_red_question_mark.svg.png" decoding="async" width="40" height="40" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Text_document_with_red_question_mark.svg/60px-Text_document_with_red_question_mark.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Text_document_with_red_question_mark.svg/80px-Text_document_with_red_question_mark.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="48" data-file-height="48" /></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This article includes a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">list of references</a>, related reading or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:External_links" title="Wikipedia:External links">external links</a>, <b>but its sources remain unclear because it lacks <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources#Inline_citations" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">inline citations</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Fact_and_Reference_Check" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Fact and Reference Check">improve</a> this article by <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:When_to_cite" title="Wikipedia:When to cite">introducing</a> more precise citations.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">August 2008</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Polish copyright law</b> is regulated by the act from 1994.
</p><p>The first Polish <a href="/wiki/Copyright_law" class="mw-redirect" title="Copyright law">copyright law</a> act has been enacted in 1926 (although copyright issues have been regulated on territories of <a href="/wiki/Partitioned_Poland" class="mw-redirect" title="Partitioned Poland">partitioned Poland</a> in the 19th century by governments of the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Empire</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Austro-Hungary" class="mw-redirect" title="Austro-Hungary">Austro-Hungary</a>, beginning with <a href="/wiki/Copyright_law_of_Russia#Copyright_in_Tsarist_Russia" title="Copyright law of Russia">the 1828 copyright law passed in Tsarist Russia</a>). Poland signed the <a href="/wiki/Berne_Convention_for_the_Protection_of_Literary_and_Artistic_Works" class="mw-redirect" title="Berne Convention for the Protection of Literary and Artistic Works">Berne Convention</a> in 1919, soon after regaining its independence in 1918.
</p><p>The main acts that have regulated Polish copyright law have been:
</p>
<dl><dd><dl><dd><ul><li><i><a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/pl:Prawo_autorskie_(ustawa_z_29_marca_1926_r.)" class="extiw" title="s:pl:Prawo autorskie (ustawa z 29 marca 1926 r.)">Polish Copyright Law from 1926 (Polish original, amended version from 1935)</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dziennik_Ustaw" title="Dziennik Ustaw">Dz.U.</a> RP 1935; Pos. 260)</i></li>
<li><i><a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/pl:Prawo_autorskie_(ustawa_z_10_lipca_1952_r.)" class="extiw" title="s:pl:Prawo autorskie (ustawa z 10 lipca 1952 r.)">Polish Copyright Law from 1926 (Polish original, amended in 1952)</a> (Dz.U.52.34.234)</i></li>
<li><i><a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/pl:Prawo_autorskie_(ustawa)" class="extiw" title="s:pl:Prawo autorskie (ustawa)">Polish Copyright Law from 4 February 1994 (Polish original)</a></i> (Dz. U. nr 80 z 2000r. poz. 904)</li>
<li><i><a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Polish_Copyright_Law_from_4_February_1994" class="extiw" title="s:Polish Copyright Law from 4 February 1994">Polish Copyright Law from 4 February 1994 (English)</a></i></li></ul></dd></dl></dd></dl>
<p>Polish copyright law complies to a large extent with legislation in European Union, see <a href="/wiki/Information_Society_Directive" title="Information Society Directive">EU Copyright Directive</a>.
</p><p>According to the Art.3 of <a href="/wiki/File:Prawo_autorskie_1935.pdf" title="File:Prawo autorskie 1935.pdf">copyright law of March 29, 1926</a> (valid until 1952) and Art. 2 of <a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/pl:Prawo_autorskie_(ustawa_z_10_lipca_1952_r.)" class="extiw" title="s:pl:Prawo autorskie (ustawa z 10 lipca 1952 r.)">copyright law of July 10, 1952</a> of the People's Republic of Poland, all photographs by Polish photographers (or published for the first time in Poland or simultaneously in Poland and abroad) printed without a clear copyright notice before the law was changed on May 23, 1994 are public domain. Status of those photographs did not change after <a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/pl:Prawo_autorskie_(ustawa)" class="extiw" title="s:pl:Prawo autorskie (ustawa)">Polish Copyright Law of February 4, 1994</a> was enacted. (See: <a href="/wiki/Template:PD-Polish" class="mw-redirect" title="Template:PD-Polish">Template:PD-Polish</a>)
</p><p>According to the <a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/pl:Prawo_autorskie_(ustawa)" class="extiw" title="s:pl:Prawo autorskie (ustawa)">Polish Copyright Law of February 4, 1994</a> (Article 4, case 2) "governmental symbols, documents, materials and signs are not subject to copyrights". However, in some instances the use of this image in Poland might be regulated by other laws. It is being <a href="https://pl.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dyskusja_Wikipedii:Prawa_autorskie" class="extiw" title="pl:Dyskusja Wikipedii:Prawa autorskie">debated</a> if postage stamps fall into this category. (See: <a href="/wiki/Template:PD-Polishsymbol" class="mw-redirect" title="Template:PD-Polishsymbol">Template:PD-Polishsymbol</a>)
</p><p>According to the Art.21 of <a href="/wiki/File:Prawo_autorskie_1935.pdf" title="File:Prawo autorskie 1935.pdf">copyright law of March 29, 1926</a> (valid until 1952) photographs lose copyright protection ten years after picture was taken. Series of scientific or artistic pictures lose copyright protection after 50 years. According to Art. 27 of <a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/pl:Prawo_autorskie_(ustawa_z_10_lipca_1952_r.)" class="extiw" title="s:pl:Prawo autorskie (ustawa z 10 lipca 1952 r.)">copyright law of July 10, 1952</a> (valid until May 23, 1994) photographs and  series of photographs lose copyright protection ten years after publication date.
</p><p><a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/pl:Prawo_autorskie_(ustawa)" class="extiw" title="s:pl:Prawo autorskie (ustawa)">The copyright act from February 4, 1994</a> in article 33 point 1 allows the propagation of works that are permanently exhibited on the publicly accessible roads, streets, squares or gardens provided that the propagation is not for the same use. The name of the creator and source should be provided if it is possible by article 34. This use is royalty free, provided that it does not harm the legitimate interests of the creator by article 34.
</p><p>Polish copyright law prohibits copyright for:
</p>
<ol><li>legislative acts and their official drafts,</li>
<li>official documents, materials, logos and symbols,</li>
<li>published patent specifications and industrial design specifications,</li>
<li>simple press information.</li></ol>
