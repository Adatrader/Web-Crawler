<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Welcome">Welcome</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=English_in_Use&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Welcome">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Welcome to English in Use, a book about the actual use of the English language.  It features sections about types of grammar, punctuation and formality.  This wikibook is intended for use by native speakers of English or advanced learners of English as a second/foreign language.
</p><p>If you wish to learn English, then you should use one of the English books for students learning English as a second/foreign language.  For new learners of English, try <a href="/wiki/English_as_an_Additional_Language" title="English as an Additional Language">English for beginners</a> book.  If you have some experience of English but need to refresh your knowledge, try the <a href="/wiki/English/English_for_B2_Students" class="mw-redirect" title="English/English for B2 Students">English for B2 Students</a> Wikibook.  If you're preparing for the Cambridge FCE exam (Cambridge English: First), then head to the <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_English:_First" title="Cambridge English: First">Cambridge English: First</a> module.  Those interested in English for business contexts should try the <a href="/wiki/English/Business_English" class="mw-redirect" title="English/Business English">Business English</a> Wikibook.
</p>
