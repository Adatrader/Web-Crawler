<p><b><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_content" class="extiw" title="w:Free content">Free content</a></b> (Wikipedia) licenses give others the right to re-use content. 
</p><p>This is intended to be a learning project about <b>free content</b>, what "free" and "unfree" content is, and what kinds of free content there is (i.e. what licences are used). This could a central node for all Wikimedia contributors who want to 1) find out more about free content, and 2) help others with licencing issues, and 3) discuss the various benefits and limitations of free content licences. However, as this page is only just beginning, please add <i>any</i> information, comments and questions, which we could eventually refactor into some useful learning material.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_issues">Major issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Free_content&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Major issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="What_is_.22free.22_content.3F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="What_is_&quot;free&quot;_content?">What is "free" content?</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Free_content&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: What is &quot;free&quot; content?">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Free content is content (ie books, music, images, etc.) which can be used freely, under one or a combination of free licences. Examples of this are any of Wikimedia projects' content - amongst which is this project, Wikiversity. For a breakdown of what licences are available, and what they mean, see below. Also, see <a href="/wiki/Wikiversity:Copyrights" title="Wikiversity:Copyrights">Wikiversity:Copyrights</a> and <a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Commons:Licensing" class="extiw" title="commons:Commons:Licensing">Commons:Licensing</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="What_is_.22unfree.22_content.3F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="What_is_&quot;unfree&quot;_content?">What is "unfree" content?</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Free_content&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: What is &quot;unfree&quot; content?">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In contrast to free content, "unfree" content (or content under <b>copyright</b>), is released under heavy restriction of use. This means (amongst other things) that the content cannot be reproduced in other formats, publications etc., except by permission of the author and/or publisher, or except under certain specific legal clauses, such as that of "fair" or "educational" use (though this varies between countries and contexts).
</p><p>More things than you might imagine are <a href="/wiki/Copyright" title="Copyright">copyright</a>, for example:
</p>
<ul><li>A photograph you have made of a person who has not given their consent to be photographed.</li>
<li>A photograph you have made of another creative work - a public sculpture, a detail of an architectural interior, even an action figure! (though this depends on the country, author rights etc.)</li>
<li>A screen shot you have made of proprietary (i.e. "non-free") software.</li>
<li>A scan you have made of a book/album cover.</li></ul>
