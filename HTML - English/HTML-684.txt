<p><b>Tides</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Charitable_organization" title="Charitable organization">public charity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fiscal_sponsorship" title="Fiscal sponsorship">fiscal sponsor</a> working to advance progressive policy in areas such as the environment, health care, labor issues, immigrant rights, gay rights, women's rights and human rights. It was founded in San Francisco in 1976. Through <a href="/wiki/Donor_advised_fund" class="mw-redirect" title="Donor advised fund">donor advised funds</a>, Tides distributes money from anonymous donors to other organizations, which are often politically <a href="/wiki/Liberalism" title="Liberalism">liberal</a>. It manages two centers in San Francisco and <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a> that offer collaborative spaces for social ventures and other nonprofits.
</p><p>Since 1996, Tides has overseen the Tides Center, which is an <a href="/wiki/Business_incubator" title="Business incubator">incubator</a> for smaller progressive organizations.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2004, Tides formed the Tides Shared Spaces offshoot which specializes in renting office space to nonprofit tenants.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 2008, <a href="/wiki/Stephanie_Strom" title="Stephanie Strom">Stephanie Strom</a> referred to Tides Network as the umbrella organization for these entities.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tides_(organization)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tides was founded in 1976 by <a href="/wiki/Drummond_Pike" title="Drummond Pike">Drummond Pike</a>, who worked with Jane Bagley Lehman, heir to the <a href="/wiki/R._J._Reynolds_Tobacco_Company" title="R. J. Reynolds Tobacco Company">R. J. Reynolds Tobacco Company</a> fortune.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECallahan2010265_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECallahan2010265-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Lehman served as the chair of the organization from its founding to her death in 1988.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Tides was conceived as a nationally oriented community foundation, and founded out of Pike's frustration with established philanthropy's perceived neglect of progressive issues.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMcCarthyFaber2005133_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMcCarthyFaber2005133-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He envisioned using <a href="/wiki/Fiscal_sponsorship" title="Fiscal sponsorship">fiscal sponsorship</a> for progressive political activism.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEManheim200469_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEManheim200469-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Fiscal sponsorship uses a <a href="/wiki/Tax_exemption" title="Tax exemption">tax-exempt</a> charity to provide financial support to a non-exempt project or organization, therefore lending it tax exemption as long as the charity retains control of the way its funds are spent.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEManheim200467_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEManheim200467-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He served as its CEO until he was replaced by Melissa L. Bradley in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Pike founded a Canadian version of the organization, Tides Canada, in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Tides Canada, which is based in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, makes grants through its foundation and has a charity that supports environmental and social justice projects.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> It consists of the Tides Canada Foundation and the Tides Canada Initiatives Society.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By 2009, Tides allocated $75 million per year in donor money, most of which went to fund progressive political causes.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECallahan2010265_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECallahan2010265-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, Tides received about $90 million in funding, and awarded about $96 million to various individuals and organizations.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tides_(organization)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tides makes charitable contributions through donor-advised and collective action funds and provides consulting, investment, fiscal management services, and a collaborative space for their partners in their New York City and San Francisco offices.<sup id="cite_ref-AnnualReport15_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AnnualReport15-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Donations">Donations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tides_(organization)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Donations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Organizations that began as projects of Tides include <a href="/wiki/Campaign_to_Defend_the_Constitution" title="Campaign to Defend the Constitution">Campaign to Defend the Constitution</a>, <a href="/wiki/Higher_Education_Recruitment_Consortium" title="Higher Education Recruitment Consortium">Higher Education Recruitment Consortium</a>, <a href="/wiki/People_for_the_American_Way" title="People for the American Way">People for the American Way</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pew_Research_Center" title="Pew Research Center">Pew Internet and American Life Project</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rockridge_Institute" title="Rockridge Institute">Rockridge Institute</a>, <a href="/wiki/Social_Venture_Network" title="Social Venture Network">Social Venture Network</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Urgent_Action_Fund&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Urgent Action Fund (page does not exist)">Urgent Action Fund</a>, and <a href="/wiki/V-Day_(movement)" title="V-Day (movement)">V-Day</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The Tides website lists 130 current grantees.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> As Tides is a public charity, it allows sponsors to donate money to different organizations—including for-profit as well as nonprofit entities—through donor-advised funds.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKellow2007144_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKellow2007144-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Donor-advised funds are funds held in accounts by nonprofit organizations, like Tides, that then make grants to third-party entities on the donor's behalf.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Organizations that have partnered with Tides to setup these funds include <a href="/wiki/Girl_Rising" title="Girl Rising">Girl Rising</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Humble_Bundle" title="Humble Bundle">Humble Bundle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2000, Tides launched a program called "Bridging the Economic Divide." It focused on funding living wage campaigns and economic justice coalitions. Tides also launched the Tides Death Penalty Mobilization Fund, which supports the anti-death penalty movement. The Michigan Partnership to Prevent Gun Violence was founded with support from Tides.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEAnheierLeat200657_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAnheierLeat200657-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tides has received at least $3.5 million from financier and political activist <a href="/wiki/George_Soros" title="George Soros">George Soros</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 2003 to 2012, Tides gave around $4.4 million to media advocacy organization <a href="/wiki/Media_Matters_for_America" title="Media Matters for America">Media Matters for America</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> It has stated that it supports the Occupy Wall Street movement. The CEO of Tides, Melissa L. Bradley, stated in a blog post on October 2011 that the movement "represents the best of American ideals and ingenuity."<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Advocacy_Fund">The Advocacy Fund</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tides_(organization)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Advocacy Fund">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tides is affiliated with the Advocacy Fund, a liberal lobbying group.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In the 2012 election cycle, the Advocacy Fund gave $11.5 million to <a href="/wiki/501(c)_organization#501.28c.29.284.29" title="501(c) organization">501(c)(4) organizations</a>, including $2 million to the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Conservation_Voters" title="League of Conservation Voters">League of Conservation Voters</a>, $1.8 million to <a href="/wiki/America_Votes" title="America Votes">America Votes</a> and $1.3 million to the <a href="/wiki/Center_for_Community_Change" title="Center for Community Change">Center for Community Change</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-blumenthal_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blumenthal-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The Advocacy Fund has also supported the environmentally-focused groups <a href="/wiki/Bold_Nebraska" title="Bold Nebraska">Bold Nebraska</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Wildlife_Federation" title="National Wildlife Federation">National Wildlife Federation Action Fund</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natural_Resources_Defense_Council" title="Natural Resources Defense Council">NRDC Action Fund</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Club" title="Sierra Club">Sierra Club</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2008, the Advocacy Fund contributed to campaigns opposing <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Amendment_46" title="Colorado Amendment 46">Colorado Amendment 46</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Amendment_47" title="Colorado Amendment 47">Colorado Amendment 47</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Amendment_49" title="Colorado Amendment 49">Colorado Amendment 49</a> and <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Amendment_54" title="Colorado Amendment 54">Colorado Amendment 54</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The Advocacy Fund distributed $11.8 million in grants in 2013 to groups promoting immigration reform, increased worker protections, chemical safety legal reform, and increased investment in the solar energy industry.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
