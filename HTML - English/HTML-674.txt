<h2 style="margin-right:60px;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Wikimedia_avoids_liability_in_French_lawsuit">Wikimedia avoids liability in French lawsuit</span></h2>
<dl><dd><small>By <a href="/wiki/User:Michael_Snow" title="User:Michael Snow">Michael Snow</a>, <a href="/wiki/5_November" class="mw-redirect" title="5 November">5 November</a>, <a href="/wiki/2007" title="2007">2007</a></small></dd></dl>
<p>In a decision announced last week and proclaimed a "landmark", the <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Foundation" title="Wikimedia Foundation">Wikimedia Foundation</a> prevailed in a lawsuit brought by three people over alleged defamation on the <a href="/wiki/French_Wikipedia" title="French Wikipedia">French Wikipedia</a>.
</p><p>The plaintiffs were attempting to have the Wikimedia Foundation declared responsible for an article in the French Wikipedia that identified them as gay activists. The statements, which also referred to one of them having difficulty adopting children because of his activism, were introduced by an unregistered editor and subsequently removed. The plaintiffs sought to hold Wikimedia liable for € 69,000 in damages based on a claim of <a href="/wiki/Defamation" title="Defamation">defamation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_privacy" class="mw-redirect" title="Invasion of privacy">invasion of privacy</a>.
</p><p>Taking the position that the Wikimedia Foundation had been notified of the problem, one of the plaintiffs claimed to have contacted the foundation to complain. Efforts to locate a message from the plaintiffs failed to yield fruit, and Wikimedia's position was that the disputed content was removed promptly once it was notified. While not reaching the issue of whether the statements were legally defamatory, the judge noted that any damage was mitigated by the removal of the material from Wikipedia. He concluded that Wikimedia "acted promptly to cease giving access to the content once it became aware of its character."
</p><p>The decision came Monday, <a href="/wiki/October_29" title="October 29">October 29</a>, from Judge Emmanuel Binoche of the <a href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tribunal_de_grande_instance_(France)" class="extiw" title="fr:Tribunal de grande instance (France)"><i>tribunal de grande instance</i></a> (the court of initial jurisdiction) in Paris. The ruling was based on a French law known as the <a href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loi_pour_la_confiance_dans_l%27%C3%A9conomie_num%C3%A9rique" class="extiw" title="fr:Loi pour la confiance dans l&#39;économie numérique">LCEN</a> (Law for the Security of the Digital Economy). Somewhat like <a href="/wiki/Section_230_of_the_Communications_Decency_Act" title="Section 230 of the Communications Decency Act">Section 230 of the Communications Decency Act</a> in the U.S., the LCEN distinguishes Internet hosts from content publishers, and provides some protection from liability for hosts who are unaware of illegal content. By classifying Wikimedia as a host, it would relieve the foundation from the burden of actively screening for defamatory material.
</p><p>Some news reports pointed to the case as the first European litigation involving the Wikimedia Foundation. However, it should be mentioned that the local chapter for Germany, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.wikimedia.de/">Wikimedia Deutschland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikipedia_Signpost/2006-05-15/Tron_appeal" title="Wikipedia:Wikipedia Signpost/2006-05-15/Tron appeal">prevailed in a lawsuit last year</a> over whether the name of a deceased individual could be published in Wikipedia. In the United States, Wikimedia seems to have avoided direct involvement in litigation so far, although Wikipedia edits have been the subject of lawsuits involving other parties, such as one involving a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikipedia_Signpost/2006-07-24/Skutt_suit" title="Wikipedia:Wikipedia Signpost/2006-07-24/Skutt suit">Nebraska school</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikipedia_Signpost/2007-02-26/Fuzzy_suit" title="Wikipedia:Wikipedia Signpost/2007-02-26/Fuzzy suit">professional golfer complaining about defamation</a>. The Wikimedia Foundation was included as a party in a wide-ranging lawsuit filed in Canada by a Green Party activist earlier this year, with such companies as <a href="/wiki/Yahoo" class="mw-redirect" title="Yahoo">Yahoo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> as co-defendants, over postings about the plaintiff on Wikipedia and elsewhere; Wikimedia is seeking to have the matter dismissed.
</p><p><br />
