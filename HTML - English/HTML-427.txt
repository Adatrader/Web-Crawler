<p>This is the category for  <b><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copyright" class="extiw" title="w:Copyright">copyright law</a></b>.  <a href="https://en.wikinews.org/w/index.php?title=Special:NewsFeed&amp;feed=atom&amp;categories=Published%7CCopyright&amp;notcategories=No%20publish%7Cdisputed&amp;namespace=0&amp;count=15&amp;ordermethod=categoryadd&amp;stablepages=only"><img alt="Feed-icon.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Feed-icon.svg/18px-Feed-icon.svg.png" decoding="async" width="18" height="18" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Feed-icon.svg/27px-Feed-icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Feed-icon.svg/36px-Feed-icon.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="128" data-file-height="128" /></a>
</p><p>Although somewhat different from copyright violation, as of this writing <b>plagiarism</b> and <b>plagiary</b> redirect here.  Plagiarism/plagiary is presenting someone else's work as one's own, considered unethical in journalism and academia.
