<p><i><b>C.H.U.D.</b></i> is a 1984 American <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a> <a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror film</a> directed by Douglas Cheek, produced by Andrew Bonime, and starring <a href="/wiki/John_Heard_(actor)" title="John Heard (actor)">John Heard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Stern_(actor)" title="Daniel Stern (actor)">Daniel Stern</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Curry_(actor)" title="Christopher Curry (actor)">Christopher Curry</a> in his film debut. The plot concerns a New York City police officer and a homeless shelter manager who join forces to investigate a series of disappearances, and discover the missing are taken by humanoid monsters that live below the city. 
</p><p>The title of the movie stands for <b>C</b>annibalistic <b>H</b>umanoid <b>U</b>nderground <b>D</b>wellers.
</p><p>The film was released in North America on August 31, 1984 and grossed $4.7 million. It was followed in 1989 by <i><a href="/wiki/C.H.U.D._II:_Bud_the_C.H.U.D." title="C.H.U.D. II: Bud the C.H.U.D.">C.H.U.D. II: Bud the C.H.U.D.</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C.H.U.D.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film opens with a woman, Flora Bosch, walking her dog down an empty, darkened city street. As she passes by a <a href="/wiki/Manhole" title="Manhole">manhole</a>, a creature attacks Mrs. Bosch and drags her and the dog into the sewers.
</p><p>George Cooper lives with his girlfriend Lauren. George, a once-prominent fashion photographer, has since forgone fame and fortune. His current project is photographing <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City's</a> <a href="/wiki/Homeless" class="mw-redirect" title="Homeless">homeless</a> population, specifically those known as "undergrounders", or people who reside within the bowels of the city.
</p><p>Captain Bosch has a personal interest in the recent flood of missing persons being reported to his precinct, as Flora, his wife, remains missing. Bosch interviews A.J. "The Reverend" Shepherd, who runs the local homeless shelter. Shepherd believes recent events to be a part of a massive government cover-up and has the evidence to prove it. Bosch's superiors know more than they are letting on and seem to be taking their cues from Wilson, who works for the <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_Regulatory_Commission" title="Nuclear Regulatory Commission">Nuclear Regulatory Commission</a> (NRC).
</p><p>It turns out that monsters are lurking beneath the streets; beings that were once human, but have been mutated by radioactive, chemical <a href="/wiki/Toxic_waste" title="Toxic waste">toxic waste</a> into hideous, flesh-eating creatures that prey on the homeless who live in the underground. Given the recent drop in the underground transient population, the creatures have resorted to coming to the surface through sewer manholes to feed. Through a series of events, both George and A.J. find themselves trapped in the sewers, a reporter gets involved and eaten, and Lauren has a problem with both a clogged shower drain and a mutant that comes up through the sewer access point that she opened in the basement of her apartment building. A.J. and George, both discover that The <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_Regulatory_Commission" title="Nuclear Regulatory Commission">Nuclear Regulatory Commission</a> is directly involved in the slaughter that has been going on.
</p><p>Although the political bureaucracy has forbidden the NRC to transport the toxic wastes through New York because of the large-scale danger to the public, it has secretly been hiding the waste by-products, marked as "Contamination Hazard Urban Disposal", beneath Manhattan in the abandoned subway tunnels. The underground homeless population has been coming into contact with these by-products, turning them into the mutated creatures. To protect this secret Wilson sends a man to disrupt A.J. from making phone calls to the press.  This thug then locks A.J. in an underground access tunnel either to suffocate from the gas to be used to <a href="/wiki/Asphyxiate" class="mw-redirect" title="Asphyxiate">asphyxiate</a> the C.H.U.D.s, or to leave him to become their prey. Later that evening at a diner, two police officers enter and while the waitress and the two are discussing, the monsters return and attack the diner inhabitants.
</p><p>Captain Bosch argues with Wilson over how to deal with the threat: Wilson wants to seal the sewers, open up some gas lines, and asphyxiate the C.H.U.D.s despite the inherent danger to the city.
</p><p>Wilson, after being overwhelmed by Bosch, shoots him and drives the truck in reverse aiming for George and AJ, but they escape from the manhole just in time as Wilson passes them over. AJ finds Bosch's gun and shoots and kills Wilson before he hits them. The truck explodes as it falls on the manhole. Bosch is found to be alive and George, Lauren, and AJ are safe.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C.H.U.D.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Theatrical_release">Theatrical release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C.H.U.D.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Theatrical release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>C.H.U.D.</i> was given a <a href="/wiki/Limited_release" class="mw-redirect" title="Limited release">limited release</a> theatrically by <a href="/wiki/New_World_Pictures" title="New World Pictures">New World Pictures</a> beginning in August 1984. The film opened at #13 at the box office, grossing $1,762,922 with an average gross of $5,686 in 310 theaters.<sup id="cite_ref-weekendboxoffice_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weekendboxoffice-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Its second week saw it dropping to #14 at the box office, with a gross of only $834,465 and a worldwide gross of $2,846,756.<sup id="cite_ref-weekendboxoffice2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weekendboxoffice2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C.H.U.D.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>C.H.U.D.</i> was released for home video on <a href="/wiki/Betamax" title="Betamax">Betamax</a> and <a href="/wiki/VHS" title="VHS">VHS</a> by <a href="/wiki/Media_Home_Entertainment" title="Media Home Entertainment">Media Home Entertainment</a> in 1984.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The film was released on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> by <a href="/wiki/Anchor_Bay_Entertainment" title="Anchor Bay Entertainment">Anchor Bay Entertainment</a> on January 30, 2001. The company released the film again in 2008 as a part of its <i>Cult Fiction</i> series.<sup id="cite_ref-allmoviedvd_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmoviedvd-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Image_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="Image Entertainment">Image Entertainment</a> would release the film in 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and in 2012, with the later release being a part of a two-disc multi-feature alongside <i><a href="/wiki/Children_of_the_Corn_(1984_film)" title="Children of the Corn (1984 film)">Children of the Corn</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Creepshow_2" title="Creepshow 2">Creepshow 2</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/House_(1986_film)" title="House (1986 film)">House</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmoviedvd_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmoviedvd-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> A two-disc <a href="/wiki/Special_Edition" class="mw-redirect" title="Special Edition">Special Edition</a> was released on DVD and <a href="/wiki/Blu-ray" title="Blu-ray">Blu-ray</a> by <a href="/wiki/Arrow_Video" class="mw-redirect" title="Arrow Video">Arrow Video</a> on November 22, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-allmoviedvd_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmoviedvd-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-specialedition_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-specialedition-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=C.H.U.D.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p><a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">review aggregator</a>, reports a 29% approval rating based on 14 reviews; the average rating is 3.89/10.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Van_Gelder" title="Lawrence Van Gelder">Lawrence Van Gelder</a> from <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> stated in his review for the film, "<i>C.H.U.D.</i> makes no pretension toward serious thesis about government or the environment. It is meant to be light commercial entertainment, and in the category of horror films it stands as a praiseworthy effort".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  Keith Phipps of <i><a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">The A.V. Club</a></i> wrote, "Perfect for bleary-eyed late-night viewing and pretty much unwatchable at any other hour."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  Patrick Naugle of <a href="/wiki/DVD_Verdict" title="DVD Verdict">DVD Verdict</a> called it a fun film that focuses more on entertainment than deeper issues.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  Joshua Rothkopf of <i><a href="/wiki/Time_Out_(magazine)" title="Time Out (magazine)">Time Out New York</a></i> included it <i>Time Out</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s list of best New York-set films, calling it "more funny than scary".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  <a href="/wiki/Bloody_Disgusting" title="Bloody Disgusting">Bloody Disgusting</a> rated it 4.5/5 stars and called it "definitely one of b-movies best kept secrets".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It won Best Fantasy Film at <a href="/wiki/Brussels_International_Festival_of_Fantasy_Film" class="mw-redirect" title="Brussels International Festival of Fantasy Film">Brussels International Festival of Fantasy Film</a> in 1985.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
