<p><b>Michael Davies</b> (born 3 March 1966) is a United States-based British television game shows producer.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As president and CEO of <a href="/wiki/Embassy_Row_(production_company)" title="Embassy Row (production company)">Embassy Row</a>, a New York City-based television production company that is a unit of <a href="/wiki/Sony_Pictures_Television" title="Sony Pictures Television">Sony Pictures Television</a>, he was the executive producer of <i>Wife Swap</i>. He produced <i><a href="/wiki/2_Minute_Drill_(game_show)" title="2 Minute Drill (game show)">ESPN's 2 Minute Drill</a></i>, VH1's <i><a href="/wiki/World_Series_of_Pop_Culture" class="mw-redirect" title="World Series of Pop Culture">World Series of Pop Culture</a></i>, CBS's <i><a href="/wiki/Power_of_10_(U.S._game_show)" class="mw-redirect" title="Power of 10 (U.S. game show)">Power of 10</a></i>, and the GSN originals: <i><a href="/wiki/Chain_Reaction_(game_show)" title="Chain Reaction (game show)">Chain Reaction</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Slam_(U.S._game_show)" class="mw-redirect" title="Grand Slam (U.S. game show)">Grand Slam</a></i>, and GSN's revival of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Newlywed_Game" title="The Newlywed Game">The Newlywed Game</a></i>, as well as the US version of <i><a href="/wiki/Who_Wants_to_Be_a_Millionaire_(U.S._game_show)" class="mw-redirect" title="Who Wants to Be a Millionaire (U.S. game show)">Who Wants to Be a Millionaire</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He wrote a blog about the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 FIFA World Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a> for <a href="/wiki/ESPN.com" title="ESPN.com">ESPN.com</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_and_family">Early years and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Davies_(television_producer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Davies grew up in <a href="/wiki/Blackheath,_London" title="Blackheath, London">Blackheath</a>, London<sup id="cite_ref-MIB6172015_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MIB6172015-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and attended <a href="/wiki/Mercersburg_Academy" title="Mercersburg Academy">Mercersburg Academy</a> in Pennsylvania, graduating from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Edinburgh" title="University of Edinburgh">University of Edinburgh</a>. He is the younger brother of opera singer <a href="/wiki/Rebecca_de_Pont_Davies" title="Rebecca de Pont Davies">Rebecca de Pont Davies</a> and screenwriter <a href="/wiki/William_Davies_(screenwriter)" title="William Davies (screenwriter)">William Davies</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <p>Beginning in 2011, Davies and co-host and ESPNFC.com writer <a href="/wiki/Roger_Bennett_(journalist)" title="Roger Bennett (journalist)">Roger Bennett</a>, brought the <i><a href="/wiki/Men_in_Blazers" title="Men in Blazers">Men in Blazers</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Podcast" title="Podcast">podcast</a> to <a href="/wiki/Grantland.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Grantland.com">Grantland.com</a>. The weekly podcast includes analysis of recent matches in the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> as well as coverage of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> and various international fixtures, specifically the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States men&#39;s national soccer team">United States men's national soccer team</a> games.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The duo were invited to provide analysis and humorous segments for ESPN live from Rio de Janeiro during the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>, especially from <a href="/wiki/Bob_Ley" title="Bob Ley">Bob Ley</a>'s "Panic Room".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The duo did a live show from <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland, Oregon</a> for the 2014 MLS All-Star Game, interviewing the likes of <a href="/wiki/Clint_Dempsey" title="Clint Dempsey">Clint Dempsey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alex_Morgan" title="Alex Morgan">Alex Morgan</a> while raising money for MLS Works and the Wounded Warrior Project.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2014, they conducted a <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_FC" title="New York City FC">New York City FC</a> exclusive interview with <a href="/wiki/Frank_Lampard" title="Frank Lampard">Frank Lampard</a> after Lampard joined NYCFC from Chelsea.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fox_Sports" title="Fox Sports">Fox Sports</a> announced in April 2014 they had cancelled the Davies-produced program <i><a href="/wiki/Crowd_Goes_Wild" class="mw-redirect" title="Crowd Goes Wild">Crowd Goes Wild</a></i>, a daily sports talk show from the national sports network <a href="/wiki/Fox_Sports_1" title="Fox Sports 1">Fox Sports 1</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ahead of the 2014–15 Premier League season, <a href="/wiki/NBC_Sports" title="NBC Sports">NBC Sports</a> signed Davies and Bennett from <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a> for its <a href="/wiki/English_Premier_League" class="mw-redirect" title="English Premier League">English Premier League</a> coverage and confirmed that Men in Blazers would have its own half-hour television show on Monday nights at 10&#160;pm EST, starting 22 September 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Davies and Bennett host the <i><a href="/wiki/Men_in_Blazers" title="Men in Blazers">Men in Blazers</a></i> channel on EA Sports Talk Radio on the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_Brazil_(video_game)" title="2014 FIFA World Cup Brazil (video game)">official 2014 FIFA World Cup video game</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Andy_Goldstein" title="Andy Goldstein">Andy Goldstein</a> of <a href="/wiki/TalkSPORT" class="mw-redirect" title="TalkSPORT">TalkSPORT</a> and Darke hosting the only other channel.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
