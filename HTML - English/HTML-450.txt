<p><b>Rick Derringer</b> (born <b>Ricky Dean Zehringer</b>; August 5, 1947) is an American guitarist, vocalist, <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a>-winning producer and writer of multiple hit songs. He came to prominence in the 1960s as founding member of his band, <a href="/wiki/The_McCoys" title="The McCoys">The McCoys</a>.  At that time, they were brought to New York City to record what became the number-one hit song, "<a href="/wiki/Hang_On_Sloopy" title="Hang On Sloopy">Hang On Sloopy</a>". The McCoys then had seven songs that charted in the top 100, including their versions of “<a href="/wiki/Fever_(Little_Willie_John_song)" title="Fever (Little Willie John song)">Fever</a>” and “Come On Let’s Go”. 
</p><p>Later, in the 1970s, Derringer had another major hit with his self-written song, "<a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll,_Hoochie_Koo" title="Rock and Roll, Hoochie Koo">Rock and Roll, Hoochie Koo</a>". He has worked extensively with brothers <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Winter" title="Edgar Winter">Edgar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Winter" title="Johnny Winter">Johnny Winter</a>, playing lead guitar in their bands, and also producing all of their gold and platinum disc earning recordings. He has worked with <a href="/wiki/Steely_Dan" title="Steely Dan">Steely Dan</a>, then discovered <a href="/wiki/Cyndi_Lauper" title="Cyndi Lauper">Cyndi Lauper</a> and <a href="/wiki/%22Weird_Al%22_Yankovic" title="&quot;Weird Al&quot; Yankovic">"Weird Al" Yankovic</a>, producing Yankovic's Grammy Award-winning songs "<a href="/wiki/Eat_It" title="Eat It">Eat It</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Fat_(song)" title="Fat (song)">Fat</a>". "Eat It" included Derringer's guitar solo, which emulated <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Van_Halen" title="Eddie Van Halen">Eddie Van Halen</a>'s solo on <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Beat_It" title="Beat It">Beat It</a>". The work he did with Weird Al convinced <a href="/wiki/Vince_McMahon" title="Vince McMahon">Vince McMahon</a>, president of the <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">World Wrestling Federation</a> that Derringer should be the producer of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wrestling_Album" title="The Wrestling Album">The Wrestling Album</a></i> (1985) and then the follow up <i><a href="/wiki/Piledriver:_The_Wrestling_Album_II" title="Piledriver: The Wrestling Album II">Piledriver: The Wrestling Album II</a></i> (1987). The albums included the entrance song for <a href="/wiki/Hulk_Hogan" title="Hulk Hogan">Hulk Hogan</a>, "Real American" and the tag team's eponymously's titled <a href="/wiki/Demolition_(professional_wrestling)" title="Demolition (professional wrestling)">Demolition</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rick_Derringer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_1960s">Early life and 1960s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rick_Derringer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life and 1960s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Derringer was born in <a href="/wiki/Celina,_Ohio" title="Celina, Ohio">Celina, Ohio</a>, United States, and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Recovery,_Ohio" title="Fort Recovery, Ohio">Fort Recovery</a>, the son of Janice Lavine (Thornburg) and John Otto Zehringer, a Section Foreman on the Nickel Plate Railroad.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> According to Derringer, other than his parents' extensive record collection, his first major influence was an uncle, Jim Thornburg, a popular guitarist and singer in Ohio.  Derringer has related first hearing him play guitar in the kitchen of his parents' home, and knowing immediately that he wanted to play guitar.  He was then eight years old, and his parents gave him his first electric guitar for his ninth birthday. <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was not long before he and his brother, Randy were playing music together.
</p><p>After the eighth grade, the family moved to Union City, Indiana, and it was there that he formed his band that he called <a href="/wiki/The_McCoys" title="The McCoys">The McCoys</a>. He later changed the name to the Rick Z Combo and then Rick and the Raiders before going back to the band's original name.
</p><p>In the summer of 1965, when Derringer was 17, the band was hired to back up a band called <a href="/wiki/The_Strangeloves" title="The Strangeloves">the Strangeloves</a> in concert. When not in Strangelove garb, they were three record producers from New York City, looking for a band (in the mold of The Beatles) to record "My Girl Sloopy", which Derringer later convinced them to call the more appropriate title "<a href="/wiki/Hang_On_Sloopy" title="Hang On Sloopy">Hang On Sloopy</a>". After playing all the guitar parts, he and The McCoys sang the recording that we've all heard since. The song was an instant success, reaching number one in every country that sold records.  It was the first record played in Moscow's Red Square when the government decided to play Rock and Roll for the proletariat.  It stayed in the number one slot while "<a href="/wiki/Yesterday_(Beatles_song)" title="Yesterday (Beatles song)">Yesterday</a>" (The Beatles song) was number two. <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Derringer has admitted of the time that “My parents never thought that I’d be able to make a living playing music; but instead of going to school, I went to New York City to do what my parents dreamed was impossible.”<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1970s">1970s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rick_Derringer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1970s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rick_Derringer.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Rick_Derringer.jpg/150px-Rick_Derringer.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="225" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Rick_Derringer.jpg/225px-Rick_Derringer.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Rick_Derringer.jpg/300px-Rick_Derringer.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1947" data-file-height="2922" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rick_Derringer.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Derringer, 1978</div></div></div>
<p>Derringer, with his band, The McCoys, joined <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Winter" title="Johnny Winter">Johnny Winter</a> in a band that they called "Johnny Winter And"; the "And" referring to The McCoys. Derringer joined <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Winter%27s_White_Trash" class="mw-redirect" title="Edgar Winter&#39;s White Trash">Edgar Winter's White Trash</a> and then, the <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Winter_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Edgar Winter Group">Edgar Winter Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-And_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-And-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1973, Derringer released his first solo album <i><a href="/wiki/All_American_Boy_(Rick_Derringer_album)" title="All American Boy (Rick Derringer album)">All-American Boy</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> which included his song "<a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll,_Hoochie_Koo" title="Rock and Roll, Hoochie Koo">Rock and Roll, Hoochie Koo</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> By that time the song had appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Johnny_Winter_And" title="Johnny Winter And">Johnny Winter And</a></i> (1970),<sup id="cite_ref-And_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-And-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Live_Johnny_Winter_And" title="Live Johnny Winter And">Live Johnny Winter And</a></i> (1971), and also the White Trash <i><a href="/wiki/Roadwork_(album)" title="Roadwork (album)">Roadwork</a></i> (1972) albums.  Derringer's version rose to the Top 20 on the Billboard Hot 100 charts, becoming his highest-charting single.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  Critics have described the album as a "sadly neglected album of great merit." <sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Derringer's later albums, both solo and with his eponymous band Derringer included 1977's <i>Sweet Evil</i> included co-writes with legendary songwriter <a href="/wiki/Cynthia_Weil" title="Cynthia Weil">Cynthia Weil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rolling_Thunder_Revue" title="Rolling Thunder Revue">Rolling Thunder Revue</a> author <a href="/wiki/Larry_Sloman" title="Larry Sloman">Larry Sloman</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>, and the critically acclaimed <i>Guitars and Women</i> (1979), which was re-released with liner notes by <a href="/wiki/Razor_%26_Tie" title="Razor &amp; Tie">Razor &amp; Tie</a> in 1998.
</p><p>Around this time he contributed guitar to two <a href="/wiki/Steely_Dan" title="Steely Dan">Steely Dan</a> tracks, "Show Biz Kids" on <i><a href="/wiki/Countdown_to_Ecstasy" title="Countdown to Ecstasy">Countdown to Ecstasy</a></i> (1973) and "Chain Lightning" on <i><a href="/wiki/Katy_Lied" title="Katy Lied">Katy Lied</a></i> (1975). In 1977, Derringer worked hours on the recording of the guitar solo for "<a href="/wiki/Peg_(song)" title="Peg (song)">Peg</a>" on <i><a href="/wiki/Aja_(album)" title="Aja (album)">Aja</a></i>, with <a href="/wiki/Donald_Fagen" title="Donald Fagen">Donald Fagen</a>, producer <a href="/wiki/Gary_Katz" title="Gary Katz">Gary Katz</a>, and engineer <a href="/wiki/Roger_Nichols_(recording_engineer)" title="Roger Nichols (recording engineer)">Roger Nichols</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Derringer is credited with helping Donald Fagen gain a record deal in 1972.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Derringer worked with his neighbor <a href="/wiki/Todd_Rundgren" title="Todd Rundgren">Todd Rundgren</a> during this time period, playing on four of Rundgren's solo albums. Derringer was also a regular in <a href="/wiki/Andy_Warhol" title="Andy Warhol">Andy Warhol</a>'s circle,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and he often frequented Warhol's studio <a href="/wiki/The_Factory" title="The Factory">The Factory</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Of the period, Derringer has said "Liz [his wife at the time] and I were always on the scene. We were the consummate partiers." <sup id="cite_ref-punkglobe.com_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-punkglobe.com-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1980s_and_1990s">1980s and 1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rick_Derringer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1980s and 1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Derringer contributed guitar to "My Rival" on Steely Dan's <i><a href="/wiki/Gaucho_(album)" title="Gaucho (album)">Gaucho</a></i> (1980) and also <a href="/wiki/Donald_Fagen" title="Donald Fagen">Donald Fagen</a>'s debut solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Nightfly" title="The Nightfly">The Nightfly</a></i> (1982). In 1983, he played guitar on two songs written and produced by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Steinman" title="Jim Steinman">Jim Steinman</a>: <a href="/wiki/Air_Supply" title="Air Supply">Air Supply</a>'s hit <a href="/wiki/Power_ballad" class="mw-redirect" title="Power ballad">power ballad</a> "<a href="/wiki/Making_Love_Out_of_Nothing_at_All" title="Making Love Out of Nothing at All">Making Love Out of Nothing at All</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Bonnie_Tyler" title="Bonnie Tyler">Bonnie Tyler</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Total_Eclipse_of_the_Heart" title="Total Eclipse of the Heart">Total Eclipse of the Heart</a>". He has publicly stated that his <a href="/wiki/Guitar_solo" title="Guitar solo">guitar solo</a> in "Making Love Out of Nothing at All" is his favorite guitar solo out of the many he has recorded.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> That same year, he also recorded guitar parts for <a href="/wiki/Meat_Loaf" title="Meat Loaf">Meat Loaf</a>'s poorly received album <i><a href="/wiki/Midnight_at_the_Lost_and_Found" title="Midnight at the Lost and Found">Midnight at the Lost and Found</a></i>. Both "Making Love Out of Nothing at All" and "Total Eclipse of the Heart" were originally offered to Meat Loaf by Steinman for that album, but Meat Loaf's record company refused to pay Steinman for the compositions.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In 1983, Derringer also wrote "Shake Me" from his <i>Good Dirty Fun</i> solo album. A video followed which was produced by <a href="/wiki/Jake_Hooker_(musician)" title="Jake Hooker (musician)">Jake Hooker</a> (husband of Lorna Luft), singer <a href="/wiki/Lourett_Russell_Grant" title="Lourett Russell Grant">Lourett Russell Grant</a> modeled in the video production alongside Derringer.
</p><p>1984 saw Derringer playing guitar on <a href="/wiki/Barbra_Streisand" title="Barbra Streisand">Barbra Streisand</a>'s cover of Steinman's "<a href="/wiki/Left_in_the_Dark" title="Left in the Dark">Left in the Dark</a>", which was released as the lead single of <i><a href="/wiki/Emotion_(Barbra_Streisand_album)" title="Emotion (Barbra Streisand album)">Emotion</a></i>.
</p><p>In 1985, Derringer's friendship with <a href="/wiki/Cyndi_Lauper" title="Cyndi Lauper">Cyndi Lauper</a> led him and Steinman to collaborating again, Derringer producing <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wrestling_Album" title="The Wrestling Album">The Wrestling Album</a></i> (1985) for the <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">World Wrestling Federation</a>, an album consisting mostly of wrestlers' theme songs. He wrote a couple of songs on it, including <a href="/wiki/Hulk_Hogan" title="Hulk Hogan">Hulk Hogan</a>'s theme song "Real American",<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> with Bernard Kenny. That song was also used by US President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> at the 2011 <a href="/wiki/White_House_Correspondents_Dinner" class="mw-redirect" title="White House Correspondents Dinner">White House Correspondents' Dinner</a>, where he played the song while unveiling his birth certificate.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> It was also used as a campaign song by <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton" title="Hillary Clinton">Hillary Clinton</a>, a victory song by <a href="/wiki/Newt_Gingrich" title="Newt Gingrich">Newt Gingrich</a>, and then in four videos during the campaign of <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1986, he returned to the Meat Loaf fold for <i><a href="/wiki/Blind_Before_I_Stop" title="Blind Before I Stop">Blind Before I Stop</a></i>.  Derringer co-wrote the song "Masculine".
</p><p>In 1987 <a href="/wiki/Meat_Loaf" title="Meat Loaf">Meat Loaf</a> guested on Way Off Broadway, a nationally distributed cable TV show that featured Derringer as the music director with the show's host, comedienne/interviewer <a href="/wiki/Joy_Behar" title="Joy Behar">Joy Behar</a>. Other guests on the show included <a href="/wiki/Larry_Carlton" title="Larry Carlton">Larry Carlton</a> <a href="/wiki/Robbie_Dupree" title="Robbie Dupree">Robbie Dupree</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Winter" title="Edgar Winter">Edgar Winter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Also in 1987, Derringer returned to the World Wrestling Federation and produced their second music album, <i><a href="/wiki/Piledriver:_The_Wrestling_Album_II" title="Piledriver: The Wrestling Album II">Piledriver: The Wrestling Album II</a></i>. He co-wrote the theme tune for <a href="/wiki/Demolition_(professional_wrestling)" title="Demolition (professional wrestling)">Demolition</a> and also added a fresh version of "Rock and Roll, Hoochie Koo" as a duet with <a href="/wiki/Gene_Okerlund" title="Gene Okerlund">Gene Okerlund</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He worked for several New York City-based <a href="/wiki/Jingle" title="Jingle">jingle</a> houses in the 1980s.  Unbeknownst to him at the time, his future wife Brenda Jean was also writing commercial jingles from her commercial recording studio, MusiCraft Productions. This fallow period in Derringer's career ended once he discovered <a href="/wiki/%22Weird_Al%22_Yankovic" title="&quot;Weird Al&quot; Yankovic">"Weird Al" Yankovic</a> and produced his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/%22Weird_Al%22_Yankovic_(album)" title="&quot;Weird Al&quot; Yankovic (album)">"Weird Al" Yankovic</a></i> (1983), believing that Yankovic was going to be a success and not waiting for record company involvement. Derringer ultimately produced six Yankovic albums between 1983 and 1989; for this work, he received his only <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Yankovic has stated that he is open to working with Derringer again; however, such a reunion has not occurred as of 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1997 Derringer became an <a href="/wiki/Evangelical_Christian" class="mw-redirect" title="Evangelical Christian">Evangelical Christian</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Since that point, he has consistently aligned himself with <a href="/wiki/Conservatism_in_the_United_States" title="Conservatism in the United States">conservative causes in the United States.</a> <sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>  Derringer describes himself as a "<a href="/wiki/Jesus_freak" title="Jesus freak">Jesus freak</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-punkglobe.com_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-punkglobe.com-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s">2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rick_Derringer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2001, Derringer, along with <a href="/wiki/Tim_Bogert" title="Tim Bogert">Tim Bogert</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carmine_Appice" title="Carmine Appice">Carmine Appice</a>, released the album Derringer, Bogert &amp; Appice (DBA): <i>Doin' Business as...</i> on the German label <a href="/wiki/SPV_GmbH" title="SPV GmbH">Steamhammer Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Derringer had, previously, worked with Carmine Appice on an album, <i>Party Tested</i> by DNA (Derringer'n'Appice), and it was re-released in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In 2001, the couple and their children released the first of four Christian music albums, all Panda Studio Productions:<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> <i>Aiming 4 Heaven</i> (2001),<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> <i>Derringer X 2</i> (2001), <i>Winter Wonderland</i> (2004) and <i>We Live</i> (2008).<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>  
</p><p>In 2002, Derringer was featured in a book, written by Dan Muise, called <i>Gallagher, Marriott, Derringer &amp; Trower - Their Lives and Music</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He released <i>Free Ride Smooth Jazz</i> (2002), which featured vocals by his wife Jenda (<a href="/wiki/N%C3%A9e" class="mw-redirect" title="Née">née</a> Brenda Jean), who sang the title song "Free Ride" and, with Rick, wrote the song "Hot &amp; Cool".<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2009, he self-released the album <i>Knighted by the Blues</i> and its popular song, "Sometimes", once again, co-written with Jenda. Derringer followed up with the release of <i>The Three Kings of the Blues</i> (<a href="/wiki/Freddie_King" title="Freddie King">Freddie King</a>, <a href="/wiki/B.B._King" title="B.B. King">B.B. King</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albert_King" title="Albert King">Albert King</a>) on <a href="/wiki/Mike_Varney" title="Mike Varney">Mike Varney</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Blues_Bureau_International" title="Blues Bureau International">Blues Bureau International</a> Records.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:20110626_070_All-Starr-Band-in-Paris_Rick_Derringer_WP.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/20110626_070_All-Starr-Band-in-Paris_Rick_Derringer_WP.jpg/220px-20110626_070_All-Starr-Band-in-Paris_Rick_Derringer_WP.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/20110626_070_All-Starr-Band-in-Paris_Rick_Derringer_WP.jpg/330px-20110626_070_All-Starr-Band-in-Paris_Rick_Derringer_WP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/20110626_070_All-Starr-Band-in-Paris_Rick_Derringer_WP.jpg/440px-20110626_070_All-Starr-Band-in-Paris_Rick_Derringer_WP.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4000" data-file-height="2248" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:20110626_070_All-Starr-Band-in-Paris_Rick_Derringer_WP.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Derringer playing with <a href="/wiki/Ringo_Starr%27s_All-Starr_Band" class="mw-redirect" title="Ringo Starr&#39;s All-Starr Band">Ringo Starr's All-Starr Band</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, June 26, 2011</div></div></div>
<p>Derringer and a range of hitmakers are part of Voices, a company that finds private events many times a year. Some of the artists involved with Voices are <a href="/wiki/Tone-Loc" class="mw-redirect" title="Tone-Loc">Tone-Loc</a>,
<a href="/wiki/Wally_Palmar" title="Wally Palmar">Wally Palmar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Carnes" title="Kim Carnes">Kim Carnes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belinda_Carlisle" title="Belinda Carlisle">Belinda Carlisle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Tutone" title="Tommy Tutone">Tommy Tutone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_McGrath" title="Mark McGrath">Mark McGrath</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fastball_(band)" title="Fastball (band)">Fastball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skip_Martin" title="Skip Martin">Skip Martin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jakob_Dylan" title="Jakob Dylan">Jakob Dylan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natasha_Bedingfield" title="Natasha Bedingfield">Natasha Bedingfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coolio" title="Coolio">Coolio</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Rzeznik" title="John Rzeznik">John Rzeznik</a>, <a href="/wiki/Martha_Davis_(musician)" title="Martha Davis (musician)">Martha Davis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Silverchair" title="Silverchair">Silverchair</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Augeri" title="Steve Augeri">Steve Augeri</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Elefante" title="John Elefante">John Elefante</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alex_Ligertwood" title="Alex Ligertwood">Alex Ligertwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Lyons" class="mw-redirect" title="Jeff Lyons">Jeff Lyons</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Rembrandts" title="The Rembrandts">the Rembrandts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><br />
Derringer went on three world tours with <a href="/wiki/Ringo_Starr_%26_His_All-Starr_Band" title="Ringo Starr &amp; His All-Starr Band">Ringo Starr &amp; His All-Starr Band</a>. Rehearsals started in June 2010.  He toured with Starr from 2011 to 2014. They traveled all of Europe, Russia, South America, Mexico and the USA. His tour featured <a href="/wiki/Wally_Palmar" title="Wally Palmar">Wally Palmar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Winter" title="Edgar Winter">Edgar Winter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gary_Wright" title="Gary Wright">Gary Wright</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Page" title="Richard Page">Richard Page</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gregg_Bissonette" title="Gregg Bissonette">Gregg Bissonette</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
Derringer has long been fascinated by Asian music.  In the 1980s he produced the Kodomo Band.  He has toured Asia quite a bit, including with <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Winter" title="Edgar Winter">Edgar Winter</a>, the 1990 White Lightning Tour in both Japan and Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In 2013, Rick and Jenda created the Asia Project after Jenda discovered that the two largest-selling songs in history are Chinese. As Ricky Wu and Jenda Tu, the Derringers recorded and released their versions after taking great pains to make sure the English translation was what the Chinese writers were trying to convey. The songs are Wang Qiwen and <a href="/wiki/Yang_Chengang" title="Yang Chengang">Yang Chengang</a>’s 2004 song “Mouse Loves Rice”,<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> and actress Lui Shi Shi's “Season of Waiting”.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, Derringer performed on <a href="/wiki/Peter_Frampton" title="Peter Frampton">Peter Frampton</a>'s Guitar Circus tour along with other notable guitarists, including <a href="/wiki/B.B._King" title="B.B. King">B.B. King</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roger_McGuinn" title="Roger McGuinn">Roger McGuinn</a> (ex-<a href="/wiki/The_Byrds" title="The Byrds">The Byrds</a>), <a href="/wiki/Don_Felder" title="Don Felder">Don Felder</a> (ex-<a href="/wiki/Eagles_(band)" title="Eagles (band)">Eagles</a>), <a href="/wiki/Leslie_West" title="Leslie West">Leslie West</a> (ex-<a href="/wiki/Mountain_(band)" title="Mountain (band)">Mountain</a>), <a href="/wiki/Cheap_Trick" title="Cheap Trick">Cheap Trick</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Rick_Nielsen" title="Rick Nielsen">Rick Nielsen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toto_(band)" title="Toto (band)">Toto</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Steve_Lukather" title="Steve Lukather">Steve Lukather</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Lobos" title="Los Lobos">Los Lobos</a>' <a href="/wiki/David_Hidalgo" title="David Hidalgo">David Hidalgo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pearl_Jam" title="Pearl Jam">Pearl Jam</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mike_McCready" title="Mike McCready">Mike McCready</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In 2017, Derringer was charged with carrying a loaded gun on a <a href="/wiki/Delta_Air_Lines" title="Delta Air Lines">Delta Air Lines</a> flight from <a href="/wiki/Canc%C3%BAn" title="Cancún">Cancún</a>, Mexico to <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, Georgia. According to his manager, Derringer thought he was permitted to carry the gun, based on his possession of a valid Florida <a href="/wiki/Concealed_weapon" class="mw-redirect" title="Concealed weapon">concealed weapon</a> permit. Derringer said that he flew between 30 and 50 times a year.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Derringer later pled guilty to the charge, agreeing to pay a $1000 fine, saying it will not happen again, "not even a water pistol."<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A re-recording of 1985's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wrestling_Album" title="The Wrestling Album">The Wrestling Album</a>'</i>s "Real American" with updated lyrics was released on May 28, 2017, debuting on <a href="/wiki/Alex_Jones_(radio_host)" class="mw-redirect" title="Alex Jones (radio host)">Alex Jones</a>' radio show.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> "I gotta be a man, I can't let it slide" was changed to "I gotta lend a hand, I can't let it slide" and "fight for the right of every man" became "fight for the rights of everyone". "Best not mess with my US" is interjected before the second verse, and a new line says "Ours is a cause that's right and just, we're built on truth, in God we trust."<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> The same year, Derringer appeared on Alex Jones' show where he was interviewed by political consultant <a href="/wiki/Roger_Stone" title="Roger Stone">Roger Stone</a> about Derringer's support for <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, Derringer collaborated with baseball players <a href="/wiki/Tom_Seaver" title="Tom Seaver">Tom Seaver</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gary_Redus" title="Gary Redus">Gary Redus</a> to release a version of "<a href="/wiki/Take_Me_Out_to_the_Ball_Game" title="Take Me Out to the Ball Game">Take Me Out to the Ballgame</a>", honoring his lifelong love of <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a>.
</p><p>In 2018, Derringer embarked on a tour with <a href="/wiki/Vanilla_Fudge" title="Vanilla Fudge">Vanilla Fudge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mitch_Ryder" title="Mitch Ryder">Mitch Ryder</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Badfinger" title="Badfinger">Badfinger</a> under the moniker 'HippieFest'.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early 2019, he started an “uncomplicated” crowdfunding page at <a href="/wiki/Patreon" title="Patreon">Patreon</a>. Derringer asks his fans for $10 a month in which he gives them exclusive content including new music premieres, “I’ve been able to post quite a bit of stuff there.”<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Recently, he contributed the guitar solo to an anti-bullying campaign version of "Hang on Sloopy" by The Love Love Kids.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_other_media">In other media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rick_Derringer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: In other media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll,_Hoochie_Koo" title="Rock and Roll, Hoochie Koo">Rock and Roll, Hoochie Koo</a>" is featured in the 1993 film <i><a href="/wiki/Dazed_and_Confused_(film)" title="Dazed and Confused (film)">Dazed and Confused</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-IMDB_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMDB-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> as well as in the <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a> version of <i><a href="/wiki/Guitar_Hero_II" title="Guitar Hero II">Guitar Hero II</a></i> in 2007 and <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_Band_4" title="Rock Band 4">Rock Band 4</a></i> in 2015. The song was also made available as downloadable content (DLC) for guitar learning software/game <i><a href="/wiki/Rocksmith_2014" title="Rocksmith 2014">Rocksmith 2014</a></i> in January 2015.
