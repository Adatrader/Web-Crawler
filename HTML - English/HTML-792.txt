<p>This is a guide to spotting violations of the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copyrights" title="Wikipedia:Copyrights">Wikipedia copyright policy</a> that are simple <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copy-paste" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Copy-paste">copy-and-pastes</a> from other websites. Please remember to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AGFC" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AGFC">assume good faith</a> when doing the important work of keeping Wikipedia compliant with <a href="/wiki/CC-BY-SA" class="mw-redirect" title="CC-BY-SA">CC-BY-SA</a> and, where co-licensed, <a href="/wiki/GFDL" class="mw-redirect" title="GFDL">GFDL</a>. It's important to keep in mind as well that what appears to be copied content may not be a copyright issue in some cases - for example, when Wikipedia had the content first or when the content is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PD">public domain</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:COMPLIC" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:COMPLIC">compatibly licensed</a>. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Signs_that_an_article_might_be_copy-and-pasted">Signs that an article might be copy-and-pasted</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Spotting_possible_copyright_violations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Signs that an article might be copy-and-pasted">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are a number of signs that an article might be copy-and-pasted. None of these are conclusive evidence, but more than one of these signs tends to be apparent in a copy-and-pasted article.
</p>
<h3><span id="Indicative.2C_but_by_no_means_conclusive_signs:"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Indicative,_but_by_no_means_conclusive_signs:">Indicative, but by no means conclusive signs:</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Spotting_possible_copyright_violations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Indicative, but by no means conclusive signs:">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>The text is not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Glossary" title="Wikipedia:Glossary">wikified</a> or is over-wikified, with every occurrence of a word or phrase made into a wiki link (as if search-and-replace had been used to insert the links)</li>
<li>The text was added all at once by one person in finished form with no spelling or other errors.</li>
<li>The writing style is "too good to be true"</li>
<li>The text has a strange tone of voice, such as an overly informal tone or a very slanted marketing voice with <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AWW" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AWW">weasel words</a></li>
<li>Use of first person pronouns ("we/our/us...")</li>
<li>Use of non-standard characters such as Microsoft "<a href="/wiki/Smart_quotes" class="mw-redirect" title="Smart quotes">smart quotes</a>" (Note that these may have been created in Microsoft Word or another word processing software offline)</li>
<li>In-line footnote links such as "[1]", especially when no footnotes are given.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Strong_signs_of_copy_and_pasting:">Strong signs of copy and pasting:</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Spotting_possible_copyright_violations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Strong signs of copy and pasting:">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>Out of context phrases like "this site/page/book/whitepaper"</li>
<li>Isolated or out-of-context words or phrases such as "top", "go to top", "next page", and "<a href="/wiki/Click_here" class="mw-redirect" title="Click here">click here</a>", that were originally part of the navigation structure of the original website</li>
<li>Use of trademark symbols (™,®) and similar typical signs of commercial text</li>
<li>A writing style that rarely occurs outside of a specific, invariably copyrighted, use, such as an advertisement or press release</li>
<li>A contribution from a user who has a history of violating copyright</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Irrefutable_evidence:">Irrefutable evidence:</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Spotting_possible_copyright_violations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Irrefutable evidence:">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>Pages which exhibit the above characteristics, and include the original site's copyright notice, copied intact!</li>
<li>A copy of the page source, including links to other pages on the same server which would not occur on Wikipedia or a wiki (e.g., a link to /home/news/latest.html)</li>
<li>A URL, labeled as "reference" or "source", which links to a page on a copyrighted website containing the exact (or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PARAPHRASE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PARAPHRASE">almost exact</a>) same text where the <a href="/wiki/Help:Using_the_Wayback_Machine" title="Help:Using the Wayback Machine">Wayback Machine</a> verifies they had it first.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Checking_it_out">Checking it out</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Spotting_possible_copyright_violations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Checking it out">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Once alerted by one or more of these suspicious signs, you can then check the article by highlighting a sentence or non-trivial sentence fragment that is unlikely to be found by chance in many documents, copying and pasting it into a <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engine</a>. You should then check the matching pages, if any, for further correspondence to the submitted article.  Be aware that many sites "mirror" content from Wikipedia, so a search engine may find several sites with the exact content.  Those sites should list Wikipedia as the source of the article, but do not always do so. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Mirrors_and_forks" title="Wikipedia:Mirrors and forks">Wikipedia:Mirrors and forks</a> can help you identify known mirror sites, if you suspect that's what you've encountered. The <a href="/wiki/Help:Using_the_Wayback_Machine" title="Help:Using the Wayback Machine">Wayback Machine</a> can also help <i>confirm</i> copying but has limitations in eliminating it - it does not store every site or every page within a site and may lag by six months or so even on pages that it does store. For extra thoroughness, you may also want to check out the "groups" or "books" options in Google.
</p><p>Many times an image from some other website is uploaded here under the same name. Hence if you suspect an image to be a copyright violation, you can try searching <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://images.google.com/">Google Images</a> for the filename of the image to check if there are matches from other websites for the same image. Even if the image was uploaded with a different name, a google image search for relevant search terms might help finding the original image in case of a copyright violation. <a href="/wiki/TinEye" title="TinEye">TinEye</a> and other reverse image search engines can also be useful.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="If_you_suspect_that_a_page_is_a_copyright_infringement">If you suspect that a page is a copyright infringement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Spotting_possible_copyright_violations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: If you suspect that a page is a copyright infringement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>If you suspect one, you should at the very least bring up the issue on that page's talk page. Others can then examine the situation and take action if needed. The most helpful piece of information you can provide is a <a href="/wiki/URL" title="URL">URL</a> or other reference to what you believe may be the source of the text. If the talk page is not watched, however, your note on the talk page may go unseen. If you suspect this will be the case, please consider also using &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:Copypaste" title="Template:Copypaste">Copypaste</a>&#125;&#125; or opening a section listing your concern at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:CP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia talk:CP">WT:CP</a>.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Remember: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_do_not_bite_the_newcomers" title="Wikipedia:Please do not bite the newcomers">please don't bite the newbies</a></b> -- many copy-and-paste contributors may not understand that what they are doing is wrong, and some may turn into valuable contributors if educated rather than punished. You can use the user's talk page to discuss your concerns with them. The &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:Uw-copyright-new" title="Template:Uw-copyright-new">Uw-copyright-new</a>&#125;&#125; template may be useful for this.</li></ul>
<ul><li><b>Some cases will be false alarms.</b> For example:
<ul><li>if the contributor was in fact the author of the text that is published elsewhere under different terms, that does not affect their right to post it here under the CC-BY-SA and GFDL. Point them to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Donating_copyrighted_materials" title="Wikipedia:Donating copyrighted materials">Wikipedia:Donating copyrighted materials</a>.</li>
<li>Material from <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Public_domain_resources" title="Wikipedia:Public domain resources">public domain resources</a> is sometimes republished with unclear or misleading copyright notices which may obscure the origin. Please see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Plagiarism" title="Wikipedia:Plagiarism">Wikipedia:Plagiarism</a> for how this is corrected.</li>
<li>Content might be copied from one Wikipedia article to another <i>or</i> an article from another language's Wikipedia might be translated and published here (bringing with it seemingly suspicious anomalies, particularly if the contributor's understanding of English and/or wikification is limited). As long as attribution is supplied to meet licensing requirements, these are not a copyright violation. Please see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copying_within_Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia:Copying within Wikipedia">Wikipedia:Copying within Wikipedia</a>.</li>
<li>Also, sometimes you will find text elsewhere on the Web that was copied from Wikipedia. In these cases, it is a good idea to make a note in the talk page to discourage such false alarms in the future. See &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:Backwardscopyvio" class="mw-redirect" title="Template:Backwardscopyvio">backwardscopyvio</a>&#125;&#125;.</li></ul></li></ul>
<ul><li><b>List the page on <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copyright_problems" title="Wikipedia:Copyright problems">Wikipedia:Copyright problems</a></b> - follow the instructions there, if you get no response, or you still have doubts.</li></ul>
<ul><li><b>Please see the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copyrights" title="Wikipedia:Copyrights">Wikipedia copyright policy</a> document for what to do in difficult cases</b>, such as where a user continues to post copyrighted material in spite of warnings.</li></ul>
