<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Adjective">Adjective</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=fils&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Adjective">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">fils</strong> (<i>not <a href="/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#comparable" title="Appendix:Glossary">comparable</a></i>)
</p>
<ol><li><span class="use-with-mention">Used after a proper name that is common to a father and his son to indicate that the son is being referred to rather than the father.</span></li></ol>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_notes">Usage notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=fils&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Usage notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<ul><li>Current usage of differentiating fathers and sons is borrowed from French; hence this term follows the name as it does in French grammar.</li></ul>

exist)">*dʰeh₁y-li-os</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">sucker</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, a derivation from the verbal root <i class="Latinx mention" lang="ine-pro"><a href="/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/d%CA%B0eh%E2%82%81(y)-" title="Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/dʰeh₁(y)-">*dʰeh₁(y)-</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">to suck</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>. Cognate to <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_language" class="extiw" title="w:Portuguese language">Portuguese</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="pt"><a href="/wiki/filho#Portuguese" title="filho">filho</a></i>, <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_language" class="extiw" title="w:Spanish language">Spanish</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="es"><a href="/wiki/hijo#Spanish" title="hijo">hijo</a></i><span class="serial-comma">,</span> and <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_language" class="extiw" title="w:Italian language">Italian</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="it"><a href="/wiki/figlio#Italian" title="figlio">figlio</a></i>, among others.
</p><p>Final <i>-s</i> regularly became mute before consonants in late Old French but was then still pronounced in <a href="/wiki/pausa" title="pausa">pausa</a>. In the 18th century, these pausal forms widely fell out of use; they remained, however, as variants in a small number of words (cf. <i class="Latn mention" lang="fr"><a href="/wiki/tous#French" title="tous">tous</a></i>, <i class="Latn mention" lang="fr"><a href="/wiki/ours#French" title="ours">ours</a></i>). By the 20th century, the regular pronunciation <span class="IPA">/fi/</span> had become archaic or dialectal.
</p>
