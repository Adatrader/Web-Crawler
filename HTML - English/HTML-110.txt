<p><b>Darrell Charles Schweitzer</b> (born August 27, 1952) is an American writer, editor, and critic in the field of <a href="/wiki/Speculative_fiction" title="Speculative fiction">speculative fiction</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SFE_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SFE-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Much of his focus has been on <a href="/wiki/Dark_fantasy" title="Dark fantasy">dark fantasy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horror_fiction" title="Horror fiction">horror</a>, although he does also work in <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fantasy" title="Fantasy">fantasy</a>.  Schweitzer is also a prolific writer of literary criticism and editor of collections of essays on various writers within his preferred genres.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darrell_Schweitzer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Schweitzer was born in <a href="/wiki/Woodbury,_New_Jersey" title="Woodbury, New Jersey">Woodbury, New Jersey</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-SFE_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SFE-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-StJames_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StJames-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CAO_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CAO-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> son of Francis Edward and Mary Alice Schweitzer.<sup id="cite_ref-CAO_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CAO-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Villanova_University" title="Villanova University">Villanova University</a> from 1970–1976, from which he received a B.S. in geography (1974) and an M.A. in English (1976).<sup id="cite_ref-StJames_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StJames-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CAO_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CAO-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He started his literary career as a reviewer and columnist.<sup id="cite_ref-CAO_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CAO-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He worked as an editorial assistant for <i><a href="/wiki/Asimov%27s_Science_Fiction" title="Asimov&#39;s Science Fiction">Isaac Asimov's SF Magazine</a></i> from 1977–1982 and <i><a href="/wiki/Amazing_Stories" title="Amazing Stories">Amazing Stories</a></i> from 1982–1986, was co-editor with <a href="/wiki/George_H._Scithers" title="George H. Scithers">George H. Scithers</a> and  <a href="/wiki/John_Gregory_Betancourt" title="John Gregory Betancourt">John Gregory Betancourt</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Weird_Tales" title="Weird Tales">Weird Tales</a></i> from 1987–1990 and sole editor of the same magazine from 1991–1994 and its successor, <i>Worlds of Fantasy &amp; Horror</i>, from 1994–1996. From 1998–2007 he was again co-editor of the revived <i>Weird Tales</i>, first with Scithers and then with Scithers and Betancourt. He has also been a part-time literary agent for the Owlswick Agency in Philadelphia.<sup id="cite_ref-StJames_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StJames-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and a World Fantasy Award judge.<sup id="cite_ref-CAO_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CAO-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He is a member of <a href="/wiki/Science_Fiction_and_Fantasy_Writers_of_America" title="Science Fiction and Fantasy Writers of America">Science Fiction Writers of America</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horror_Writers_Association" title="Horror Writers Association">Horror Writers of America</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CAO_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CAO-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He lives and works in the Philadelphia area.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fiction">Fiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darrell_Schweitzer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most of Schweitzer's fiction is in the areas of dark fantasy and horror. He works most frequently in fiction of shorter lengths, though he has also written a number of novels. His first, <i><a href="/wiki/The_White_Isle" title="The White Isle">The White Isle</a></i>, an epic, disillusioning quest to the underworld, was written in 1976 but remained unpublished until 1989. <i><a href="/wiki/The_Shattered_Goddess" title="The Shattered Goddess">The Shattered Goddess</a></i> (1982) takes place in a far future "<a href="/wiki/Dying_Earth_(subgenre)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dying Earth (subgenre)">Dying Earth</a>" setting, which he later revisited for a sequence of short stories collected as <i><a href="/wiki/Echoes_of_the_Goddess" title="Echoes of the Goddess">Echoes of the Goddess</a></i> (2013). The first work in his tales of the world of the Great River focusing on child-sorcerer Sekenre, "To Become a Sorcerer" (1991), was nominated for the 1992 <a href="/wiki/World_Fantasy_Award%E2%80%94Long_Fiction" title="World Fantasy Award—Long Fiction">World Fantasy Award for Best Novella</a> and later expanded into the novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mask_of_the_Sorcerer" title="The Mask of the Sorcerer">The Mask of the Sorcerer</a></i> (1995). Additional stories in the series have been collected in <i><a href="/wiki/Sekenre:_The_Book_of_the_Sorcerer" title="Sekenre: The Book of the Sorcerer">Sekenre: The Book of the Sorcerer</a></i> (2004). His latest novel, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dragon_House" title="The Dragon House">The Dragon House</a></i> (2018), melds his customary dark tone with elements of humor in a lighter work for young adults. Other works include his stories of the lapsed knight Julian, most collected in <i><a href="/wiki/We_Are_All_Legends" title="We Are All Legends">We Are All Legends</a></i> (1981),<sup id="cite_ref-StJames_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StJames-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> his tales of legendary madman <a href="/wiki/Tom_O%27Bedlam" class="mw-redirect" title="Tom O&#39;Bedlam">Tom O'Bedlam</a>, numerous works utilizing <a href="/wiki/H._P._Lovecraft" title="H. P. Lovecraft">H. P. Lovecraft</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Cthulhu_Mythos" title="Cthulhu Mythos">Cthulhu Mythos</a>, and a large body of unconnected short stories.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nonfiction">Nonfiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darrell_Schweitzer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Nonfiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Schweitzer is an authority on the history of speculative fiction and has written numerous critical and bibliographical works on both the field in general and such writers as <a href="/wiki/Lord_Dunsany" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord Dunsany">Lord Dunsany</a>, <a href="/wiki/H._P._Lovecraft" title="H. P. Lovecraft">H. P. Lovecraft</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Robert_E._Howard" title="Robert E. Howard">Robert E. Howard</a>. Many of his essays, reviews and author interviews have been collected into book form. He has also edited a number of anthologies and short story collections.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darrell_Schweitzer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Together with his editorial colleagues Schweitzer won the 1992 <a href="/wiki/World_Fantasy_Special_Award:_Professional" class="mw-redirect" title="World Fantasy Special Award: Professional">World Fantasy Award</a> special award in the professional category for <i>Weird Tales</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-StJames_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StJames-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His poem <i>Remembering the Future</i> won the 2006 <i>Asimov's Science Fiction'</i>s Readers' Award for best poem.
</p>
