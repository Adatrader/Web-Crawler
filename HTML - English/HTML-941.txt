	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Cambridge English: First</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikibooks, open books for an open world</div>
		<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">(Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=FCE_English&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="FCE English">FCE English</a>)</span></div>
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><br />
Cambridge English: First (First Certificate in English (FCE)) is a qualification offered by Cambridge University for learners of English as second language.  It is intended for students of an Upper Intermediate (CEFR B2 Level) to Advanced level of English who normally have studied the language for at least 5 years.  FCE is currently the most popular certificate for learners of English and the exam can be sat in most countries around the world. This book aims to provide a guide to students who plan on taking this exam. It will not deal directly with English grammar or vocabulary but instead will concentrate on the background to the exam and what is required for each paper.
</p><p>For grammar, vocabulary and structure of the English language see the <a href="/wiki/English" class="mw-redirect" title="English">English</a> or <a href="/wiki/English_as_an_Additional_Language" title="English as an Additional Language">English as an Additional Language</a> Wikibooks.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span id="What_is_FCE.3F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="What_is_FCE?">What is FCE?</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambridge_English:_First&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: What is FCE?">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h2><span id="What_are_the_papers_of_the_FCE_exam.3F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="What_are_the_papers_of_the_FCE_exam?">What are the papers of the FCE exam?</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambridge_English:_First&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: What are the papers of the FCE exam?">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are 4 papers in the FCE exam.  Before sitting the exams, you should find out what is expected of you by practising questions on the Internet or enrolling in a course.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span id="Paper_1_-_Reading_and_Use_of_English_.281_hour_15_min.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Paper_1_-_Reading_and_Use_of_English_(1_hour_15_min)">Paper 1 - Reading and Use of English (1 hour 15 min)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambridge_English:_First&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Paper 1 - Reading and Use of English (1 hour 15 min)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:332px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Three_cliffs_bay_gower_rb_200507.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Three_cliffs_bay_gower_rb_200507.jpg/330px-Three_cliffs_bay_gower_rb_200507.jpg" decoding="async" width="330" height="248" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Three_cliffs_bay_gower_rb_200507.jpg/495px-Three_cliffs_bay_gower_rb_200507.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Three_cliffs_bay_gower_rb_200507.jpg/660px-Three_cliffs_bay_gower_rb_200507.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Three_cliffs_bay_gower_rb_200507.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Three Cliffs Bay, Gower, Wales</div></div></div>
<p>The Reading and Use of English paper consists of 7 parts:
</p>
<ol><li>Part 1  - Multiple choice</li>
<li>Part 2- Gap-fill</li>
<li>Part 3 - Gap-fill</li>
<li>Part 4 - Key Word Transformations</li>
<li>Part 5 - Multiple choice reading</li>
<li>Part 6 - Paragraph matching</li>
<li>Part 7 - Matching</li></ol>
<h2><span id="Paper_2_-_Writing_.281_hour_20_min.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Paper_2_-_Writing_(1_hour_20_min)">Paper 2 - Writing (1 hour 20 min)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambridge_English:_First&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Paper 2 - Writing (1 hour 20 min)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Writing paper consists of 2 parts:
</p>
<ol><li>Part 1 - Essay (compulsory)</li>
<li>Part 2 - One writing task from a choice of three</li></ol>
<h2><span id="Paper_3_-_Listening_.2840_min.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Paper_3_-_Listening_(40_min)">Paper 3 - Listening (40 min)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambridge_English:_First&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Paper 3 - Listening (40 min)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Listening paper consists of 4 parts:
</p>
<ol><li>Part 1 - Multiple choice</li>
<li>Part 2 - Note taking / sentence completion</li>
<li>Part 3 - Multiple matching</li>
<li>Part 4 - Choosing from 2 or 3 answers</li></ol>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Paper_4_-_Speaking">Paper 4 - Speaking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambridge_English:_First&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Paper 4 - Speaking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Speaking paper consists of 4 parts:
</p>
<ol><li>Part 1 - Interview</li>
<li>Part 2 - Individual long turn task</li>
<li>Part 3 - Collaborative task</li>
<li>Part 4 - Discussion</li></ol>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scoring">Scoring</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambridge_English:_First&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Scoring">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Candidates who pass Cambridge English: First receive either a <i>B1 Level</i> certificate, a <i>C</i>, a <i>B</i> or an <i>A</i>.  Grades C-A are considered to be real passes.  Grades C and B are passes at Cambridge English: First level (CEFR B2) while an <i>A</i> grade is equivalent to C1 level.
</p>
