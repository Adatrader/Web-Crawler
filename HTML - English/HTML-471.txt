<p><b>Indiana University</b> (<b>IU</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/University_system" title="University system">system</a> of <a href="/wiki/Public_university" title="Public university">public universities</a> in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Indiana University has a combined student body of more than 110,000 students, which includes approximately 46,000 students enrolled at the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_Bloomington" title="Indiana University Bloomington">Indiana University Bloomington</a> campus.<sup id="cite_ref-10Factbook_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10Factbook-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campuses">Campuses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Indiana_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Campuses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Indiana University has two core campuses and seven regional campuses. Each one of the campuses is an accredited, four-year degree-granting institution.
</p><p>The flagship campus of Indiana University is located in <a href="/wiki/Bloomington,_Indiana" title="Bloomington, Indiana">Bloomington</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_Bloomington" title="Indiana University Bloomington">Indiana University Bloomington</a> (IU Bloomington) is the location of Indiana University.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Bloomington campus is home to numerous premier Indiana University schools, including the College of Arts and Sciences, the <a href="/wiki/Jacobs_School_of_Music" title="Jacobs School of Music">Jacobs School of Music</a>, an extension of the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_School_of_Medicine" title="Indiana University School of Medicine">Indiana University School of Medicine</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_School_of_Informatics" title="Indiana University School of Informatics">School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering</a>, which includes the former School of Library and Information Science (now Department of Library and Information Science), School of Optometry, the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_School_of_Public_and_Environmental_Affairs" class="mw-redirect" title="Indiana University School of Public and Environmental Affairs">O'Neil School of Public and Environmental Affairs</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_Maurer_School_of_Law" title="Indiana University Maurer School of Law">Maurer School of Law</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_School_of_Education" title="Indiana University School of Education">School of Education</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Kelley_School_of_Business" title="Kelley School of Business">Kelley School of Business</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_%E2%80%93_Purdue_University_Indianapolis" title="Indiana University – Purdue University Indianapolis">Indiana University – Purdue University Indianapolis</a> (IUPUI) is an urban expansion, co-locating degree programs of Indiana University alongside those of <a href="/wiki/Purdue_University" title="Purdue University">Purdue University</a> and extending public higher education to the capitol.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Located just west of downtown Indianapolis, it is the central location of several Indiana University schools, including the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_School_of_Medicine" title="Indiana University School of Medicine">School of Medicine</a>, the IU School of Health and Rehabilitation Sciences, the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_School_of_Dentistry" title="Indiana University School of Dentistry">School of Dentistry</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Kelley_School_of_Business" title="Kelley School of Business">Kelley School of Business</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_School_of_Nursing" title="Indiana University School of Nursing">School of Nursing</a>, the School of Social Work, the Indiana University administrated <a href="/wiki/Herron_School_of_Art_and_Design" title="Herron School of Art and Design">Herron School of Art and Design</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_Robert_H._McKinney_School_of_Law" title="Indiana University Robert H. McKinney School of Law">Robert H. McKinney School of Law</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>In addition to its core campuses, Indiana University comprises seven extensions throughout <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>:<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_East" title="Indiana University East">Indiana University East</a> (IU East) is located in <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Indiana" title="Richmond, Indiana">Richmond</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_Fort_Wayne" title="Indiana University Fort Wayne">Indiana University Fort Wayne</a> (IU Fort Wayne), the system's newest campus, is located in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Wayne,_Indiana" title="Fort Wayne, Indiana">Fort Wayne</a>. It was established in 2018 after the dissolution of the former entity <a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_%E2%80%93_Purdue_University_Fort_Wayne" title="Indiana University – Purdue University Fort Wayne">Indiana University – Purdue University Fort Wayne</a> (IPFW), which had been an extension similar to that of IUPUI under the administration of <a href="/wiki/Purdue_University" title="Purdue University">Purdue University</a>. IU Fort Wayne took over IPFW's academic programs in health sciences, with all other IPFW academic programs taken over by the new entity, <a href="/wiki/Purdue_University_Fort_Wayne" title="Purdue University Fort Wayne">Purdue University Fort Wayne</a> (PFW).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_Kokomo" title="Indiana University Kokomo">Indiana University Kokomo</a> (IU Kokomo) is located in <a href="/wiki/Kokomo,_Indiana" title="Kokomo, Indiana">Kokomo</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_Northwest" title="Indiana University Northwest">Indiana University Northwest</a> (IU Northwest) is located in <a href="/wiki/Gary,_Indiana" title="Gary, Indiana">Gary</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_South_Bend" title="Indiana University South Bend">Indiana University South Bend</a> (IU South Bend) is located in <a href="/wiki/South_Bend,_Indiana" title="South Bend, Indiana">South Bend</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_Southeast" title="Indiana University Southeast">Indiana University Southeast</a> (IU Southeast) is located in <a href="/wiki/New_Albany,_Indiana" title="New Albany, Indiana">New Albany</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_%E2%80%93_Purdue_University_Columbus" title="Indiana University – Purdue University Columbus">Indiana University – Purdue University Columbus</a> (IUPUC) is located in <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Indiana" title="Columbus, Indiana">Columbus</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Endowment">Endowment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Indiana_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Endowment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the National Association of College and University Business Officers (NACUBO), the value of the endowment of the Indiana University and affiliated foundations in 2016 is over $1.986 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The annual budget across all campuses totals over $3 Billion.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Indiana University Research and Technology Corporation (IURTC) is a not-for-profit agency that assists IU faculty and researchers in realizing the commercial potential of their discoveries. Since 1997, university clients have been responsible for more than 1,800 inventions, nearly 500 patents, and 38 start-up companies.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the 2016 Fiscal Year alone, the IURTC was issued 53 U.S. patents and 112 global patents.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Indiana_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Indiana University has three medals to recognize individuals.<sup id="cite_ref-medals_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-medals-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/University_Medal" title="University Medal">University Medal</a>, the only IU medal that requires approval from the Board of Trustees, was created in 1982 by then IU President <a href="/wiki/John_W._Ryan" title="John W. Ryan">John W. Ryan</a> and is the highest award bestowed by the University. It honors individuals for singular or noteworthy contributions, including service to the university and achievement in arts, letters, science, and law. The first recipient was Thomas T. Solley, former director of the IU Art Museum.<sup id="cite_ref-medals_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-medals-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Indiana University President's Medal for Excellence honors individuals for distinction in public service, service to Indiana University, achievement in a profession, and/or extraordinary merit and achievement in the arts, humanities, science, education, and industry.<sup id="cite_ref-medals_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-medals-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The first recipients were member of the <a href="/wiki/Beaux_Arts_Trio" title="Beaux Arts Trio">Beaux Arts Trio</a> on September 20, 1985.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Hart_Benton_(painter)" title="Thomas Hart Benton (painter)">Thomas Hart Benton</a> Mural Medallion "recognizes individuals who are shining examples of the values of IU and the universal academic community." President Ryan was the first to award this honor. It was first awarded to the president of Nanjing University on July 21, 1986. It honors individuals for distinction in public office or service, a significant relationship to Indiana University or Indiana, significant service to IU programs, students, or faculty, significant contribution to research or support for research.<sup id="cite_ref-medals_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-medals-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>Indiana University has several ways to recognize the accomplishments of faculty.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Distinguished Professorships – Indiana University's most prestigious academic appointment</li>
<li>University Distinguished Teaching Awards – recognizing "shining examples of dedication and excellence"</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Ehrlich" title="Thomas Ehrlich">Thomas Ehrlich</a> Award for Excellence in Service Learning – recognizing excellence in service-learning. The recipient is also the IU nominee for the national <a href="/wiki/Campus_Compact" title="Campus Compact">Campus Compact Thomas Ehrlich Award for Service Learning</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Symbols">Symbols</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Indiana_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Symbols">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The Mace, a symbol of authority dating back to medieval times, when it was a studded, club-like weapon that was made of iron and could break armor. It later would be used in processions of city mayors and other dignitaries and became an emblem of order and authority during academic ceremonies. The staff of IU's Mace is 30 inches long and made of polished ebony encircled with four brass, gold-plated collars and entwined by swirled gold bands. Atop the staff is a globe of plated brass with four flat sides. The sides of the globe are embossed with IU's seal, the seal of the state of Indiana, the emblematic initials "IU," and the donor inscription. The Mace was presented to the university by Indiana Alpha of <a href="/wiki/Phi_Delta_Theta" title="Phi Delta Theta">Phi Delta Theta</a> in 1949. Mounted on the globe of the Mace are 12 large synthetic jewels of blue sapphire, ruby, garnet, and topaz. Atop this rests an eagle with outstretched wings.
</p><p>The Jewel and Chain of Office are worn by the university president at ceremonial occasions. The Jewel of Office is handcrafted of gold-plated sterling silver and precious jewels. Each part of the design has a symbolic meaning that reflects IU's historic origin and educational mission, noting such things as the number of states in the Union when the university was founded in 1820 (22 states), the year Indiana became a state (1816), and the years that mark IU's evolution from a seminary to a university (1820, 1828 and 1838). The jewels in the item include emeralds, sapphires, topaz, rubies, and diamonds. The Jewel of Office was presented to the university in 1946 by the Pi chapter of <a href="/wiki/Beta_Theta_Pi" title="Beta Theta Pi">Beta Theta Pi</a>. The Chain of Office was donated to the university in 1958 by the Lambda chapter of <a href="/wiki/Sigma_Chi" title="Sigma Chi">Sigma Chi</a>. The chain is handcrafted of gold-plated sterling silver and contains 44 linked panels, eight of which are engraved with the names of the presidents who have served the university since the Jewel of Office was first worn as the symbol of the presidency.
</p>
