<p><b>Daniel A. Bell</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>&#58; <span lang="zh-Hans">贝淡宁</span>; born 1964) is Dean of the School of Political Science and Public Administration at <a href="/wiki/Shandong_University" title="Shandong University">Shandong University</a> and professor at <a href="/wiki/Tsinghua_University" title="Tsinghua University">Tsinghua University</a> (Schwarzman College and Department of Philosophy). He was born in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, educated at McGill and Oxford, has taught in Singapore, Hong Kong and Shanghai, and has held research fellowships at <a href="/wiki/Princeton_University" title="Princeton University">Princeton's</a> University Center for Human Values, <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford's</a> Center for Advanced Study in the Behavioral Sciences and <a href="/wiki/Hebrew_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Hebrew University">Hebrew University</a>'s Department of Political Science. He has put forward his views in favour of China's political <a href="/wiki/Meritocracy" title="Meritocracy">meritocracy</a> and against one person one vote as a mode of selection for political leaders in two books<sup id="cite_ref-two_books_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-two_books-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and in comments published in <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> the <i><a href="/wiki/Financial_Times" title="Financial Times">Financial Times</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-FT_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FT-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> the <i><a href="/wiki/Global_Times" title="Global Times">Global Times</a></i> (<span lang="zh-Hans" title="Chinese language text">《环球时报》</span>),<sup id="cite_ref-Huanqiushibao_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Huanqiushibao-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and in regular columns published in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Huffington_Post" class="mw-redirect" title="The Huffington Post">The Huffington Post</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-Huffington_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Huffington-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Project_Syndicate" title="Project Syndicate">Project Syndicate</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Syndicate_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Syndicate-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> as well as the Chinese-language periodical <i><a href="/wiki/South_Reviews" title="South Reviews">South Reviews</a></i> (<span lang="zh-Hans" title="Chinese language text">《南风窗》</span>)<sup id="cite_ref-NFC_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NFC-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and a Chinese-language blog site on <i><a href="/wiki/Caijing" title="Caijing">Caijing</a></i> (<span lang="zh-Hans" title="Chinese language text">《财经》</span>).<sup id="cite_ref-CJW_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CJW-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He was the recipient of the Huilin Prize in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
