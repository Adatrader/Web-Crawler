<p>The <b>Salvador Dalí Museum</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Art_museum" title="Art museum">art museum</a> in <a href="/wiki/St._Petersburg,_Florida" title="St. Petersburg, Florida">St. Petersburg, Florida</a>, United States, dedicated to the works of <a href="/wiki/Salvador_Dal%C3%AD" title="Salvador Dalí">Salvador Dalí</a>. It houses the largest collection of Dalí's works outside Europe. It is located on the <a href="/wiki/Downtown_St._Petersburg_Historic_District" title="Downtown St. Petersburg Historic District">downtown St. Petersburg</a> waterfront by 5th Avenue Southeast, Bay Shore Drive, and Dan Wheldon Way. On April 18, 2012, the <a href="/wiki/American_Institute_of_Architects" title="American Institute of Architects">AIA</a>'s Florida Chapter placed the building on its list of <i>Florida Architecture: 100 Years. 100 Places</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salvador_Dal%C3%AD_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:TampaDaliMuseumExterior.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/TampaDaliMuseumExterior.jpg/220px-TampaDaliMuseumExterior.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="127" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/TampaDaliMuseumExterior.jpg/330px-TampaDaliMuseumExterior.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/TampaDaliMuseumExterior.jpg/440px-TampaDaliMuseumExterior.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1964" data-file-height="1136" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:TampaDaliMuseumExterior.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The old Salvador Dalí Museum facility</div></div></div>
<p>Shortly before marrying in 1942, <a href="/wiki/Reynolds_and_Eleanor_Morse" title="Reynolds and Eleanor Morse">Reynolds and Eleanor Morse</a> attended a Dalí retrospective at the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Museum_of_Art" title="Cleveland Museum of Art">Cleveland Museum of Art</a>. Intrigued by the artist's subject matter, and impressed by his draftsmanship, they bought their first painting a year later. This purchase began a 40-year relationship as patrons and friends of Dalí that resulted in a comprehensive collection of original Dalí work.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Until 1971, the Morses displayed their collection in their <a href="/wiki/Cleveland,_Ohio" class="mw-redirect" title="Cleveland, Ohio">Cleveland, Ohio</a>, home. When they loaned over 200 pieces to a Dalí retrospective in 1965, they realized that 25 years of collecting produced a mini-retrospective that needed a permanent home.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:StPetersburgDaliMuseumSignature.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/StPetersburgDaliMuseumSignature.jpg/220px-StPetersburgDaliMuseumSignature.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="117" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/StPetersburgDaliMuseumSignature.jpg/330px-StPetersburgDaliMuseumSignature.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/StPetersburgDaliMuseumSignature.jpg/440px-StPetersburgDaliMuseumSignature.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1843" data-file-height="983" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:StPetersburgDaliMuseumSignature.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Salvador Dalí's signature as seen on the outside of the museum</div></div></div>
<p>In March 1971, with Salvador Dalí presiding over the opening, the Morses opened a museum adjacent to their office building in <a href="/wiki/Beachwood,_Ohio" title="Beachwood, Ohio">Beachwood, Ohio</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Wach1996_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wach1996-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> By the end of the decade with an overwhelming number of visitors, the Morses decided to again move their collection.
</p><p>After a drawn out search which drew national attention, a marine warehouse in downtown St. Petersburg, Florida was rehabilitated and the museum opened on March 7, 1982.<sup id="cite_ref-Wach1996_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wach1996-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:St._Pete_Dali_Museum06.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/St._Pete_Dali_Museum06.jpg/220px-St._Pete_Dali_Museum06.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/St._Pete_Dali_Museum06.jpg/330px-St._Pete_Dali_Museum06.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/St._Pete_Dali_Museum06.jpg/440px-St._Pete_Dali_Museum06.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4288" data-file-height="3216" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:St._Pete_Dali_Museum06.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Salvador Dalí Museum picture from the side</div></div></div>
<p>In mid-2008, a new location for the Dali museum was announced. Designed by <a href="/wiki/Yann_Weymouth" title="Yann Weymouth">Yann Weymouth</a> of the architectural firm <a href="/wiki/Hellmuth,_Obata_and_Kassabaum" class="mw-redirect" title="Hellmuth, Obata and Kassabaum">HOK</a> and built by <a href="/wiki/The_Beck_Group" title="The Beck Group">The Beck Group</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> under the leadership of then-CEO <a href="/wiki/Henry_C._Beck_III" title="Henry C. Beck III">Henry C. Beck III</a>, it was built on the downtown waterfront next to the Mahaffey Theater, on the former site of the <a href="/wiki/Bayfront_Center" title="Bayfront Center">Bayfront Center</a>, an arena which had been demolished in 2004.
</p><p>The new, larger and more storm-secure museum opened on January 11, 2011.
</p><p>Reportedly costing over $30 million,<sup id="cite_ref-Adam2011_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Adam2011-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> the surrealism-inspired structure<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> features a large glass entryway and skylight<sup id="cite_ref-Post2011_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Post2011-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> made of 1.5 inch thick glass. Referred to as the "Enigma", the glass entryway is 75 feet tall and encompasses a spiral staircase.<sup id="cite_ref-Post2011_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Post2011-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The remaining walls are composed of 18-inch thick concrete, designed to protect the collection from hurricanes.<sup id="cite_ref-DeMonchaux2011_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DeMonchaux2011-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Atrium_of_Salvador_Dal%C3%AD_Museum_in_Florida.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Atrium_of_Salvador_Dal%C3%AD_Museum_in_Florida.jpg/220px-Atrium_of_Salvador_Dal%C3%AD_Museum_in_Florida.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="391" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Atrium_of_Salvador_Dal%C3%AD_Museum_in_Florida.jpg/330px-Atrium_of_Salvador_Dal%C3%AD_Museum_in_Florida.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Atrium_of_Salvador_Dal%C3%AD_Museum_in_Florida.jpg/440px-Atrium_of_Salvador_Dal%C3%AD_Museum_in_Florida.jpg 2x" data-file-width="918" data-file-height="1632" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Atrium_of_Salvador_Dal%C3%AD_Museum_in_Florida.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Atrium with spiral staircase</div></div></div>
<p>The Museum is a member of the <a href="/wiki/American_Alliance_of_Museums" title="American Alliance of Museums">American Alliance of Museums</a> (AAM)<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and of the <a href="/wiki/North_American_Reciprocal_Museums" title="North American Reciprocal Museums">North American Reciprocal Museums</a> program.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salvador_Dal%C3%AD_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The museum features a variety of different events for families to attend. Some events include performances, workshops, films, lectures, different types of fundraising, and food &amp; drink events. Many previous events have allowed members to participate in handmade holiday card workshops where participants created their very own holiday card including a Dalí theme. In addition, there has been various poetry performances which have occurred every Thursday of the month, and lectures such as, coffee with a curator, a presentation on a theme-oriented topic that discusses a variety of topics in relation to Dalí.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artworks">Artworks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salvador_Dal%C3%AD_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Artworks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The museum's collection includes 96 oil paintings, over 100 watercolors and drawings, 1,300 graphics, photographs, sculptures and objets d'art, and an extensive archival library.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The Museum Library contains over 7,000 volumes, exhibition and sale catalogs, video, sound recordings, and special collections related to Dalí's life, Surrealism, and the Avant-garde. The library also holds the donated collection of Albert Field, New York collector and Dalí archivist.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Recently, the museum opened a new exhibit named Dali Lives, which uses artificial intelligence for visors to experience an interactive modern day Dali.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Permanent collection displays are periodically rotated, and several temporary shows are mounted each year.
</p><p>The museum is home to 7 of the 18 "masterwork" paintings by Dalí (including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hallucinogenic_Toreador" title="The Hallucinogenic Toreador">The Hallucinogenic Toreador</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Discovery_of_America_by_Christopher_Columbus" title="The Discovery of America by Christopher Columbus">The Discovery of America by Christopher Columbus</a></i>), the most of any museum in the world. To be considered a masterwork these paintings must measure at least 5 feet (1.5&#160;m) in any direction, and have been worked on for over a year.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to displaying the work of Dalí, the museum aims to educate the public and promote understanding, enjoyment and scholarly examination of art through the exhibition of works by Dalí and artists of similar vision.
</p><p>With the exception of the <a href="/wiki/Dal%C3%AD_Theater-Museum" class="mw-redirect" title="Dalí Theater-Museum">Dalí Theater-Museum</a> created by Dalí himself in his home town of <a href="/wiki/Figueres" title="Figueres">Figueres</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, St. Petersburg's Dalí Museum has the world's largest collections of Dalí's works.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
