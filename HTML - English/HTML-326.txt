<p><b>Andrew M. Niccol</b> (born 10 June 1964)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> screenwriter, producer, and director. He wrote and directed <i><a href="/wiki/Gattaca" title="Gattaca">Gattaca</a></i> (1997), <i><a href="/wiki/Simone_(2002_film)" title="Simone (2002 film)">Simone</a></i> (2002), <i><a href="/wiki/Lord_of_War" title="Lord of War">Lord of War</a></i> (2005), <i><a href="/wiki/In_Time" title="In Time">In Time</a></i> (2011),  <i><a href="/wiki/The_Host_(2013_film)" title="The Host (2013 film)">The Host</a></i> (2013), and <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Kill" title="Good Kill">Good Kill</a></i> (2014).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He also wrote and co-produced <i><a href="/wiki/The_Truman_Show" title="The Truman Show">The Truman Show</a></i>, which earned an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">Academy Award</a> nomination for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Original_Screenplay" title="Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay">Best Original Screenplay</a> in 1999 and won a <a href="/wiki/BAFTA" class="mw-redirect" title="BAFTA">BAFTA</a> award for Best Screenplay. His films tend to explore social, cultural and political issues, as well as artificial realities or simulations.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His film <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Kill" title="Good Kill">Good Kill</a></i> was selected to compete for the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Lion" title="Golden Lion">Golden Lion</a> at the <a href="/wiki/71st_Venice_International_Film_Festival" title="71st Venice International Film Festival">71st Venice International Film Festival</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Venice_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Venice-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Deadline_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Deadline-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_and_personal_life">Early and personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Niccol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early and personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Niccol was born in <a href="/wiki/Paraparaumu" title="Paraparaumu">Paraparaumu</a>, New Zealand, and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Auckland" title="Auckland">Auckland</a>, where he attended <a href="/wiki/Auckland_Grammar_School" title="Auckland Grammar School">Auckland Grammar School</a> beginning in 1973. He left New Zealand at age 21 and began directing <a href="/wiki/Television_advertisements" class="mw-redirect" title="Television advertisements">TV ads</a> in London, which he did for more than ten years before his directorial debut, <i><a href="/wiki/Gattaca" title="Gattaca">Gattaca</a></i> (1997). During production of <i><a href="/wiki/S1m0ne" class="mw-redirect" title="S1m0ne">S1m0ne</a></i>, he met model and actress <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Roberts_(model)" title="Rachel Roberts (model)">Rachel Roberts</a>, with whom he has two children, Jack, born in 2003 (who also played "Young Nicolai" in the 2005 film <i><a href="/wiki/Lord_of_War" title="Lord of War">Lord of War</a></i>) and Ava, born in 2008.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Niccol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Directing">Directing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Niccol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Directing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Niccol has directed the films <i><a href="/wiki/Gattaca" title="Gattaca">Gattaca</a></i> (1997), <i><a href="/wiki/Simone_(2002_film)" title="Simone (2002 film)">Simone</a></i> (2002), <i><a href="/wiki/Lord_of_War" title="Lord of War">Lord of War</a></i> (2005), <i><a href="/wiki/In_Time" title="In Time">In Time</a></i> (2011),  <i><a href="/wiki/The_Host_(2013_film)" title="The Host (2013 film)">The Host</a></i> (2013), and <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Kill" title="Good Kill">Good Kill</a></i> (2014) (reuniting after 17 years with actor <a href="/wiki/Ethan_Hawke" title="Ethan Hawke">Ethan Hawke</a> in a lead role; Hawke also appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Lord_of_War" title="Lord of War">Lord of War</a></i> as a supporting character named Jack Valentine). He has also directed a short film entitled <i>The Minutes</i> (2012), which is a documentary-esque, narrative tie-in to <i><a href="/wiki/In_Time" title="In Time">In Time</a></i> that describes in more detail the world and characters from the film.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For his directorial debut and first film (which he also wrote), <i><a href="/wiki/Gattaca" title="Gattaca">Gattaca</a></i> (1997), he won a Best Film award from the <a href="/wiki/Sitges_Film_Festival" title="Sitges Film Festival">Sitges - Catalan International Film Festival</a> and both a Special Jury Prize and the Fun Trophy from the <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A9rardmer_Film_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Gérardmer Film Festival">Gérardmer Film Festival</a>.
</p><p>For his film <i><a href="/wiki/Lord_of_War" title="Lord of War">Lord of War</a></i> (2005), he received a Special Recognition for Excellence in Filmmaking from the <a href="/wiki/National_Board_of_Review" title="National Board of Review">National Board of Review</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_and_producing">Writing and producing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Niccol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Writing and producing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Niccol's breakthrough screenplay was his script for the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Truman_Show" title="The Truman Show">The Truman Show</a></i> (1998), directed by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Weir" title="Peter Weir">Peter Weir</a> and starring <a href="/wiki/Jim_Carrey" title="Jim Carrey">Jim Carrey</a>. He also served as a producer on the film. The film received an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a> nomination for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Writing_Original_Screenplay" class="mw-redirect" title="Academy Award for Writing Original Screenplay">Best Original Screenplay</a> (Best Writing, Screenplay Written Directly for the Screen) and a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globes" class="mw-redirect" title="Golden Globes">Golden Globes</a> nomination for Best Screenplay in 1999 and won a <a href="/wiki/BAFTA" class="mw-redirect" title="BAFTA">BAFTA</a> award for Best Screenplay, a <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Award_for_Best_Writing" title="Saturn Award for Best Writing">Saturn Award for Best Writing</a> or Best Writer, an Awards Circuit Community Award for Best Original Screenplay and Best Motion Picture, a <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Award_for_Best_Dramatic_Presentation" title="Hugo Award for Best Dramatic Presentation">Hugo Award for Best Dramatic Presentation</a> (shared with <a href="/wiki/Peter_Weir" title="Peter Weir">Peter Weir</a>), and an <a href="/wiki/Online_Film_Critics_Society" title="Online Film Critics Society">Online Film Critics Society</a> Award for Best Original Screenplay.
</p><p>In 1999, Niccol received the ALFS Award for "Screenwriter of the Year" from the <a href="/wiki/London_Critics_Circle_Film_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="London Critics Circle Film Awards">London Critics Circle Film Awards</a> for his screenwriting work on the screenplays of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Truman_Show" title="The Truman Show">The Truman Show</a></i> (1998) and <i><a href="/wiki/Gattaca" title="Gattaca">Gattaca</a></i> (1997).
</p><p>Niccol has written for all the films that he has directed, including <i><a href="/wiki/Gattaca" title="Gattaca">Gattaca</a></i> (1997), <i><a href="/wiki/Simone_(2002_film)" title="Simone (2002 film)">Simone</a></i> (2002), <i><a href="/wiki/Lord_of_War" title="Lord of War">Lord of War</a></i> (2005), <i><a href="/wiki/In_Time" title="In Time">In Time</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Host_(2013_film)" title="The Host (2013 film)">The Host</a></i> (2013), and <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Kill" title="Good Kill">Good Kill</a></i> (2014). Out of the films he has written and directed, he has produced <i><a href="/wiki/S1m0ne" class="mw-redirect" title="S1m0ne">S1m0ne</a></i> (2002), <i><a href="/wiki/Lord_of_War" title="Lord of War">Lord of War</a></i> (2005), <i><a href="/wiki/In_Time" title="In Time">In Time</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Kill" title="Good Kill">Good Kill</a></i> (2014) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anon_(film)" title="Anon (film)">Anon</a></i> (2018).
</p><p>He also wrote and came up with the story for the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Terminal" title="The Terminal">The Terminal</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Steven_Spielberg" title="Steven Spielberg">Steven Spielberg</a>. He also served as an executive producer on the film.
</p>
