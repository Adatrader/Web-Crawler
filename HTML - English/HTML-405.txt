<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Latin">Latin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=caballus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Latin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=caballus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Disputed. Generally believed<sup id="cite_ref-origins_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-origins-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> to be from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaulish_language" class="extiw" title="w:Gaulish language">Gaulish</a></span>/<span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaulish_language" class="extiw" title="w:Gaulish language">Transalpine Gaulish</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="cel-gau"><a href="/w/index.php?title=cel-gau&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="cel-gau (page does not exist)">caballos</a></i>, from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Celtic_language" class="extiw" title="w:Proto-Celtic language">Proto-Celtic</a></span> <i class="Latinx mention" lang="cel-pro"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Reconstruction:Proto-Celtic/kapallos&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Reconstruction:Proto-Celtic/kapallos (page does not exist)">*kapallos</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>. This term is cognate to Welsh <i class="Latn mention" lang="cy"><a href="/wiki/ceffyl#Welsh" title="ceffyl">ceffyl</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-origins_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-origins-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Manx <i class="Latn mention" lang="gv"><a href="/wiki/cabbyl#Manx" title="cabbyl">cabbyl</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-origins_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-origins-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Scottish Gaelic and Irish <i class="Latn mention" lang="gd"><a href="/wiki/capall#Scottish_Gaelic" title="capall">capall</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-origins_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-origins-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Also compared is <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek" class="extiw" title="w:Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a></span> <i class="polytonic mention" lang="grc"><a href="/w/index.php?title=%CE%BA%CE%B1%CE%B2%CE%AC%CE%BB%CE%BB%CE%B7%CF%82&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="καβάλλης (page does not exist)">καβάλλης</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span lang="grc-Latn" class="mention-tr tr Latn">kabállēs</span>, <span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">nag</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, in turn possibly a borrowing from a Balkan, <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatolian_languages" class="extiw" title="w:Anatolian languages">Anatolian</a></span>, or northeast European language. Compare <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_language" class="extiw" title="w:Turkish language">Turkish</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="tr"><a href="/wiki/kaval#Turkish" title="kaval">kaval</a></i>, adjunct of <i class="Latn mention" lang="tr"><a href="/wiki/at#Turkish" title="at">at</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">horse</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Slavic" class="extiw" title="w:Proto-Slavic">Proto-Slavic</a></span> <i class="Latinx mention" lang="sla-pro"><a href="/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Slavic/kobyla" title="Reconstruction:Proto-Slavic/kobyla">*kobýla</a></i>.
</p><p>According to Watkins, the Greek and Latin terms could be <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iranian_languages" class="extiw" title="w:Iranian languages">Iranian</a></span> borrowings, such as <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saka_language" class="extiw" title="w:Saka language">Khotanese</a></span> <small>[script needed]</small> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span lang="kho-Latn" class="mention-tr tr Latn">kabä</span>, <span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">horse</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persian_language" class="extiw" title="w:Persian language">Persian</a></span> <i class="fa-Arab mention" lang="fa"><a href="/wiki/%DA%A9%D9%88%D9%84#Persian" title="کول">کول</a></i>&#8206; <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span lang="fa-Latn" class="mention-tr tr Latn">kaval</span>, <span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">slow, clumsy horse</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iranian_languages" class="extiw" title="w:Iranian languages">Old Iranian</a></span> <i class="Latinx mention" lang="iir-pro"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-Iranian/kaba&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-Iranian/kaba (page does not exist)">*kaba</a></i>, <i class="Latinx mention" lang="iir-pro"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-Iranian/kabala&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-Iranian/kabala (page does not exist)">*kabala</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">horse</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, related to <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin" class="extiw" title="w:Latin">Latin</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="la"><a href="/wiki/cabo#Latin" title="cabo">cabō</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">gelding</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span> and <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Church_Slavonic" class="extiw" title="w:Old Church Slavonic">Old Church Slavonic</a></span> <i class="Cyrs mention" lang="cu"><a href="/wiki/%D0%BA%D0%BE%D0%B1%EA%99%91%D0%BB%D0%B0#Old_Church_Slavonic" title="кобꙑла">кобꙑла</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span lang="cu-Latn" class="mention-tr tr Latn">kobyla</span>, <span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">mare</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, which could ultimately be of <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Indo-European_language" class="extiw" title="w:Proto-Indo-European language">Proto-Indo-European</a></span> origin.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pronunciation">Pronunciation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=caballus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pronunciation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_notes">Usage notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=caballus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Usage notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul><li>In Classical Latin, the word <i class="Latn mention" lang="la"><a href="/wiki/equus#Latin" title="equus">equus</a></i> is used for a horse, and <i>caballus</i> is used only by poets. It is only later, in Vulgar and Late Latin, that <i>caballus</i> appears in prose.</li></ul>
