<p><b>Dean Baker</b> (born July 13, 1958) is an <a href="/wiki/Americans" title="Americans">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Macroeconomist" class="mw-redirect" title="Macroeconomist">macroeconomist</a> and co-founder, with <a href="/wiki/Mark_Weisbrot" title="Mark Weisbrot">Mark Weisbrot</a>, of the <a href="/wiki/Center_for_Economic_and_Policy_Research" title="Center for Economic and Policy Research">Center for Economic and Policy Research</a> (CEPR) in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a> He is credited as being one of the first economists to have identified the 2007–2008 <a href="/wiki/United_States_housing_bubble" title="United States housing bubble">United States housing bubble</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SwarthmoreBulletin-BubbleBuster-2010_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SwarthmoreBulletin-BubbleBuster-2010-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dean_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baker grew up in the <a href="/wiki/Lake_View,_Chicago" title="Lake View, Chicago">Lake View</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WhatBestCollegeStudentsDo-2012_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WhatBestCollegeStudentsDo-2012-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>205–209</span></sup>
</p><p>In 1981, Baker graduated from <a href="/wiki/Swarthmore_College" title="Swarthmore College">Swarthmore College</a> with a bachelor's degree in history with minors in economics and philosophy. In 1983, he received a master's degree in economics from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Denver" title="University of Denver">University of Denver</a>. In 1988, he received a PhD from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a> in economics.<sup id="cite_ref-PhD-LogicNeoClassicalConsumptionTheory-1988_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PhD-LogicNeoClassicalConsumptionTheory-1988-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USHouseRep-CV-2014_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USHouseRep-CV-2014-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dean_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 1988 to 1989, Baker was a lecturer at <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a> and then, from 1989 to 1992, an assistant professor of economics at <a href="/wiki/Bucknell_University" title="Bucknell University">Bucknell University</a>. From 1992 to 1998, Baker was an economist at the <a href="/wiki/Economic_Policy_Institute" title="Economic Policy Institute">Economic Policy Institute</a>. During this time, he published a paper with <a href="/wiki/Mark_Weisbrot" title="Mark Weisbrot">Mark Weisbrot</a> in a journal of <a href="/wiki/Evolutionary_economics" title="Evolutionary economics">evolutionary economics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LogicOfContestedExchange-1994_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LogicOfContestedExchange-1994-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1999, Baker co-founded, together with economist <a href="/wiki/Mark_Weisbrot" title="Mark Weisbrot">Mark Weisbrot</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Center_for_Economic_and_Policy_Research" title="Center for Economic and Policy Research">Center for Economic and Policy Research</a> (CEPR), an independent, nonpartisan <a href="/wiki/Think_tank" title="Think tank">think tank</a>, which produces economic research on topics that affect people's lives to contribute to the public debate (social security, healthcare, the national budget), and internationally (global economy, International Monetary Fund, and Latin America policy).<sup id="cite_ref-CEPR-ClosingGap-Video-2011_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CEPR-ClosingGap-Video-2011-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Also in 1999, Baker was a senior research fellow at the Preamble Center for Public Policy.
</p><p>Baker has consulted with officials from the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a> and has provided testimony to the <a href="/wiki/Joint_Economic_Committee" class="mw-redirect" title="Joint Economic Committee">Joint Economic Committee</a> of the U.S. Congress and to the <a href="/wiki/OECD" title="OECD">OECD</a>'s Trade Union Advisory Council.
</p><p>From 1996 to 2006, Baker was the author of a weekly online commentary the economic reporting of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Washington_Post" title="The Washington Post">The Washington Post</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The <i>Economic Reporting Review</i> was published from 1996 to 2006. From 2006, he has continued this commentary on his weblog <i>Beat The Press,</i> which was formerly published at <i><a href="/wiki/The_American_Prospect" title="The American Prospect">The American Prospect</a></i>, but is now located at the CEPR website.
</p>
<h3><span id="2007.E2.80.932008_United_States_housing_bubble"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2007–2008_United_States_housing_bubble">2007–2008 United States housing bubble</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dean_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2007–2008 United States housing bubble">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Baker won the Revere Award,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (August 2018)">failed verification</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> along with Steve Keen and Nouriel Roubini, for predicting the crash of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_housing_bubble" title="United States housing bubble">United States housing bubble</a> and the resulting recession, which occurred from 2007 to 2008.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Reliable sources"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on an unreliable source. (August 2018)">unreliable source?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He warned about the coming crisis and the related government policies in multiple articles, op-eds and interviews from 2002 to 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-economicpredictions.org_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-economicpredictions.org-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Basing his outlook on house-price data-sets produced by the U.S. government, Baker asserted that there was a bubble in the US housing market in August 2002,<sup id="cite_ref-baker_bubble_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-baker_bubble-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> well before its peak,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and predicted that the collapse of this bubble would lead to recession. His prediction for when the recession would start was off by only one <a href="/wiki/Fiscal_quarter" class="mw-redirect" title="Fiscal quarter">quarter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Monthly_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Monthly-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Prosper_Australia_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Prosper_Australia-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Regarding the housing bubble, Baker was critical of chairman of the Federal Reserve, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Greenspan" title="Alan Greenspan">Alan Greenspan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He has been critical of the regulatory framework of the real estate and financial industries, the use of financial instruments like <a href="/wiki/Collateralized_debt_obligation" title="Collateralized debt obligation">collateralized debt obligation</a>, and the performance and <a href="/wiki/Conflict_of_interest" title="Conflict of interest">conflict of interest</a> of U.S. politicians and regulators.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Baker opposed the U.S. government bailout of Wall Street banks on the basis that the only people who stood to lose from their collapse were their shareholders and high-income CEOs.  Regarding any hypothetical, negative effects of not extending the bailout, he explained, "We know how to keep the financial system operating even as banks go into bankruptcy and receivership,"<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> citing U.S. government action taken during the <a href="/wiki/S%26L_crisis" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;L crisis">S&amp;L crisis</a> of the 1980s.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He has ridiculed the U.S. elite for favoring it, asking, "How do you make a DC intellectual look less articulate than <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Palin_interviews_with_Katie_Couric" class="mw-redirect" title="Sarah Palin interviews with Katie Couric">Sarah Palin being interviewed by Katie Couric</a>? That's easy. You ask them how failure to pass the bailout will give us a <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dean_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a graduate student at the University of Michigan, Baker participated in and was arrested at two sit-ins protesting Representative <a href="/wiki/Carl_Pursell" title="Carl Pursell">Carl Pursell</a>'s votes for military aid to the <a href="/wiki/Contras" title="Contras">Contras</a>. In 1986, Baker defeated Donald Grimes in the Democratic primary and ran unsuccessfully against Carl Pursell to represent Michigan's second Congressional district; his candidacy opposed aid to the Contras.<sup id="cite_ref-MichiganDaily-BakerPursellDebate-1986_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MichiganDaily-BakerPursellDebate-1986-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Nation-DeanBakerForCongress-1986_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nation-DeanBakerForCongress-1986-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MichiganDaily-Endorsement-1988_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MichiganDaily-Endorsement-1988-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Baker is married to economist Helene Jorgensen. They live in Washington, DC.<sup id="cite_ref-LATimes-HousingBubble-2009_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes-HousingBubble-2009-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
