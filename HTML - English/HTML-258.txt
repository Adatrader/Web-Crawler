<p>The <b>Wikimedia Foundation, Inc.</b> is the parent organization of the <b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia">Wikipedia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiktionary" title="Wiktionary">Wiktionary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikiquote" title="Wikiquote">Wikiquote</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikibooks" title="Wikibooks">Wikibooks</a></b> (including <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikijunior" class="extiw" title="w:Wikijunior">Wikijunior</a>), <b><a href="/wiki/Wikisource" title="Wikisource">Wikisource</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Commons" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikimedia Commons">Wikimedia Commons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikispecies" title="Wikispecies">Wikispecies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikinews" title="Wikinews">Wikinews</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikiversity" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikiversity">Wikiversity</a></b>, and <b><a href="/wiki/Meta-Wiki" class="mw-redirect" title="Meta-Wiki">Meta</a></b> collaborative projects. It is a <b><a href="/wiki/Wikiversity_NPC" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikiversity NPC">not-for-profit corporation</a></b> based in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St._Petersburg,_Florida" class="extiw" title="w:St. Petersburg, Florida">St. Petersburg</a>, <b><a href="/wiki/USA" class="mw-redirect" title="USA">USA</a></b>, and organized under the laws of the state of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florida" class="extiw" title="w:Florida">Florida</a>. Its existence was officially announced by <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jimmy_Wales" class="extiw" title="w:Jimmy Wales">Jimmy Wales</a>, who was hitherto running Wikipedia within his company <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bomis" class="extiw" title="w:Bomis">Bomis</a>, on <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/June_20" class="extiw" title="w:June 20">June 20</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2003" class="extiw" title="w:2003">2003</a>. Its approval by the U.S. <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internal_Revenue_Service" class="extiw" title="w:Internal Revenue Service">Internal Revenue Service</a>, by letter in April 2005, as an educational foundation in the category "Adult, Continuing Education" means all contributions to the Wikimedia Foundation are <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tax_deduction" class="extiw" title="w:Tax deduction">tax deductible</a> for U.S. federal income tax purposes. <i>see <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikimedia_Foundation" class="extiw" title="w:Wikimedia Foundation">w:Wikimedia Foundation</a> for more information</i>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wikiversity_and_Wikimedia">Wikiversity and Wikimedia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikimedia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Wikiversity and Wikimedia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="messagebox cleanup metadata plainlinks">
<table style="width:100%;background:none;">
<tbody><tr>
<td style="width:60px;text-align:center;vertical-align:top;padding:.3em;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nuvola_apps_kfig.svg" class="image" title="Dev icon"><img alt="Dev icon" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Nuvola_apps_kfig.svg/40px-Nuvola_apps_kfig.svg.png" decoding="async" width="40" height="40" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Nuvola_apps_kfig.svg/60px-Nuvola_apps_kfig.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Nuvola_apps_kfig.svg/80px-Nuvola_apps_kfig.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="109" data-file-height="110" /></a>
</td>
<td>This article is about the <b>development of <a href="/wiki/Wikiversity" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikiversity">Wikiversity</a></b>. <br />Please help Wikiversity by adding your ideas about what Wikiversity should become, or what it should do.
</td></tr></tbody></table></div>
<p>Wikiversity has a definite <b>role</b> within the Wikimedia <a href="/wiki/Metacommunity" title="Metacommunity">Metacommunity</a>. This learning project focuses on how to define and extend that role within a universal or university context, applying the traditional paradigm of how universities collect learning resourses and apply them to real-world needs. Because <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia">Wikipedia</a> focuses on the encyclopedic context, it must maintain a neutral point of view, prohibit and discourage <a href="/wiki/Wikiversity:Original_research" title="Wikiversity:Original research">original research</a>, prohibit and discourage <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_self-references" class="extiw" title="w:Wikipedia:Avoid self-references">self references</a> and other aspects of creating content that <b><a href="/wiki/UML/Glossary" title="UML/Glossary">constrain</a></b> it to the encyclopedic realm. Wikiversity, conversely, can enter these prohibitive spaces from the universal or university context. Let's give it a whirl...
</p>
<h3><span id="Lab:_Establishing_Wikiversity.27s_role_and_context"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Lab:_Establishing_Wikiversity's_role_and_context">Lab: Establishing Wikiversity's role and context</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikimedia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Lab: Establishing Wikiversity&#039;s role and context">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Wikiversity" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikiversity">Wikiversity</a> has a <b><a href="/wiki/UML/Glossary" title="UML/Glossary">role</a></b> to perform within the <a class="mw-selflink selflink">Wikimedia</a> context and within a much larger <b><a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a></b> context. This <a href="/wiki/Learning_project" class="mw-redirect" title="Learning project">learning project</a> lab will focus on creating a <a href="/wiki/UML" title="UML">relationship diagram</a> that shows what Wikimedia looks like. We begin by working with <a href="/wiki/Wikiversity:Templates" title="Wikiversity:Templates">templates</a>, starting with &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:Wikiversitysister-list" title="Template:Wikiversitysister-list">Wikiversitysister-list</a>&#125;&#125; and &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:WikiversityUsers" title="Template:WikiversityUsers">WikiversityUsers</a>&#125;&#125;.
</p><p><b>Challenge:</b> Create local articles for each of the <b>Wikimedia collaborative projects:</b>
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia">Wikipedia</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wiktionary" title="Wiktionary">Wiktionary</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wikiquote" title="Wikiquote">Wikiquote</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wikibooks" title="Wikibooks">Wikibooks</a> (including <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikijunior" class="extiw" title="w:Wikijunior">Wikijunior</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wikisource" title="Wikisource">Wikisource</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Commons" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikimedia Commons">Wikimedia Commons</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wikispecies" title="Wikispecies">Wikispecies</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wikinews" title="Wikinews">Wikinews</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wikiversity" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikiversity">Wikiversity</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Meta-Wiki" class="mw-redirect" title="Meta-Wiki">Meta-Wiki</a></li></ul>
<p><br />
Include in these articles an opening statement based on the Wikipedia article of the same name. Include also a section <b>Wikiversity and ...</b> like this one.
</p><p><b>Discussion:</b> Get in touch with folks like Jimbo, Angela, and other Wikimedia heavyweights. Let them know that we are interested in studying Wikimedia from the university perspective. Contact mediawiki developers, stewards, admins and other folks that help form policies and guidelines for Wikimedia collaborative efforts.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lab:_Strengthening_Wikiversity">Lab: Strengthening Wikiversity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikimedia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Lab: Strengthening Wikiversity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Learning_to_learn_a_wiki_way" title="Learning to learn a wiki way">Learning to learn a wiki way</a> article focuses on a "learn-by-doing" model. This model defines Wikiversity as a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/learning_community" class="extiw" title="w:learning community">learning community</a>. We can develop visualization tools through <a href="/wiki/Wikiversity:Templates" title="Wikiversity:Templates">templates</a>, <a href="/wiki/UML" title="UML">diagrams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portal:Portals" title="Portal:Portals">Portals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Help:Link" class="mw-redirect" title="Help:Link">navigational aids</a>, <a href="/wiki/Learning_object" class="mw-redirect" title="Learning object">learning objects</a> and a number of other instruments.

<p>Edit the <a href="/wiki/Wikiversity" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikiversity">Wikiversity</a> article to include links to the new local articles. Update &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:Wikiversitysister-list" title="Template:Wikiversitysister-list">Wikiversitysister-list</a>&#125;&#125; and &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:WikiversityUsers" title="Template:WikiversityUsers">WikiversityUsers</a>&#125;&#125; to link to them.
</p><p>The idea is to establish within Wikiversity a means to strengthen its effectiveness as an online university and Wikimedia's role as a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virtual_community_of_practice" class="extiw" title="w:Virtual community of practice">Virtual community of practice</a>.  <i>See also <b><a href="/wiki/Metacommunity" title="Metacommunity">Metacommunity</a></b></i>
</p><p><b>Discussion:</b> Get in touch with the folks that help form policies and guidelines for each of the sisterprojects. Participate in our own <b><a href="/wiki/Wikiversity:Colloquium#Wikimedia" title="Wikiversity:Colloquium">colloquium</a></b> to make other Wikiversitans aware of <b>this</b> learning project. Use the <a href="/wiki/Talk:Wikimedia" title="Talk:Wikimedia">talk page</a> to present problems and ideas concerning <a href="/wiki/Wikiversity" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikiversity">Wikiversity</a>'s relationship to its parent organization, <a class="mw-selflink selflink">Wikimedia</a>. 
</p><p><b>Examples:</b> 
</p>
<ul><li>One important discussion is taking place at <a href="/wiki/Talk:Wikibooks" title="Talk:Wikibooks">Talk:Wikibooks</a> about <a href="/wiki/Wikiversity_and_Wikibooks_services" title="Wikiversity and Wikibooks services">Wikiversity and Wikibooks services</a> that help determine how the two projects can interface with each other more effectively.</li>
<li>Another exemplary development is the relationship between <a href="/wiki/School:Journalism" title="School:Journalism">School of Journalism</a> with <a href="/wiki/Wikinews" title="Wikinews">Wikinews</a>.</li>
<li>Another open project called <a href="https://strategy.wikimedia.org/wiki/Main_Page" class="extiw" title="strategy:Main Page">strategy</a> has been put in place by the <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Mediawiki_Foundation" class="extiw" title="m:Mediawiki Foundation">Mediawiki Foundation</a> for coming up with long-term strategic planning for all of the Wikimedia projects and languages. See <i><a href="/wiki/Wikiversity:Strategy" title="Wikiversity:Strategy">Wikiversity:Strategy</a></i> and <i><a href="https://strategy.wikimedia.org/wiki/Wikiversity" class="extiw" title="strategy:Wikiversity">Strategy:Wikiversity</a></i></li>
<li><i>add others here!</i></li></ul>
