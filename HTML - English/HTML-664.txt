<p><b>Wikiversity</b> is a Wikimedia Foundation project devoted to learning resources, learning projects, and research for use in all levels, types, and styles of education from pre-school to university, including professional training and informal learning. We invite teachers, students, and researchers to join us in creating open educational resources and collaborative learning communities. 
</p><p>This page is an index of all Wikiversity' help and maintenance pages. The following articles contain guidance and information about Editing, Policies and guidelines, and participating in the Wikiversity community.
</p><p>Having trouble finding out what you need to know? If it's not covered by the <a href="/wiki/Wikiversity:FAQ" title="Wikiversity:FAQ">FAQ</a>, try asking at the <b><a href="/wiki/Wikiversity:Colloquium" title="Wikiversity:Colloquium">Colloquium</a></b> or the <b><a href="/wiki/Wikiversity:Help_desk" title="Wikiversity:Help desk">Wikiversity:Help desk</a></b>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="MediaWiki">MediaWiki</span></h3>
<p>The function of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MediaWiki" class="extiw" title="w:MediaWiki">MediaWiki</a> itself (the software of Wikiversity) is described at the <b><a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Help:Contents" class="extiw" title="m:Help:Contents">MediaWiki User's Guide</a></b> in Meta-wiki. The MediaWiki-handbook in Meta-wiki is not kept here in order to avoid duplicate maintenance work (the same applies to all help pages not specific to Wikiversity).
</p><p>If you suspect a software bug, ask for feedback at the <a href="/wiki/Wikiversity:C" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikiversity:C">Colloquium</a>, and then report it using the <b><a class="external text" href="https://bugs.wikimedia.org">BugZilla</a></b> bug report system. This is the only way to get MediaWiki developers to notice your bug reports.
</p><p><br />
</p>
