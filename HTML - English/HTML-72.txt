<p><b>Ancestral domain</b> or <b>ancestral lands</b> refers to the <a href="/wiki/Lands_inhabited_by_indigenous_peoples" title="Lands inhabited by indigenous peoples">lands, territories and resources of indigenous peoples</a>, particularly in the <a href="/wiki/Asia-Pacific" title="Asia-Pacific">Asia-Pacific</a> region. The term differs from <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_land_rights" title="Indigenous land rights">indigenous land rights</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aboriginal_title" title="Aboriginal title">Aboriginal title</a> or <a href="/wiki/Native_title_in_Australia" title="Native title in Australia">Native Title</a> by directly indicating relationship to land based on <a href="/wiki/Ancestry" class="mw-redirect" title="Ancestry">ancestry</a>, while <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Domain" class="extiw" title="wiktionary:Domain">domain</a> indicates relationships beyond material lands and territories, including <a href="/wiki/Spirituality" title="Spirituality">spiritual</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cultural" class="mw-redirect" title="Cultural">cultural</a> aspects that may not be acknowledged in <a href="/wiki/Land_titles" class="mw-redirect" title="Land titles">land titles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Legal_doctrine" title="Legal doctrine">legal doctrine</a> about <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">trading</a> <a href="/wiki/Ownership" title="Ownership">ownership</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concept">Concept</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ancestral_domain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Concept">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Indigenous peoples may prefer to be described as custodians or guardians of their ancestral domain or lands rather than as title owners or <a href="/wiki/Land_owner" class="mw-redirect" title="Land owner">land owners</a>.
</p><p>The concept of <a href="/wiki/Individual" title="Individual">individual</a> <a href="/wiki/Property_rights_(economics)" title="Property rights (economics)">property ownership</a> and <a href="/wiki/Land_tenure" title="Land tenure">land tenure</a> that can be traded was often introduced as part of <a href="/wiki/Colonialism" title="Colonialism">colonialism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> While the Western model of land ownership gives an individual the property right to control land as a <a href="/wiki/Commodity" title="Commodity">commodity</a>, indigenous conceptions have a cultural and spiritual character. They invoke a mutual responsibility and relationship between land and people.<sup id="cite_ref-journal_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-journal-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Professor <a href="/wiki/Mick_Dodson" title="Mick Dodson">Michael Dodson</a> <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Australia" title="Order of Australia">AM</a> described <a href="/wiki/Aboriginal_Australians" title="Aboriginal Australians">Aboriginal Australians</a>' idea of country using the term during his <a href="/wiki/Australian_of_the_Year" title="Australian of the Year">Australian of the Year</a> speech. He said:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886047036">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>For us, country is a word for all the values, places, resources, stories and cultural obligations associated with the area and its features. It in fact describes the entirety of our ancestral domains. So when we acknowledge traditional country, as increasingly people do so in Australia, it's no empty <a href="/wiki/Ritual" title="Ritual">ritual</a>: it's to acknowledge who we, the Aboriginal people, are and our place in this nation. In doing that, it seems to me, all Australians might have a clearer notion of who they are and where they stand in relation to their history and the land they live in.<sup id="cite_ref-museumvid_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-museumvid-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-country_protocol_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-country_protocol-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p></blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ancestral_domain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The term was used as early as the 1920s in the work of the <a href="/wiki/International_Labour_Organization" title="International Labour Organization">International Labour Organization</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ILO_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ILO-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Initially, the ILO was concerned with the situation of indigenous and tribal peoples in their roles as workers in the overseas colonies of European powers. It became increasingly evident that indigenous peoples were exposed to severe labour exploitation and had a need for special protection in cases where they were expelled from their ancestral domains only to become seasonal, migrant, bonded or home-based <a href="/wiki/Labourers" class="mw-redirect" title="Labourers">labourers</a>. This recognition led to adoption in 1930 of the ILO's <a href="/wiki/Forced_Labour_Convention" title="Forced Labour Convention">Forced Labour Convention</a> (No. 29).<sup id="cite_ref-ILO_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ILO-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the creation of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>, the ILO with the participation of other parts of the UN system created the <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_and_Tribal_Populations_Convention" class="mw-redirect" title="Indigenous and Tribal Populations Convention">Indigenous and Tribal Populations Convention</a> (No. 107). Convention No. 107 was adopted in 1957 as the first international treaty on this subject.<sup id="cite_ref-ILO_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ILO-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This involved the underlying assumption that the only possible future for indigenous and tribal peoples was integration into larger society, and that the state should make decisions regarding indigenous development. In 1986 an ILO committee of experts concluded that "the integrationist approach of the Convention was obsolete and that its application was detrimental in the modern world." <sup id="cite_ref-ILO_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ILO-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1988 and 1989, the revision of Convention No. 107 was on the agenda of the International Labour Conference (ILC) and in June 1989, the Indigenous and Tribal Peoples Convention (No. 169) was adopted.<sup id="cite_ref-ILO_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ILO-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This paved the way for the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Declaration_on_the_Rights_of_Indigenous_Peoples" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples">United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples</a> in 2007.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Documentation">Documentation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ancestral_domain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Documentation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Indigenous peoples in Asia have used <a href="/wiki/Counter-mapping" title="Counter-mapping">community mapping</a> to advocate for <a href="/wiki/Land_rights" class="mw-redirect" title="Land rights">land rights</a>. This mapping may include documenting <a href="/wiki/Sacred_sites" class="mw-redirect" title="Sacred sites">sacred sites</a> and <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/immaterial" class="extiw" title="wikt:immaterial">immaterial</a> but geographically-linked <a href="/wiki/Traditional_knowledge" title="Traditional knowledge">traditional knowledge</a> of land.<sup id="cite_ref-map1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-map1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-map2_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-map2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-map3_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-map3-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regional_contexts">Regional contexts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ancestral_domain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Regional contexts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Indonesia">Indonesia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ancestral_domain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Indonesia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, the Indonesian <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_Court" class="mw-redirect" title="Constitutional Court">Constitutional Court</a> amended the 1999 Law on Forestry to return land rights to indigenous peoples whose ancestral lands are forested.<sup id="cite_ref-court_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-court-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-redd_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-redd-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Indonesia, there have been many land conflicts regarding indigenous community territories in resource-rich <a href="/wiki/Kalimantan" title="Kalimantan">Kalimantan</a>. According to data quoted in the <a href="/wiki/Jakarta_Post" class="mw-redirect" title="Jakarta Post">Jakarta Post</a>, following the enactment of the Masterplan for the Acceleration and Expansion of Indonesian Economic Development (MP3EI) in 2011, some 135 communities became involved in conflict with businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-Indonews_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Indonews-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Many conflicts involve indigenous peoples' traditional cultures being uprooted by <a href="/wiki/Palm_oil" title="Palm oil">palm oil</a> plantations, or <a href="/wiki/Logging" title="Logging">logging</a> or <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mining</a> interests.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/NGOs" class="mw-redirect" title="NGOs">NGOs</a> in Indonesia set up the Ancestral Domain Registration Agency. A community mapper said the agency was created to "be prepared for the court ruling. If it the Constitutional Court ruled that customary forests belonged to indigenous peoples, we wanted to be able to show where those customary forests were located." <sup id="cite_ref-Indonews_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Indonews-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ancestral_domain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/List_of_traditional_territories_of_the_indigenous_peoples_of_North_America" title="List of traditional territories of the indigenous peoples of North America">List of traditional territories of the indigenous peoples of North America</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Registered_traplines&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Registered traplines (page does not exist)">registered traplines</a></div>
<p>The common term in Canada is "traditional territory".  This can refer the entire <a href="/wiki/Homeland" title="Homeland">homeland</a> of a large ethnic group or the particular hunting and trapping grounds of a small <a href="/wiki/Band_society" title="Band society">band society</a>.  In areas where treaties have been signed, it refers to the land outside of <a href="/wiki/Indian_reserves" class="mw-redirect" title="Indian reserves">Indian reserves</a> in which a particular indigenous nation still claims an interest.  In the case of specific rights to hunt and fish in a territory, these are called "<a href="/wiki/Aboriginal_rights" class="mw-redirect" title="Aboriginal rights">aboriginal rights</a>" and are a separate legal category from "<a href="/wiki/Treaty_rights" title="Treaty rights">treaty rights</a>".  In the case of areas where there are no treaties, such as most of <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, the Supreme Court of Canada has ruled that <a href="/wiki/Aboriginal_title" title="Aboriginal title">aboriginal title</a> applies to the entirety of a nation's claimed homeland, and not just to the particular locations of villages and other intensely-used sites.  This was a unanimous ruling by the court in the 2017 case <i><a href="/wiki/Tsilhqot%27in_Nation_v_British_Columbia" title="Tsilhqot&#39;in Nation v British Columbia">Tsilhqot'in Nation v British Columbia</a></i>.  However, in that same year, the Canadian court rejected the spiritual use of a site as being sufficient to establish aboriginal title, in <i><a href="/wiki/Ktunaxa_Nation_v_British_Columbia_(Forests,_Lands_and_Natural_Resource_Operations)" title="Ktunaxa Nation v British Columbia (Forests, Lands and Natural Resource Operations)">Ktunaxa Nation v British Columbia (Forests, Lands and Natural Resource Operations)</a></i>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Philippines">Philippines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ancestral_domain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Philippines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, the term is used to refer to indigenous peoples' <a href="/wiki/Land_rights" class="mw-redirect" title="Land rights">land rights</a> in law.<sup id="cite_ref-UP_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UP-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Ancestral lands are referred to in the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Philippines" title="Constitution of the Philippines">Philippines Constitution</a>. Article XII, Section 5 says: "The State, subject to the provisions of this Constitution and national development policies and programs, shall protect the rights of indigenous cultural communities to their ancestral lands to ensure their economic, social, and cultural well-being." <sup id="cite_ref-Inq_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Inq-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Food_and_Agriculture_Organization" title="Food and Agriculture Organization">Food and Agriculture Organization</a>'s research on forest land ownership in the Philippines found conflicts in institutional mandates among the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Code" class="mw-redirect" title="Local Government Code">Local Government Code</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mining_law" title="Mining law">mining law</a> and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Integrated_Protected_Areas_Act&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="National Integrated Protected Areas Act (page does not exist)">National Integrated Protected Areas Act</a>, and recommended exclusive resource use rights to community-based forest management communities.<sup id="cite_ref-law1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-law1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
