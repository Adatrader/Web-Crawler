	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Thomas A'Becket (composer)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Thomas a'Becket</b> (March 17, 1808 - January 6, 1890) was an actor and musician credited with writing the music and the words, in 1843, to "<a href="/wiki/Columbia,_the_Gem_of_the_Ocean" title="Columbia, the Gem of the Ocean">Columbia, the Gem of the Ocean</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-snac_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snac-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
Born in Chatham, England, A'Becket came to the United States in 1837 and spent much of his life in Philadelphia.<sup id="cite_ref-fuld_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fuld-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> At one time, he served as the stage manager of the actor <a href="/wiki/Edwin_Booth" title="Edwin Booth">Edwin Booth</a> and for many years was the director of the <a href="/wiki/Walnut_Street_Theatre" title="Walnut Street Theatre">Walnut Street Theatre</a>, in Philadelphia, where he also had a long acting career. During his early years in America, he gave music lessons and sang in operas.
</p><p>He died in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> from heart failure and was buried in the Fernwood Cemetery, west of the city..<sup id="cite_ref-snac_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snac-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> His diaries are in deposit at the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Public_Library" title="New York Public Library">New York Public Library</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> 
(Only one photograph of him is known to exist, and it is in Widener Library, of Harvard University.)
His son, Thomas a'Becket, Jr. (1843-1918), was also a musician and, in his youth, worked with <a href="/wiki/John_Philip_Sousa" title="John Philip Sousa">John Philip Sousa</a>.
</p><p>Despite the great renown of "Columbia, the Gem of the Ocean," A'Becket never received any fame from having composed it. This is quite probably because of the confusion over the anthem's authorship.
</p>
