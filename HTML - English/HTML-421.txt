	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Trésor de la langue française informatisé</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="infobox vcard" style="width:22em"><caption class="fn org">Trésor de la language française informatisé</caption><tbody><tr><th scope="row"><div style="padding:0.1em 0;line-height:1.2em;">Type of site</div></th><td>Digital dictionary</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Available&#160;in</th><td>French</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Created&#160;by</th><td>Computer Processing and Analysis of the French Language</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Website</th><td class="url"><span class="url"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://atilf.atilf.fr">atilf<wbr />.atilf<wbr />.fr</a></span></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Registration</th><td>None</td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>The <b>Trésor de la langue française informatisé</b> or <b>TLFi</b> ("Digitized Treasury of the French Language") is a digital version of the <i><a href="/wiki/Tr%C3%A9sor_de_la_langue_fran%C3%A7aise" title="Trésor de la langue française">Trésor de la langue française</a></i> ("Treasury of the French Language"), a 16-volume dictionary of the <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French language</a> of the 19th and 20th centuries, which was published between 1971 and 1994. It is freely available via a web interface. It was previously sold as a <a href="/wiki/CD-ROM" title="CD-ROM">CD-ROM</a> for Mac and Windows.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The TFLi was created by the <i><a href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Analyse_et_traitement_informatique_de_la_langue_fran%C3%A7aise" class="extiw" title="fr:Analyse et traitement informatique de la langue française">Analyse et traitement informatique de la langue française</a></i> (ATILF; Computer Processing and Analysis of the French Language) joint research group, a collaboration between the <a href="/wiki/Centre_national_de_la_recherche_scientifique" class="mw-redirect" title="Centre national de la recherche scientifique">French National Center for Scientific Research</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Lorraine" title="University of Lorraine">University of Lorraine</a>. French linguist <a href="/wiki/Alain_Rey" title="Alain Rey">Alain Rey</a> participated in its creation.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The director of the TFLi plans to integrate the evolution of the language.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tr%C3%A9sor_de_la_langue_fran%C3%A7aise_informatis%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first upload of the TLFi took place in the early 90s at the National Institute of the French Language (INaLF) with the help of Alain Rey and <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Cerquiglini" title="Bernard Cerquiglini">Bernard Cerquiglini</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The online version of the dictionary is presented without any modifications or updates. In 2001, the INaLF and <a href="/wiki/Nancy_2_University" title="Nancy 2 University">Nancy 2 University</a> collaborated to create the ATILF, a research lab associated with the <a href="/wiki/French_National_Center_for_Scientific_Research" class="mw-redirect" title="French National Center for Scientific Research">French National Center for Scientific Research</a> and the University of Lorraine.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The dictionary became available on CD-ROM on November 5, 2004, for Windows and Mac OS X.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tr%C3%A9sor_de_la_langue_fran%C3%A7aise_informatis%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The dictionary stems from the <a href="/wiki/Semantics" title="Semantics">semantic</a> analysis of the <i>TFL</i> and its analysis in several domains: definitions, usage examples, and semantic and lexical information.
</p><p>Taking advantage of the rich <a href="/wiki/Database" title="Database">database</a> extracted from its analysis, the dictionary offers not only the capability to search by entry but also the possibility to do more complex searches. The <a href="/wiki/Hypertext" title="Hypertext">hypertext</a> interface allows for referencing across different versions of the dictionary.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Contents">Contents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tr%C3%A9sor_de_la_langue_fran%C3%A7aise_informatis%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Contents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The TFLi contains definitions, examples from literary excerpts, technical field usages guides, information on semantics, history, etymology, grammar, usages, and synonyms and antonyms as well as hierarchical analyses linking these individual pieces of information.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The dictionary is composed of many different elementary components (definitions, examples, technical usage guides, etc) that allow complex searches at multiple levels:
</p>
<ol><li>"Article by article" visualization, allowing for the search of particular information (definitions, phrases)</li>
<li>Assisted search allowing more specific searches (by etymology, usage domain, region, etc)</li>
<li>Complex searches, such as searching within a subdomain with only two characters</li></ol>
<p>The content of the TFLi contains the content of the first edition of the TFL, and the ATILF indicates on its website that it has no plans to be updated. The TFLi thus contains errors that were in the original TFL and errors that occurred during its digitalization.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The TFLi contains:<sup id="cite_ref-:2_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>100,000 words and their history</li>
<li>270,000 definitions</li>
<li>430,000 examples</li>
<li>350,000,000 characters</li></ul>
