<p><b>Mirrors and forks of Wikipedia</b> are publications that <a href="/wiki/Mirror_website" class="mw-redirect" title="Mirror website">mirror</a> (copy exactly) or <a href="/wiki/Fork_(software_development)" title="Fork (software development)">fork</a> (copy, but change parts of the material of) <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia">Wikipedia</a>. Many correctly follow the licensing terms; however, many others fail – accidentally or intentionally – to place the notice required by these terms. Such pages are listed in subpages grouped alphabetically – see section <a href="#How_to_list_new_mirrors">§&#160;How to list new mirrors</a> below. If you find such links, please add them here.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Things_you_need_to_know">Things you need to know</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Mirrors_and_forks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Things you need to know">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl><dt>Using these mirrors and forks on Wikipedia</dt></dl>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BACKWARDSCOPY" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BACKWARDSCOPY">WP:BACKWARDSCOPY</a></div>
<dl><dd>Copies of Wikipedia are <b>not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RS">reliable sources</a></b> and <b>not acceptable <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:EL" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:EL">external links</a></b> in articles per the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CIRCULAR" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CIRCULAR">verifiability policy</a>. Articles that use a republished work as a source should be edited to either remove the work or to tag the source with &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:Circular-ref" class="mw-redirect" title="Template:Circular-ref">Circular-ref</a>&#125;&#125;.  Leave &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:Backwardscopy" class="mw-redirect" title="Template:Backwardscopy">backwardscopy</a>&#125;&#125; on the article's talk page to identify Wikipedia as the original source.</dd>
<dt>Copyright status of mirrors and forks</dt>
<dd>Every contribution to the English Wikipedia has been licensed for re-use, including commercial, for-profit websites.  Republication is not necessarily a breach of copyright, so long as the appropriate licenses are complied with.</dd>
<dt>Effect of non-compliance with licenses</dt>
<dd>If the license is not complied with, then the republication is a <a href="/wiki/Copyright_violation" class="mw-redirect" title="Copyright violation">copyright violation</a>.  <b>You</b> own the copyright to your contributions, not the Wikimedia Foundation.  Legally, the Wikimedia Foundation is in the same position as the republishers (except that the WMF always complies with your license terms), because the WMF is republishing your copyrighted content under your license.  If someone violates the terms of the license, then enforcement needs to come from the copyright owner.  Consequently, complaints about violations need to be made by a person who actually wrote part of the improperly republished material.  See <a href="#Non-compliance_process">#Non-compliance process</a> for one typical method for dealing with publishers who violate <i>your</i> copyright.  If your own copyright has not been violated, then you may contact one or more of the editors who own the copyright for the material in question, and suggest that they follow the steps in the suggested process.  The Wikimedia Foundation and the community cannot do this on behalf of the copyright owner.</dd></dl>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="License">License</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Mirrors_and_forks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: License">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copyrights" title="Wikipedia:Copyrights">Wikipedia's main license</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Text_of_Creative_Commons_Attribution-ShareAlike_3.0_Unported_License" title="Wikipedia:Text of Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0 Unported License">Creative Commons Attribution/Share-Alike License</a> (CC-BY-SA), requires that any derivative of works from Wikipedia must be released under that same license, must state that it is released under that license, and must acknowledge the contributors (which can be accomplished with a link back to that article on Wikipedia).
</p><p>As of 2009, most Wikipedia text is also dual-licensed under the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Text_of_GNU_Free_Documentation_License" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Text of GNU Free Documentation License">GNU Free Documentation License</a>.  However, this will gradually change as CC-BY-SA text is imported.  Thus, it is not safe for most reusers to use the GFDL.  Pages identified to use imported CC-BY-SA content are included at <a href="/wiki/Category:Articles_with_imported_Creative_Commons_Attribution-ShareAlike_3.0_text" title="Category:Articles with imported Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0 text">Category:Articles with imported Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0 text</a>.  The GFDL can still be used indefinitely for pages without CC-BY-SA only content.  Generally, the GFDL imposes requirements that are similar to the CC-BY-SA but more stringent.
</p><p>For details about Wikipedia's interpretation of the CC-BY-SA and GFDL, see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copyrights" title="Wikipedia:Copyrights">Wikipedia:Copyrights</a>.  However, always remember that only the CC-BY-SA and GFDL themselves are legally binding.
</p><p>Note that all notices and/or links must be visible to all users who can see the content. Thus, CSS and <a href="/wiki/JavaScript" title="JavaScript">JavaScript</a>-only links and/or notices are not acceptable if the Wikipedia article is plain HTML.
</p><p>The license does not apply to material in the <a href="/wiki/Public_domain" title="Public domain">public domain</a> or that is used under <a href="/wiki/Fair_use" title="Fair use">fair use</a>. Also, material can be used under other terms if and only if all contributors have approved them.
</p><p>The web sites listed on the "compliance" pages below use content original to Wikipedia as a source for at least some of their content.  Wikipedia itself is not included.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-compliance_process">Non-compliance process</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Mirrors_and_forks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Non-compliance process">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl><dd>This section describes the steps that might be taken on discovering a new site that uses Wikipedia content without properly complying with the license.</dd>
<dd>Note that Wikipedia does not give legal advice. Contributors retain their own copyright for submitted work.</dd></dl>
<p><i><b>If you do contact a website about infringement relating to work originally submitted to Wikipedia, please note it on the relevant subpage listed above. Doing this will help coordinate activities in helping other websites become compliant with our licence, without webmasters feeling harassed by lots of angry non-compliance notices.</b></i>
</p><p>You may want to consider using a <a href="/wiki/Disposable_e-mail_address" class="mw-redirect" title="Disposable e-mail address">disposable e-mail address</a> for this: since many of the websites listed here are built for advertising purposes, spamming is a possibility. Also, if the owner is planning to shut down the webpage, or remove the Wikipedia content as a whole, suggest to them that they use <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.google.com/intl/en/webmasters/remove.html#exclude_website">robots.txt</a> or <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.google.com/intl/en/webmasters/remove.html#exclude_website">meta tags</a> so we can remove and prevent future search engine indexing and caching for those websites. Also, if the owner is reachable, suggest that they update their Wiki with the latest <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Database_dumps" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Database dumps">database dumps</a> to keep up with recent changes.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Steps">Steps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Mirrors_and_forks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Steps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This is not an official guideline but a tool you can use for dealing with infringement. Continue the series below as long as the site is non-compliant.  Note that you <i>must</i> choose only pages for which <i>you</i> hold (partial) copyright. These steps only work for dealing with infringement on websites in the United States.
</p>
<ol><li>If the text is licensed under CC-BY-SA only, send a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Standard_CC-BY-SA_violation_letter" title="Wikipedia:Standard CC-BY-SA violation letter">standard CC-BY-SA violation letter</a> to the site owner.  If it is dually licensed, send a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Standard_license_violation_letter" title="Wikipedia:Standard license violation letter">standard license violation letter</a>. You can use a <a href="/wiki/Whois" class="mw-redirect" title="Whois">whois</a> lookup to get contact info if it is not otherwise available.</li>
<li>One week (or more) later, send a follow-up reminder.</li>
<li>Three weeks (or more) later, send a final warning, noting that continued infringement will result in a DMCA takedown notice being sent to their ISP.</li>
<li>Two weeks (or more) later, send a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Standard_GFDL_violation_letter#DMCA_takedown_notice" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Standard GFDL violation letter">DMCA takedown notice</a> to the ISP, enumerating articles that infringe <i><b>your</b></i> copyright.  Note separately that the site also violates the copyrights of others.  To find the appropriate address, first search the ISP's website.  To find the ISP, you can: enter the domain name in the DNS search at <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://dnsstuff.com">http://dnsstuff.com</a>, then click the IP.  First search the ISP's site for a legal address.  If that doesn't work, try to look them up at <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://www.copyright.gov/onlinesp/list/">http://www.copyright.gov/onlinesp/list/</a> .  If they're not in the directory, send the notice to the abuse address.  Note that sites are not legally required to accept DMCA notices.  If they don't the only recourse is legal action.</li></ol>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Remote_loading">Remote loading</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Mirrors_and_forks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Remote loading">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some mirrors load a page from the Wikimedia servers directly every time someone requests a page from them. They alter the text in some way, such as framing it with ads, then send it on to the reader. This is called remote loading, and it is an unacceptable use of Wikimedia server resources. Even remote loading websites with little legitimate traffic can generate significant load on our servers, due to search engine <a href="/wiki/Web_crawler" title="Web crawler">web crawlers</a>. 
</p><p>If you suspect a website is remote loading Wikipedia content, you can report it at <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Live_mirrors" class="extiw" title="meta:Live mirrors">meta:Live mirrors</a>.
</p><p>The appropriate way to run a mirror is to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Database_download" title="Wikipedia:Database download">download</a> a <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Data_dumps" class="extiw" title="m:Data dumps">dump</a> of the compressed 'pages-article' file and the images from <a class="external free" href="https://download.wikimedia.org/">http://download.wikimedia.org/</a>, and then use a modified instance of <a href="/wiki/MediaWiki" title="MediaWiki">MediaWiki</a> to generate the required HTML, along with above mentioned copyrights information. Please use <b>Articles, templates, image descriptions, and primary meta-pages</b> (<code>pages-articles.xml.bz2</code>) for mirroring purposes.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Copies_of_this_list">Copies of <i>this</i> list</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Mirrors_and_forks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Copies of this list">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A separate list of sites that utilise Wikipedia content is maintained at the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:OpenFacts" title="Wikipedia:OpenFacts">OpenFacts</a> site: <span class="plainlinks"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20081015170146/http://openfacts.berlios.de/index-en.phtml?title=Copies_of_Wikipedia_content">Copies of Wikipedia content</a></span>.
