<p>This article includes a sortable table listing the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">50 states</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Territories_of_the_United_States" title="Territories of the United States">territories</a>, and the District of Columbia by <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a>, population rank, and land area. It also includes a sortable table of density by states, territories, divisions and regions by population rank and land area, and a sortable table for density by states, divisions, regions and territories in square miles and square kilometers.
</p><p>Population density is as population per (divided by) land area. Resident population is from the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a> estimates for July 1, 2015, (for the 50 states, DC and Puerto Rico,) and from the 2015 United Nations Department of Economic and Social Affairs for territories besides Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-PopEstUS_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PopEstUS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-UN2015_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN2015-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In the second table, territorial data (except Puerto Rico's) are from the 2010 Census. Land area is from the 2010 Census.<sup id="cite_ref-LandAreaUS_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LandAreaUS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The population density of the United States is lower than that of many other countries because the United States' area is so great. For example, the population density of the U.S. is a twelfth that of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> and a fifteenth that of <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. However, it is over 8 times higher than that of <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and over 9 times higher than that of <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
