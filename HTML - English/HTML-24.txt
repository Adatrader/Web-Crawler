<p>This page is intended as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Glossary#Boilerplate_text" title="Wikipedia:Glossary">boilerplate text</a> for requesting permission to re-use somebody else's content in Wikipedia.  To do this, they must re-license it under the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Text_of_Creative_Commons_Attribution-ShareAlike_3.0_Unported_License" title="Wikipedia:Text of Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0 Unported License">Creative Commons Attribution-Sharealike 3.0 Unported License</a> (CC-BY-SA) or a CC-BY-SA-compatible license.  For more, read <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copyrights" title="Wikipedia:Copyrights">Wikipedia:copyrights</a>.  (Some other licenses are permitted for images; see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Image_copyright_tags" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Image copyright tags">Wikipedia:Image copyright tags</a> for more.) Material may also be co-licensed under <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Text_of_the_GNU_Free_Documentation_License" title="Wikipedia:Text of the GNU Free Documentation License">GNU Free Documentation License</a> (GFDL) (unversioned, with no invariant sections, front-cover texts, or back-cover texts), but this is not required for material imported from websites authored by others. It cannot be licensed under GFDL alone.
</p><p>If you have a letter that has worked in requesting permission, please add it to this page, or work the text that you think was effective into the existing letters.  Also, you should send a copy of your request and the response to <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="mailto:permissions-commons@wikimedia.org">permissions-commons<span class="nowrap"><img alt="@" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/At_sign.svg/15px-At_sign.svg.png" decoding="async" title="@" width="15" height="15" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/At_sign.svg/23px-At_sign.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/At_sign.svg/30px-At_sign.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="145" data-file-height="145" /></span>wikimedia.org</a> where it will be permanently archived.
</p><p>Due to the large number of ambiguous answers to inquiries concerning a permission of reuse for an image, text or similar (such as "I allow Wikipedia to reuse my photos") it is advisable to attach to your inquiry email a standard declaration of consent. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Requesting_copyright_permission" title="Wikipedia:Requesting copyright permission">Wikipedia requesting copyright permission</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Declaration_of_consent_for_all_enquiries" title="Wikipedia:Declaration of consent for all enquiries">Wikipedia declaration of consent</a> or <a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Commons:Email_templates" class="extiw" title="commons:Commons:Email templates">Commons email templates</a>.
</p>

<h2><span id="Concise_.28useful_for_character-limited_web_contact_forms.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Concise_(useful_for_character-limited_web_contact_forms)">Concise (useful for character-limited web contact forms)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Example_requests_for_permission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Concise (useful for character-limited web contact forms)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>To whom it concerns:
</p><p>I am a volunteer editor of Wikipedia. I would like to include a photo of [] in the Wikipedia article at []. However, Wikipedia can only include such a photo if it is released under a Creative Commons "Attribution-ShareAlike" license or one of the similar free licenses listed at <a class="external free" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:File_copyright_tags/Free_licenses">https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:File_copyright_tags/Free_licenses</a>.
</p><p>If you are able and would agree to provide a freely licensed photograph (it need not be newly created) for use on Wikimedia projects, please follow the instructions at <a class="external free" href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Commons:Email_templates">https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Commons:Email_templates</a>, or alternately, email the photo and consent text from that page to permissions-commons@wikimedia.org, or to myself at [].
</p><p>I personally thank you for helping further Wikipedia's goal of public education and outreach; Wikipedia faces a limited availability of freely licensed media, and your time and generosity are tremendously appreciated.
</p><p>[Signature]
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Example_1">Example 1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Example_requests_for_permission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Example 1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dear Jenny Darroch

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formal_request_for_high-quality_publicity_image">Formal request for high-quality publicity image</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Example_requests_for_permission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Formal request for high-quality publicity image">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Note: this is intended for use where a free image is available, and thus used, that may be unflattering to the subject.</i>
</p><p>Dear <b>&lt;Mr./Ms./etc.&gt;</b> <b>&lt;last name&gt;</b>:
</p><p>I am one of the many volunteer editors of the English Wikipedia (en.wikipedia.org), the free encyclopedia.  Wikipedia is among the most-visited sites on the Internet, ranking near the top ten according to the estimates of Alexa Internet (alexa.com), and it is likely that many people visit our article about you (<b>&lt;insert link to article here&gt;</b>) each , our article currently makes use of an image of you that might be considered unflattering due to its comparatively low quality.
</p><p>I am aware that there are many publicity images of you available, but since Wikipedia aims to be reproducible even for profit and even in nations where generous United States "fair use" provisions in copyright law are inapplicable, we cannot use an image that is not released under a so-called "free license".  Essentially, the copyright holder of any image that we use must irrevocably permit anyone else to use it, modify it, or sell it, with the only permissible requirements being that the author be named and that any modifications be released under an identical license.  (Exceptions may be made if there is no image available that meets these criteria, but that is not the case here.)
</p><p>Example licenses that would permit us to use a better-quality image would be: the GNU Free Documentation License (<a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://www.gnu.org/copyleft/fdl.html">http://www.gnu.org/copyleft/fdl.html</a>) or the Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0 license (<a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/3.0/legalcode">https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/3.0/legalcode</a>).  You are under no obligation to release any material under such licenses, but I thought that for public-relations purposes, you might want to consider it given Wikipedia's great popularity.
</p><p>Thank you for your time.
</p><p>Sincerely,
<b>&lt;name&gt;</b>

<p><span style="color: #251F86"><i>Everybody is in a hurry now. The approach I've developed starts with a Flickr message of only about 150 words.</i></span>

</p><p>Here's how to change your copyright licence. It only involves choosing which licence you prefer, then invoking your decision with two clicks.
</p><p><br />
CHOOSING YOUR LICENCE
</p><p>At present you have "All rights reserved" shown for the photo. Wikimedia Commons accepts images under either of two licences or a Public Domain Declaration. Under all three options the image would be attributed to you, and you would retain ownership of copyright.
</p><p>The option of a Public Domain (CC0) Declaration is discussed at <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://creativecommons.org/share-your-work/public-domain/cc0/">https://creativecommons.org/share-your-work/public-domain/cc0/</a> – but I'm assuming you won't choose this. Please let me know if that's not so.
</p><p>The licences accepted by Wikimedia Commons, developed by the not-for-profit Creative Commons organisation (<a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://creativecommons.org">https://creativecommons.org</a>), are:
</p>
<ul><li>Attribution ("CC BY"): this licence lets others distribute, remix, tweak, and build on your work, even commercially, as long as they credit you for the original creation. Wikipedia says most people choose this. More info: <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/2.0/">https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/2.0/</a></li></ul>
<ul><li>Attribution–Share Alike ("CC BY-SA"): this contains the same provisions but also requires anyone who modifies your work to licence their new creation under the identical terms. More info: <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/2.0/">https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/2.0/</a></li></ul>
<p><br />
STATING THE LICENCE
</p><p>1. In Flickr, go to your image's page. You'll see the "© All rights reserved" link just below the bottom right of your photo. 
</p><p>2. Click on the link. A pop-down menu will show all the Flickr licensing options. 
</p><p>3. Choose "Attribution" (or " Attribution-ShareAlike" if you prefer that). That's all you need to do.
</p><p>Anyone who clicks the link that previously read "© All rights reserved " – now showing "Some rights reserved" – will be taken to the Creative Commons page detailing the licence you have chosen.  
</p><p>Please let me know when you have changed the licence so I can upload the image to Wikimedia Commons.
</p><p><br />
FILE NAME ON WIKIMEDIA COMMONS 
</p><p>You'll be attributed as the copyright owner on the image's page in Wikimedia Commons. As a personal preference, I would also like to include your name in the file title. Along with a more comprehensive description (which is Wikimedia Commons's preference), the file title I have in mind would be: 
</p><p>"[My longer title], [year photo taken] ([photographer's first name + surname]).jpg". 
</p><p>Are you happy to have your name included in this way? 
</p><p>I really appreciate your willingness to license the photo. 
</p><p>Best wishes,
</p><p>[my Flickr name]
</p>
<hr />
<p><span style="color: #251F86"><i>If they don't respond, I send a polite hastener 3 or 4 weeks later. Once I've sent the second message, if they haven't let me know they have changed the licence within, say, a week, I check the Flickr page to see whether it has been changed. If so, I send a brief thank-you message. If the image file size was on the small side, only then do I ask whether they have a bigger one.</i></span>
</p><p><span style="color: #251F86"><i>The final step I take is to send the photographer a message with a link to the Wikimedia Commons page and, if I have already inserted it into an article, the Wikipedia page. I don't tell them this beforehand – just in case I forget. It's another opportunity to show appreciation.</i></span>
</p><p><span style="color: #251F86"><i>Of those who get back (about 95%), so far all (more than 30) have agreed to license their image.</i></span>
</p>
