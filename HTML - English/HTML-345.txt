	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Carl Neumann Degler</h1>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Carl Neumann Degler</b> (February 6, 1921 in <a href="/wiki/Newark,_New_Jersey" title="Newark, New Jersey">Newark, New Jersey</a> – December 27, 2014 in <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California" title="Palo Alto, California">Palo Alto, California</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> <a href="/wiki/Historian" title="Historian">historian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pulitzer_Prize" title="Pulitzer Prize">Pulitzer Prize</a>-winning author. He was the Margaret Byrne Professor of American History <a href="/wiki/Emeritus" title="Emeritus">Emeritus</a> at <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carl_Neumann_Degler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Degler was born New Jersey. He served in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Army_Air_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Army Air Force">U.S. Army Air Force</a> from 1942 to 1945. He earned a BA in history from <a href="/wiki/Upsala_College" title="Upsala College">Upsala College</a>, and master's and doctoral degrees from <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-stanford_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stanford-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carl_Neumann_Degler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Degler taught history at <a href="/wiki/Vassar_College" title="Vassar College">Vassar College</a> for 16 years (1952–1968). In 1968 he joined the Stanford faculty and taught there for the rest of his career, retiring as Emeritus Professor in 1990. In 1986 Degler was elected President of the <a href="/wiki/American_Historical_Association" title="American Historical Association">American Historical Association</a>. He also served as president of the Organization of American Historians and the Southern Historical Association.<sup id="cite_ref-stanford_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stanford-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1972 Degler was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Pulitzer_Prize_for_History" title="Pulitzer Prize for History">Pulitzer Prize for History</a> for his book <i><a href="/wiki/Neither_Black_nor_White" title="Neither Black nor White">Neither Black nor White</a></i> (1971), a work comparing <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a> and race relations in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. He earlier wrote <i>Out of Our Past</i> (1959), a study of United States history that is currently used in high school and college classrooms and study chambers throughout the United States.
</p><p>He has been described as "a scholarly champion of the common man and woman in American history" and as "a founding feminist".  He was one of only two male founding members of the <a href="/wiki/National_Organization_for_Women" title="National Organization for Women">National Organization for Women</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-stanford_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stanford-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1973–1974 he was the <a href="/wiki/Harold_Vyvyan_Harmsworth_Professor_of_American_History" title="Harold Vyvyan Harmsworth Professor of American History">Harold Vyvyan Harmsworth Professor of American History</a> at <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Oxford University">Oxford University</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carl_Neumann_Degler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Degler was married to Catherine Grady, whom he met at Columbia, for nearly 50 years until her death. He was married to Teresa Baker Degler for the last 14 years of his life. He had two children and four grandchildren.
</p><p>He died in <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California" title="Palo Alto, California">Palo Alto, California</a> on December 27, 2014 at the age of 93.<sup id="cite_ref-stanford_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stanford-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bibliography">Bibliography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carl_Neumann_Degler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bibliography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Degler's works include:<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i>Out of Our Past: The Forces That Shaped Modern America</i> (1959)</li>
<li><i>Neither Black Nor White: Slavery and Race Relations in Brazil and the United States</i> (1972)</li>
<li><i>The Other South - Southern Dissenters in the Nineteenth Century</i> (1974)</li>
<li><i>Place Over Time: The Continuity of Southern Distinctiveness,</i> (1977)</li>
<li><i>At Odds&#160;: Women and the Family in America from the Revolution to the Present</i> (1981)</li>
<li><i>In Search of Human Nature: The Decline and Revival of Darwinism in American Social Thought</i> (1991)</li>
<li><i>The Third American Revolution</i> (1959)</li></ul>
