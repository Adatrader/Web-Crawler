	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Hendrick Avercamp</h1>

<p><b>Hendrick Avercamp</b> (January 27, 1585 (bapt.) &#8211; May 15, 1634 (buried)) was a <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> painter. Avercamp was born in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, where he studied with the Danish-born portrait painter <a href="/wiki/Pieter_Isaacks" class="mw-redirect" title="Pieter Isaacks">Pieter Isaacks</a> (1569–1625), and perhaps also with <a href="/wiki/David_Vinckboons" title="David Vinckboons">David Vinckboons</a>. In 1608 he moved from Amsterdam to <a href="/wiki/Kampen,_Overijssel" title="Kampen, Overijssel">Kampen</a> in the province of <a href="/wiki/Overijssel" title="Overijssel">Overijssel</a>. Avercamp was deaf and <a href="/wiki/Muteness" title="Muteness">mute</a> and was known as "de Stomme van Kampen" (the mute of Kampen).
</p><p>As one of the first landscape painters of the 17th-century Dutch school, he specialized in painting the Netherlands in winter. Avercamp's paintings are colorful and lively, with carefully crafted images of the people in the landscape.  Many of Avercamp's paintings feature people ice skating on frozen lakes.
</p><p>Avercamp's work enjoyed great popularity and he sold his drawings, many of which were tinted with water-color, as finished pictures to be pasted into the albums of collectors. The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Collection" title="Royal Collection">Royal Collection</a> has an outstanding collection of his works.
</p><p>Avercamp died in Kampen and was interred there in the <a href="/wiki/Bovenkerk,_Kampen" title="Bovenkerk, Kampen">Sint Nicolaaskerk</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artwork">Artwork</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hendrick_Avercamp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Artwork">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Avercamp probably painted in his studio on the basis of sketches he had made in the winter.
Avercamp was famous even abroad for his winter landscapes. The passion for painting skating characters probably came from his childhood as he practiced skating with his parents. The last quarter of the 16th century, during which Avercamp was born, was one of the coldest periods of the <a href="/wiki/Little_Ice_Age" title="Little Ice Age">Little Ice Age</a>.
</p><p>
The Flemish painting tradition is mainly expressed in Avercamp's early work. This is consistent with the landscapes of <a href="/wiki/Pieter_Bruegel_the_Elder" title="Pieter Bruegel the Elder">Pieter Bruegel the Elder</a>. Avercamp painted landscapes with a high horizon and many figures who are working on something. The paintings are narrative, with many anecdotes. For instance, included in the painting "Winter landscape with skaters" are several prurient details: a couple making love, naked buttocks, and a peeing male. </p><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hendrick_Avercamp_-_Winterlandschap_met_ijsvermaak.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Hendrick_Avercamp_-_Winterlandschap_met_ijsvermaak.jpg/220px-Hendrick_Avercamp_-_Winterlandschap_met_ijsvermaak.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="127" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Hendrick_Avercamp_-_Winterlandschap_met_ijsvermaak.jpg/330px-Hendrick_Avercamp_-_Winterlandschap_met_ijsvermaak.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Hendrick_Avercamp_-_Winterlandschap_met_ijsvermaak.jpg/440px-Hendrick_Avercamp_-_Winterlandschap_met_ijsvermaak.jpg 2x" data-file-width="6278" data-file-height="3626" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hendrick_Avercamp_-_Winterlandschap_met_ijsvermaak.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Winter_Landscape_with_Skaters" title="Winter Landscape with Skaters">Winter Landscape with Skaters</a></div></div></div>
<p>Later in his life drawing the atmosphere was also important in his work. The horizon also gradually dropped down under more and more air.
</p><p>Avercamp used the painting technique of <a href="/wiki/Aerial_perspective" title="Aerial perspective">aerial perspective</a>. The depth is suggested by change of color in the distance. To the front objects are painted in richer colors, such as trees or a boat, while farther objects are lighter. This technique strengthens the impression of depth in the painting.
</p><p>Avercamp has also painted cattle and seascapes.
</p><p>Sometimes Avercamp used paper frames, which were a cheap alternative to oil paintings. He first drew with pen and ink. This work was then covered with finishing paint. The contours of the drawing remained. Even with this technique, Avercamp could show the pale wintry colors and nuances of the ice .
</p><p>Avercamp produced about a hundred paintings. The bulk of his artwork can be seen in the <a href="/wiki/Rijksmuseum" title="Rijksmuseum">Rijksmuseum</a> in Amsterdam and the <a href="/wiki/Mauritshuis" title="Mauritshuis">Mauritshuis</a> in The Hague. 
From November 20, 2009 to February 15, 2010 the Rijksmuseum presented an exhibition of his work entitled "Little Ice Age".
</p>
