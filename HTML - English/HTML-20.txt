	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Kardashev scale</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">A method of measuring a civilization's level of technological advancement, based on the amount of energy a civilization is able to use</div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:662px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Consommations_%C3%A9nerg%C3%A9tiques_des_trois_types_de_l%27%C3%A9chelle_de_Kardashev.svg" class="image"><img alt="Three schematic representations: Earth, Solar System and Milky Way" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Consommations_%C3%A9nerg%C3%A9tiques_des_trois_types_de_l%27%C3%A9chelle_de_Kardashev.svg/660px-Consommations_%C3%A9nerg%C3%A9tiques_des_trois_types_de_l%27%C3%A9chelle_de_Kardashev.svg.png" decoding="async" width="660" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Consommations_%C3%A9nerg%C3%A9tiques_des_trois_types_de_l%27%C3%A9chelle_de_Kardashev.svg/990px-Consommations_%C3%A9nerg%C3%A9tiques_des_trois_types_de_l%27%C3%A9chelle_de_Kardashev.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Consommations_%C3%A9nerg%C3%A9tiques_des_trois_types_de_l%27%C3%A9chelle_de_Kardashev.svg/1320px-Consommations_%C3%A9nerg%C3%A9tiques_des_trois_types_de_l%27%C3%A9chelle_de_Kardashev.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="3571" data-file-height="1378" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Consommations_%C3%A9nerg%C3%A9tiques_des_trois_types_de_l%27%C3%A9chelle_de_Kardashev.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Energy consumption estimated in three types of civilizations defined by Kardashev scale</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>Kardashev scale</b> is a method of measuring a <a href="/wiki/Civilization" title="Civilization">civilization</a>'s level of <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technological</a> advancement based on the amount of <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a> they are able to use. The measure was proposed by <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> <a href="/wiki/Astronomer" title="Astronomer">astronomer</a> <a href="/wiki/Nikolai_Kardashev" title="Nikolai Kardashev">Nikolai Kardashev</a> in 1964.<sup id="cite_ref-Kard_TransInfoCiv_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kard_TransInfoCiv-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The scale has three designated categories:
</p>
<ul><li>A <span id="TYPE_1_CIVILIZATION"></span><span id="Type&#160;I_civilization">Type&#160;I civilization</span>, also called a <a href="/wiki/Planetary_civilization" title="Planetary civilization">planetary civilization</a>—can use and store all of the energy available on its <a href="/wiki/Planet" title="Planet">planet</a>.</li>
<li>A <span id="TYPE_2_CIVILIZATION"></span><span id="Type&#160;II_civilization">Type&#160;II civilization</span>, also called a <a href="/wiki/Stellar_civilization" class="mw-redirect" title="Stellar civilization">stellar civilization</a>—can use and control energy at the scale of its <a href="/wiki/Stellar_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Stellar system">stellar system</a>.</li>
<li>A <span id="TYPE_3_CIVILIZATION"></span><span id="Type&#160;III_civilization">Type&#160;III civilization</span>, also called a <a href="/wiki/Galactic_civilization" class="mw-redirect" title="Galactic civilization">galactic civilization</a>—can control energy at the scale of its entire host <a href="/wiki/Galaxy" title="Galaxy">galaxy</a>.</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definition">Definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kardashev_scale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Definition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1964, <a href="/wiki/Nikolai_Kardashev" title="Nikolai Kardashev">Kardashev</a> defined three levels of <a href="/wiki/Civilization" title="Civilization">civilizations</a>, based on the order of magnitude of <a href="/wiki/Power_(physics)" title="Power (physics)">power</a> available to them:
</p>
<dl><dt>Type I</dt>
<dd>Technological level of a civilization that is "close to the level presently attained on Earth, with energy consumption at ≈4<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup><span style="white-space:nowrap">19</span></sup> erg/sec" (4<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup><span style="white-space:nowrap">12</span></sup> watts).<sup id="cite_ref-Kard_TransInfoCiv_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kard_TransInfoCiv-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Currently, the civilization of Type I is usually defined as one that can harness all the energy that falls on a planet from its parent star (for Earth–Sun system, this value is close to 1.74<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup><span style="white-space:nowrap">17</span></sup> watts), which is about four <a href="/wiki/Order_of_magnitude" title="Order of magnitude">orders of magnitude</a> higher than the amount presently attained on Earth, with energy consumption at ≈2<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup><span style="white-space:nowrap">13</span></sup> watts. The astronomer Guillermo A. Lemarchand stated this as a level near contemporary terrestrial civilization with an energy capability equivalent to the solar insolation on Earth, between 10<sup>16</sup> and 10<sup>17</sup> watts.<sup id="cite_ref-Lemarchand_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lemarchand-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></dd>
<dt>Type II</dt>
<dd>A civilization capable of harnessing the energy radiated by its own star—for example, the stage of successful construction of a <a href="/wiki/Dyson_sphere" title="Dyson sphere">Dyson sphere</a>—with energy consumption at ≈4<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup><span style="white-space:nowrap">33</span></sup> erg/sec.<sup id="cite_ref-Kard_TransInfoCiv_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kard_TransInfoCiv-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  Lemarchand stated this as a civilization capable of using and channeling the entire radiation output of its star.  The energy use would then be comparable to the luminosity of the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a>, about 4<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup><span style="white-space:nowrap">33</span></sup> erg/sec (4×10<sup>26</sup> <a href="/wiki/Watt" title="Watt">watts</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-Lemarchand_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lemarchand-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></dd>
<dt>Type III</dt>
<dd>A civilization in possession of energy at the scale of its own <a href="/wiki/Galaxy" title="Galaxy">galaxy</a>, with energy consumption at ≈4<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup><span style="white-space:nowrap">44</span></sup> erg/sec.<sup id="cite_ref-Kard_TransInfoCiv_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kard_TransInfoCiv-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  Lemarchand stated this as a civilization with access to the power comparable to the luminosity of the entire <a href="/wiki/Milky_Way" title="Milky Way">Milky Way</a> galaxy, about 4<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup><span style="white-space:nowrap">44</span></sup> erg/sec (4×10<sup>37</sup> watts).<sup id="cite_ref-Lemarchand_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lemarchand-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></dd></dl>
<p>Kardashev believed that a Type 4 civilization was impossible, so he did not go past Type 3. However, new types (0, IV, V, VI) have been proposed.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_status_of_human_civilization">Current status of human civilization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kardashev_scale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Current status of human civilization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Annual_world_primary_energy_consumption.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Annual_world_primary_energy_consumption.svg/250px-Annual_world_primary_energy_consumption.svg.png" decoding="async" width="250" height="181" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Annual_world_primary_energy_consumption.svg/375px-Annual_world_primary_energy_consumption.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Annual_world_primary_energy_consumption.svg/500px-Annual_world_primary_energy_consumption.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="911" data-file-height="661" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Annual_world_primary_energy_consumption.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Total World, Annual Primary Energy Consumption.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Carl_Sagan_Planetary_Society.JPG" class="image"><img alt="Color photo. <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/World_energy_consumption" title="World energy consumption">World energy consumption</a></div>
<p>At the current time, humanity has not yet reached Type 1 civilization status.
Physicist and futurist <a href="/wiki/Michio_Kaku" title="Michio Kaku">Michio Kaku</a> suggested that, if humans increase their energy consumption at an average rate of 3 percent each year, they may attain <a href="/wiki/Planetary_civilization" title="Planetary civilization">Type&#160;I</a> status in 100–200 years, Type&#160;II status in a few thousand years, and Type&#160;III status in 100,000 to a million years.<sup id="cite_ref-Interstellar_Travel_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Interstellar_Travel-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Carl_Sagan" title="Carl Sagan">Carl Sagan</a> suggested defining intermediate values (not considered in Kardashev's original scale) by <a href="/wiki/Interpolation" title="Interpolation">interpolating</a> and <a href="/wiki/Extrapolation" title="Extrapolation">extrapolating</a> the values given above for types I (10<sup>16</sup> W), II (10<sup>26</sup> W) and III (10<sup>36</sup> W), which would produce the formula
</p>

<p>where value <i>K</i> is a civilization's Kardashev rating and <i>P</i> is the power it uses, in watts.  Using this extrapolation, a "<b>Type 0</b>" civilization, not defined by Kardashev, would control about 1 MW of power, and humanity's civilization type as of 1973 was about 0.7 (apparently using 10 <a href="/wiki/Watt#Terawatt" title="Watt">terawatts</a> (TW) as the value for 1970s humanity).<sup id="cite_ref-books.google.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-books.google.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, the total <a href="/wiki/World_energy_consumption" title="World energy consumption">world energy consumption</a> was 13864.9 <a href="/wiki/Million_Tonnes_of_Oil_Equivalent" class="mw-redirect" title="Million Tonnes of Oil Equivalent">Mtoe</a> (161,249 <a href="/wiki/Kilowatt_hour#Watt_hour_multiples_and_billing_units" title="Kilowatt hour">TWh</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> equivalent to an average power consumption of 18.40 TW or 0.73 on Sagan's interpolated Kardashev scale.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Observational_evidence">Observational evidence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kardashev_scale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Observational evidence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2015, a study of galactic mid-<a href="/wiki/Infrared" title="Infrared">infrared</a> emissions came to the conclusion that "Kardashev Type-III civilizations are either very rare or do not exist in the <a href="/wiki/Observable_universe" title="Observable universe">local Universe</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 14, 2015, the detection of an unusual light curve for star <a href="/wiki/KIC_8462852" class="mw-redirect" title="KIC 8462852">KIC 8462852</a> raised speculation that a <a href="/wiki/Dyson_swarm" class="mw-redirect" title="Dyson swarm">Dyson swarm</a> (Type II civilization) may have been discovered.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/SETI_Institute" title="SETI Institute">SETI Institute</a>'s initial radio reconnaissance of KIC 8462852, however, found no evidence of technology-related radio signals from the star.<sup id="cite_ref-SETI-20151105_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SETI-20151105-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ARX-20151105_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ARX-20151105-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AXV-20151208_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AXV-20151208-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, Paul Glister, author of the Centauri Dreams website, described a signal apparently from the star <a href="/wiki/HD_164595" title="HD 164595">HD 164595</a> as requiring the power of a Type I or Type II civilization, if produced by extraterrestrial lifeforms.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> However, in August 2016 it was discovered that the signal's origin was most likely a military satellite orbiting the Earth.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Energy_development">Energy development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kardashev_scale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Energy development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Energy_development" title="Energy development">Energy development</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Type_I_civilization_methods">Type&#160;I civilization methods</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kardashev_scale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Type I civilization methods">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>Large-scale application of <a href="/wiki/Fusion_power" title="Fusion power">fusion power</a>. According to <a href="/wiki/Mass%E2%80%93energy_equivalence" title="Mass–energy equivalence">mass–energy equivalence</a>, Type&#160;I implies the conversion of about 2&#160;kg of matter to energy per second. An equivalent energy release could theoretically be achieved by fusing approximately 280&#160;kg of <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">hydrogen</a> into <a href="/wiki/Helium" title="Helium">helium</a> per second,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> a rate roughly equivalent to 8.9×10<sup>9</sup> kg/year. A cubic&#160;km of water contains about 10<sup>11</sup> kg of hydrogen, and the Earth's <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">oceans</a> contain about <a href="/wiki/Ocean#Physical_properties" title="Ocean">1.3×10<sup>9</sup> cubic&#160;km of water</a>, meaning that humans on Earth could sustain this rate of consumption over geological time-scales, in terms of available hydrogen.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Antimatter" title="Antimatter">Antimatter</a> in large quantities would have a mechanism to produce power on a scale several magnitudes above the current level of technology.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In antimatter-matter collisions, the entire <a href="/wiki/Rest_mass" class="mw-redirect" title="Rest mass">rest mass</a> of the particles is converted to <a href="/wiki/Radiant_energy" title="Radiant energy">radiant energy</a>. Their <a href="/wiki/Energy_density" title="Energy density">energy density</a> (energy released per mass) is about four orders of magnitude greater than that from using <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_fission" title="Nuclear fission">nuclear fission</a>, and about two orders of magnitude greater than the best possible yield from <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_fusion" title="Nuclear fusion">fusion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The reaction of 1&#160;<a href="/wiki/Kilogram" title="Kilogram">kg</a> of anti-matter with 1&#160;kg of matter would produce 1.8×10<sup>17</sup>&#160;<a href="/wiki/Joule" title="Joule">J</a> (180 petajoules) of energy.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Although antimatter is sometimes proposed as a source of energy, this does not appear feasible. Artificially producing antimatter—according to current understanding of the laws of physics—involves first converting energy into mass, which yields no net energy. Artificially created antimatter is only usable as a medium of energy storage, not as an energy source, unless future technological developments (contrary to the conservation of the <a href="/wiki/Baryon_number" title="Baryon number">baryon number</a>, such as a <a href="/wiki/CP_violation" title="CP violation">CP violation</a> in favour of antimatter) allow the conversion of ordinary matter into anti-matter.  Theoretically, humans may in the future have the capability to cultivate and harvest a number of naturally occurring sources of antimatter.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">Renewable energy</a> through converting sunlight into electricity—either by using <a href="/wiki/Solar_cell" title="Solar cell">solar cells</a> and <a href="/wiki/Concentrating_solar_power" class="mw-redirect" title="Concentrating solar power">concentrating solar power</a> or indirectly through <a href="/wiki/Biofuel" title="Biofuel">biofuel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wind_power" title="Wind power">wind</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hydroelectric_power" class="mw-redirect" title="Hydroelectric power">hydroelectric power</a>. There is no known way for <a href="/wiki/Civilization" title="Civilization">human civilization</a> to use the equivalent of the Earth's total absorbed solar energy without completely coating the surface with human-made structures, which is not feasible with current technology.  However, if a civilization constructed very large <a href="/wiki/Space-based_solar_power" title="Space-based solar power">space-based solar power</a> <a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">satellites</a>, Type&#160;I power levels might become achievable—these could convert sunlight to microwave power and beam that to collectors on Earth.</li></ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Type_II_civilization_methods">Type&#160;II civilization methods</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kardashev_scale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Type II civilization methods">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>Type II civilizations might use the same techniques employed by a Type I civilization, but applied to a large number of planets in a large number of planetary systems.</li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Dyson_sphere" title="Dyson sphere">Dyson sphere</a> or <a href="/wiki/Dyson_swarm" class="mw-redirect" title="Dyson swarm">Dyson swarm</a> and similar constructs are hypothetical <a href="/wiki/Megastructure" title="Megastructure">megastructures</a> originally described by <a href="/wiki/Freeman_Dyson" title="Freeman Dyson">Freeman Dyson</a> as a system of orbiting <a href="/wiki/Solar_power_satellite" class="mw-redirect" title="Solar power satellite">solar power satellites</a> meant to enclose a <a href="/wiki/Star" title="Star">star</a> completely and capture most or all of its energy output.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Perhaps a more exotic means to generate usable energy would be to feed a stellar mass into a <a href="/wiki/Black_hole" title="Black hole">black hole</a>, and collect photons emitted by the <a href="/wiki/Accretion_disc" class="mw-redirect" title="Accretion disc">accretion disc</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Less exotic would be simply to capture photons already escaping from the accretion disc, reducing a black hole's <a href="/wiki/Angular_momentum" title="Angular momentum">angular momentum</a>; this is known as the <a href="/wiki/Penrose_process" title="Penrose process">Penrose process</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Star_lifting" title="Star lifting">Star lifting</a> is a process where an advanced civilization could remove a substantial portion of a star's matter in a controlled manner for other uses.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Antimatter" title="Antimatter">Antimatter</a> is likely to be produced as an industrial <a href="/wiki/Byproduct" class="mw-redirect" title="Byproduct">byproduct</a> of a number of <a href="/wiki/Megascale_engineering" title="Megascale engineering">megascale engineering</a> processes (such as the aforementioned star lifting) and, therefore, could be recycled.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup></li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Star_system" title="Star system">multiple-star systems</a> of a sufficiently large number of stars, absorbing a small but significant fraction of the output of each individual star.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Type_III_civilization_methods">Type III civilization methods</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kardashev_scale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Type III civilization methods">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>Type&#160;III civilizations might use the same techniques employed by a Type&#160;II civilization, but applied to all possible stars of one or more galaxies individually.<sup id="cite_ref-kardashev1984_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kardashev1984-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>They may also be able to tap into the energy released from the <a href="/wiki/Supermassive_black_hole" title="Supermassive black hole">supermassive black holes</a> which are believed to exist at the center of most galaxies.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/White_holes" class="mw-redirect" title="White holes">White holes</a>, if they exist, theoretically could provide large amounts of energy from collecting the matter propelling outwards.</li>
<li>Capturing the energy of <a href="/wiki/Gamma-ray_burst" title="Gamma-ray burst">gamma-ray bursts</a> is another theoretically possible power source for a highly advanced civilization.</li>
<li>The emissions from <a href="/wiki/Quasars" class="mw-redirect" title="Quasars">quasars</a> can be readily compared to those of small active galaxies and could provide a massive power source if collectable.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Civilization_implications">Civilization implications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kardashev_scale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Civilization implications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are many historical examples of human civilization undergoing large-scale transitions, such as the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>. The transition between Kardashev scale levels could potentially represent similarly dramatic periods of social upheaval, since they entail surpassing the hard limits of the resources available in a civilization's existing territory. A common speculation<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> suggests that the transition from Type&#160;0 to Type&#160;I might carry a strong risk of self-destruction since, in some scenarios, there would no longer be room for further expansion on the civilization's home planet, as in a <a href="/wiki/Malthusian_catastrophe" title="Malthusian catastrophe">Malthusian catastrophe</a>. Excessive use of energy without adequate disposal of heat, for example, could plausibly make the planet of a civilization approaching Type&#160;I unsuitable to the biology of the <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">dominant life-forms</a> and their food sources. If Earth is an example, then sea temperatures in excess of 35&#160;°C (95&#160;°F) would jeopardize marine life and make the cooling of mammals to temperatures suitable for their <a href="/wiki/Metabolism" title="Metabolism">metabolism</a> difficult if not impossible. Of course, these <a href="/wiki/Theoretical" class="mw-redirect" title="Theoretical">theoretical</a> speculations may not become problems possibly through the applications of future <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a>. Also, by the time a civilization reaches Type&#160;I it may have colonized other planets or created <a href="/wiki/O%27Neill_cylinder" title="O&#39;Neill cylinder">O'Neill-type colonies</a>, so that waste heat could be distributed throughout the planetary system
</p><p>The limitation of biological life-form and the evolution of computing technology may lead to the transformation of the civilization through <a href="/wiki/Mind_uploading" title="Mind uploading">Mind uploading</a> during the transition from Type I to Type II, leading to <a href="/wiki/Matrioshka_brain" title="Matrioshka brain">digitalized civilization</a>).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Extensions_to_the_original_scale">Extensions to the original scale</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kardashev_scale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Extensions to the original scale">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many extensions and modifications to the Kardashev scale have been proposed.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Types 0, IV, and V Kardashev rating:</b> The most straightforward extension of the scale to even more hypothetical Type&#160;IV beings who can control or use the entire universe or Type&#160;V who control collections of universes. This would also include Type 0 civilizations, who do not rank on the Kardashev scale. The power output of the visible <a href="/wiki/Universe" title="Universe">universe</a> is within a few orders of magnitude of 10<sup>45</sup>&#160;W. Such a civilization approaches or surpasses the limits of speculation based on current scientific understanding, and may not be possible.
<ul><li>Zoltán Galántai has argued that such a civilization could not be detected, as its activities would be indistinguishable from the workings of nature (there being nothing to compare them to).<sup id="cite_ref-Zoltan_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zoltan-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In his book <i>Parallel Worlds</i>, <a href="/wiki/Michio_Kaku" title="Michio Kaku">Michio Kaku</a> has discussed a <b>Type&#160;IV civilization</b> that could harness "extragalactic" energy sources such as <a href="/wiki/Dark_energy" title="Dark energy">dark energy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ParallelWorlds_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ParallelWorlds-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup></li></ul></li>
<li><b>Kardashev alternative rating characteristics:</b> Other proposed changes to the scale use different metrics such as 'mastery' of systems, amount of information used, or progress in control of the very small as opposed to the very large.</li>
<li><b>Planet mastery (Robert Zubrin):</b> Metrics other than pure power usage have also been proposed. One is 'mastery' of a planet, system or galaxy rather than considering energy alone.<sup id="cite_ref-Entering_Space_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Entering_Space-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Information mastery (Carl Sagan):</b> Alternatively, <a href="/wiki/Carl_Sagan" title="Carl Sagan">Carl Sagan</a> suggested adding another dimension in addition to pure energy usage: the information available to the civilization.
<ul><li>He assigned the letter&#160;A to represent 10<sup>6</sup> unique bits of information (less than any recorded human culture) and each successive letter to represent an order of magnitude increase, so that a level&#160;Z civilization would have 10<sup>31</sup> bits.</li>
<li>In this classification, 1973 Earth is a 0.7&#160;H civilization, with access to 10<sup>13</sup> bits of information.</li>
<li>Sagan believed that no civilization has yet reached level&#160;Z, conjecturing that so much unique information would exceed that of all the intelligent species in a <a href="/wiki/Galactic_supercluster" class="mw-redirect" title="Galactic supercluster">galactic supercluster</a> and observing that the universe is not old enough to exchange information effectively over larger distances.</li>
<li>The information and energy axes are not strictly interdependent, so that even a level Z civilization would not need to be Kardashev Type&#160;III.<sup id="cite_ref-books.google.com_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-books.google.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li></ul></li>
<li><b>Microdimensional mastery (John Barrow):</b> <a href="/wiki/John_D._Barrow" title="John D. Barrow">John D. Barrow</a>, going by the fact that humans have found it more cost-effective to extend any abilities to manipulate their environment over increasingly smaller dimensions rather than increasingly larger ones, reverses the classification downward from Type&#160;I-minus to Type Omega-minus:
<ul><li><b>Type&#160;I-minus</b> is capable of manipulating objects over the scale of themselves: building structures, mining, joining and breaking solids;</li>
<li><b>Type&#160;II-minus</b> is capable of manipulating genes and altering the development of living things, transplanting or replacing parts of themselves, reading and engineering their genetic code;</li>
<li><b>Type&#160;III-minus</b> is capable of manipulating molecules and molecular bonds, creating new materials;</li>
<li><b>Type&#160;IV-minus</b> is capable of manipulating individual atoms, creating nanotechnologies on the atomic scale and creating complex forms of artificial life;</li>
<li><b>Type&#160;V-minus</b> is capable of manipulating the atomic nucleus and engineering the nucleons that compose it;</li>
<li><b>Type&#160;VI-minus</b> is capable of manipulating the most elementary particles of matter (quarks and leptons) to create organized complexity among populations of elementary particles; culminating in:</li>
<li><b>Type Omega-minus</b> is capable of manipulating the basic structure of space and time.<sup id="cite_ref-Impossibility:_Limits_of_Science_and_the_Science_of_Limits_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Impossibility:_Limits_of_Science_and_the_Science_of_Limits-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>According to this scale, humans, having wide expertise in various branches of chemistry and biology, have passed the stage of Type III-minus. Type IV-minus technologies (that have had practical and widespread applications) have been seen in areas like <a href="/wiki/Nanotechnology" title="Nanotechnology">nanotechnology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Semiconductors" class="mw-redirect" title="Semiconductors">semiconductors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Materials_science" title="Materials science">materials science</a> and <a href="/wiki/Genetic_engineering" title="Genetic engineering">genetic engineering</a>, whereas Type V-minus has seen large scale application in the field and subfields of <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_physics" title="Nuclear physics">nuclear physics</a>. Type VI-minus has had tentative research in the field of <a href="/wiki/Particle_physics" title="Particle physics">particle physics</a> with particle colliders such as the <a href="/wiki/Large_Hadron_Collider" title="Large Hadron Collider">Large Hadron Collider</a>.</li></ul></li>
<li><b>Civilizational range (Robert Zubrin):</b> <a href="/wiki/Robert_Zubrin" title="Robert Zubrin">Robert Zubrin</a> adapts the Kardashev scale to refer to how widespread a civilization is in space, rather than to its energy use.
<ul><li>In his definition, a Type&#160;I civilization has spread across its planet.</li>
<li>A Type&#160;II has extensive colonies in its respective stellar system, and</li>
<li>A Type&#160;III has colonized its galaxy.<sup id="cite_ref-Entering_Space_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Entering_Space-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup></li></ul></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kardashev_scale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It has been argued that, because we cannot understand advanced civilizations, we cannot predict their behavior.  Thus, the Kardashev scale may not be relevant or useful for classifying extraterrestrial civilizations. This central argument is found in the 2002 book <i><a href="/wiki/Evolving_the_Alien:_The_Science_of_Extraterrestrial_Life" class="mw-redirect" title="Evolving the Alien: The Science of Extraterrestrial Life">Evolving the Alien: The Science of Extraterrestrial Life</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Cohen_and_Stewart_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cohen_and_Stewart-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
