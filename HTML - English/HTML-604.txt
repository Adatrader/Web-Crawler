<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_notes">Usage notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=meilleur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Usage notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul><li>Early on the Middle French period, <i><span class="Latn" lang="frm">meilleur</span></i> is used as the masculine and feminine form with the plural <i class="Latn mention" lang="fr"><a href="/wiki/meilleurs#French" title="meilleurs">meilleurs</a></i> (this is the same as Old French <i class="Latn mention" lang="fro"><a href="/wiki/meillor#Old_French" title="meillor">meillor</a></i>). The forms <i class="Latn mention" lang="frm"><a href="/wiki/meilleure#Middle_French" title="meilleure">meilleure</a></i> as a feminine singular and <i class="Latn mention" lang="frm"><a href="/wiki/meilleures#Middle_French" title="meilleures">meilleures</a></i> as a feminine plural are both first attested in the 15th century.</li></ul>
