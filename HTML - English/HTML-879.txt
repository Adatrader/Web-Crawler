<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">tout</strong> (<i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en"><a href="/wiki/touts#English" title="touts">touts</a></b>)
</p>
<ol><li>Someone <a href="/wiki/advertise" title="advertise">advertising</a> for <a href="/wiki/customer" title="customer">customers</a> in an <a href="/wiki/aggressive" title="aggressive">aggressive</a> way.
<ul><li><b>1886</b>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_James" class="extiw" title="w:Henry James">Henry James</a>, <i>The Princess Casamassima</i>.
<dl><dd>Paul Muniment looked at his young friend a moment. 'Do you want to know what he is? He's a <b>tout</b>.'</dd>
<dd>'A <b>tout</b>? What do you mean?'</dd>
<dd>'Well, a cat's-paw, if you like better.'</dd>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_notes">Usage notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=tout&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Usage notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul><li>Like Modern French <i class="Latn mention" lang="fr"><a href="/wiki/tout#French" title="tout">tout</a></i>, when used as an intensifier it may inflect according to the gender and the number of what it is describing:
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="frm">Elle est <b>toute</b> morte</i> ― <span class="e-translation">she is completely dead</span></div></dd></dl></li>
<li>The uninflected form <i><span class="Latn" lang="frm">tout</span></i> is always used for describing terms that don't inflect with gender, such as verbs, adverbs and prepositions:
