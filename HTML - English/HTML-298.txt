<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology_1">Etymology 1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology 1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_language" class="extiw" title="w:Spanish language">Spanish</a></span>, a shortening of either <i class="Latn mention" lang="es"><a href="/wiki/florida#Spanish" title="florida">la Florida</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">the flowery one</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span> or <i class="Latn mention" lang="es">Pascua Florida</i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">flowery Easter</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-PDL_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PDL-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is the oldest surviving European-given place-name in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PDL_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PDL-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
