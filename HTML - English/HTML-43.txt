<p><b>Epcot</b> (stylized as <b>EPCOT</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Amusement_park" title="Amusement park">theme park</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_World" title="Walt Disney World">Walt Disney World Resort</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bay_Lake,_Florida" title="Bay Lake, Florida">Bay Lake, Florida</a>. It is owned and operated by <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">The Walt Disney Company</a> through its <a href="/wiki/Disney_Parks,_Experiences_and_Products" title="Disney Parks, Experiences and Products">Parks, Experiences and Products</a> division. Inspired by <a href="/wiki/EPCOT_(concept)" title="EPCOT (concept)">an unrealized concept</a> developed by <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney" title="Walt Disney">Walt Disney</a>, the park opened on October 1, 1982,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> as <b>EPCOT Center,</b> and was the second of four <a href="/wiki/Theme_park" class="mw-redirect" title="Theme park">theme parks</a> built at Walt Disney World, after the <a href="/wiki/Magic_Kingdom" title="Magic Kingdom">Magic Kingdom</a>. Spanning 305<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> acres (1.23&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), more than twice the size of the Magic Kingdom park,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Epcot is dedicated to the celebration of <a href="/wiki/Humanities" title="Humanities">human achievement</a>, namely technological <a href="/wiki/Innovation" title="Innovation">innovation</a> and international <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">culture</a>, and is often referred to as a "permanent <a href="/wiki/World%27s_fair" title="World&#39;s fair">world's fair</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Epcot was originally conceived by Walt Disney during the early development of Walt Disney World, as an experimental <a href="/wiki/Planned_community" title="Planned community">planned community</a> that would serve as a center for American innovation and urban living. Known as "<a href="/wiki/EPCOT_(concept)" title="EPCOT (concept)">EPCOT</a>", the idea included an urban city center, residential areas, churches, schools, and a series of mass transportation systems that would connect the community. After Disney's death in 1966, the "EPCOT" concept was abandoned as the company had uncertainty about maintaining an operating city. In the 1970s, <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Imagineering" title="Walt Disney Imagineering">WED Enterprises</a> began developing a second theme park for the resort to supplement Magic Kingdom, as that park's popularity grew. The new park maintained the idea of showcasing modern innovation and enterprise through <a href="/wiki/Avant-garde" title="Avant-garde">avant-garde</a> <a href="/wiki/Educational_entertainment" title="Educational entertainment">edutainment</a> attractions, as well as the addition of a <a href="/wiki/Nation" title="Nation">world nations</a> exposition. The newly-designed park, featuring two sections—Future World and World Showcase—opened as EPCOT Center in 1982. In 1994, the park was renamed to "Epcot", dropping the acronym and "Center" from the name.
</p><p>In 2018, Epcot hosted 13.444 million guests, ranking it as the fourth-most-visited theme park in North America and the seventh-most-visited theme park in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-2018_Report_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2018_Report-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The park is represented by <a href="/wiki/Spaceship_Earth_(Epcot)" title="Spaceship Earth (Epcot)">Spaceship Earth</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Geodesic_dome" title="Geodesic dome">geodesic sphere</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1960s:_Experimental_concept">1960s: Experimental concept</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1960s: Experimental concept">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/EPCOT_(concept)" title="EPCOT (concept)">EPCOT (concept)</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Epcot_Flower_Garden_Display_(5553395615).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Epcot_Flower_Garden_Display_%285553395615%29.jpg/220px-Epcot_Flower_Garden_Display_%285553395615%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Epcot_Flower_Garden_Display_%285553395615%29.jpg/330px-Epcot_Flower_Garden_Display_%285553395615%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Epcot_Flower_Garden_Display_%285553395615%29.jpg/440px-Epcot_Flower_Garden_Display_%285553395615%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3008" data-file-height="2000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Epcot_Flower_Garden_Display_(5553395615).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Epcot's varied architecture and landscaping.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Epcot_Disney_World_-_panoramio.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Epcot_Disney_World_-_panoramio.jpg/220px-Epcot_Disney_World_-_panoramio.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Epcot_Disney_World_-_panoramio.jpg/330px-Epcot_Disney_World_-_panoramio.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Epcot_Disney_World_-_panoramio.jpg/440px-Epcot_Disney_World_-_panoramio.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Epcot_Disney_World_-_panoramio.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Landscape of Future World West</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:EPCOT_-_panoramio_(6).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/EPCOT_-_panoramio_%286%29.jpg/220px-EPCOT_-_panoramio_%286%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/EPCOT_-_panoramio_%286%29.jpg/330px-EPCOT_-_panoramio_%286%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/EPCOT_-_panoramio_%286%29.jpg/440px-EPCOT_-_panoramio_%286%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4311" data-file-height="2851" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:EPCOT_-_panoramio_(6).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The landscape, which includes much water, grassy slopes, and trees</div></div></div>
<p>The park's name, Epcot, was previously an acronym for <a href="/wiki/EPCOT_(concept)" title="EPCOT (concept)">Experimental Prototype Community of Tomorrow</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Utopia" title="Utopia">utopian city</a> of the future planned by <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney" title="Walt Disney">Walt Disney</a>, often interchanging "city" and "community." In Walt Disney's words: "EPCOT will take its cue from the new ideas and new technologies that are now emerging from the creative centers of American industry. It will be a community of tomorrow that will never be completed but will always be introducing and testing, and demonstrating new materials and new systems. And EPCOT will always be a showcase to the world of the ingenuity and imagination of American free enterprise."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His original vision was for a model community which would have been home to twenty thousand residents and a test bed for <a href="/wiki/City_planning" class="mw-redirect" title="City planning">city planning</a> as well as organization. It was to have been built in the shape of a circle with businesses and commercial areas at its center with community buildings, schools, and recreational complexes around it while residential neighborhoods would line the perimeter. This radial plan concept is strongly influenced by British planner <a href="/wiki/Ebenezer_Howard" title="Ebenezer Howard">Ebenezer Howard</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Garden_Cities_of_To-morrow" title="Garden Cities of To-morrow">Garden Cities of To-morrow</a>. Transportation would have been provided by <a href="/wiki/Monorails" class="mw-redirect" title="Monorails">monorails</a> and <a href="/wiki/PeopleMover" title="PeopleMover">PeopleMovers</a> (like that in Magic Kingdom's <a href="/wiki/Tomorrowland" title="Tomorrowland">Tomorrowland</a>). Automobile traffic would be kept underground, leaving pedestrians safe above ground. The original model of EPCOT can still be seen by passengers riding the <a href="/wiki/Tomorrowland_Transit_Authority" class="mw-redirect" title="Tomorrowland Transit Authority">Tomorrowland Transit Authority</a> attraction in the Magic Kingdom park; when the PeopleMover enters the showhouse for <a href="/wiki/Stitch%27s_Great_Escape!" title="Stitch&#39;s Great Escape!">Stitch's Great Escape!</a>, the remaining portion of the model is visible on the left (when facing forward) behind glass. Walt Disney was not able to obtain funding and permission to start work on his Florida property until he agreed to first build Magic Kingdom. He died nearly five years before Magic Kingdom opened.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1970s:_Concept_evolves_into_park">1970s: Concept evolves into park</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1970s: Concept evolves into park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After Walt Disney's death, <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Walt Disney Productions</a> decided that it did not want to be in the business of running a city without Walt's guidance. The model community of <a href="/wiki/Celebration,_Florida" title="Celebration, Florida">Celebration, Florida</a> has been mentioned as a realization of Disney's original vision, but Celebration is based on concepts of <a href="/wiki/New_urbanism" class="mw-redirect" title="New urbanism">new urbanism</a> which is radically different from Disney's <a href="/wiki/Modernist" class="mw-redirect" title="Modernist">modernist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Futurist_architecture" title="Futurist architecture">futurist</a> visions. However, the idea of EPCOT was instrumental in prompting the state of Florida to create the <a href="/wiki/Reedy_Creek_Improvement_District" title="Reedy Creek Improvement District">Reedy Creek Improvement District</a> (RCID) and the cities of <a href="/wiki/Bay_Lake,_Florida" title="Bay Lake, Florida">Bay Lake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reedy_Creek,_Florida" class="mw-redirect" title="Reedy Creek, Florida">Reedy Creek</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Lake_Buena_Vista,_Florida" title="Lake Buena Vista, Florida">Lake Buena Vista</a>), a legislative mechanism allowing Disney to exercise governmental powers over Walt Disney World. Control over the RCID is vested in the landowners of the district, and the promise of an actual city in the district would have meant that the powers of the RCID would have been distributed among the landowners in EPCOT. Because the idea of EPCOT was never implemented, Disney remained almost the sole landowner in the district allowing it to maintain control of the RCID and the cities of Bay Lake and Lake Buena Vista; Disney further cemented this control by deannexing Celebration from the RCID.
</p><p>The original plans for the park showed indecision over the park's purpose. Some <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Imagineering" title="Walt Disney Imagineering">Imagineers</a> wanted it to represent the cutting edge of technology, while others wanted it to showcase international cultures and customs. At one point, a model of the futuristic park was pushed together against a model of a <a href="/wiki/World%27s_Fair" class="mw-redirect" title="World&#39;s Fair">World's Fair</a> international theme, and the two were combined. The park was originally named EPCOT Center to reflect the ideals and values of the city. It was constructed for an estimated $800&#160;million to $1.4&#160;billion and took three years to build, at the time the largest construction project on Earth.<sup id="cite_ref-Kurtti_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kurtti-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The parking lot serving the park is 141 acres (57&#160;ha) (including bus area) and can accommodate 11,211 vehicles (grass areas hold additional 500+ vehicles). Before it opened on October 1, 1982, Walt Disney World Ambassador Genie Field introduced <a href="/wiki/Card_Walker" title="Card Walker">E. Cardon Walker</a>, Disney's chairman and CEO, who dedicated EPCOT Center. Walker also presented a family with lifetime passes for the two Walt Disney World theme parks. His remarks were followed by Florida Governor <a href="/wiki/Bob_Graham" title="Bob Graham">Bob Graham</a> and William Ellinghaus, president of <a href="/wiki/American_Telephone_%26_Telegraph" class="mw-redirect" title="American Telephone &amp; Telegraph">AT&amp;T</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1980s:_Opening_and_operation">1980s: Opening and operation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1980s: Opening and operation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Opening_Ceremonies_for_EPCOT_Center.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Opening_Ceremonies_for_EPCOT_Center.jpg/220px-Opening_Ceremonies_for_EPCOT_Center.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Opening_Ceremonies_for_EPCOT_Center.jpg/330px-Opening_Ceremonies_for_EPCOT_Center.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Opening_Ceremonies_for_EPCOT_Center.jpg/440px-Opening_Ceremonies_for_EPCOT_Center.jpg 2x" data-file-width="900" data-file-height="600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Opening_Ceremonies_for_EPCOT_Center.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Opening ceremonies for EPCOT Center</div></div></div>
<p>As part of the opening-day ceremony, dancers and band members performed <i>We've Just Begun to Dream</i>. The <a href="/wiki/Sherman_Brothers" title="Sherman Brothers">Sherman Brothers</a> wrote a song especially for the occasion entitled "<a href="/wiki/The_World_Showcase_March" title="The World Showcase March">The World Showcase March</a>". During the finale, doves and many sets of balloons were released. Performing groups representing countries from all over the world performed in World Showcase. Water was gathered from major rivers across the globe and emptied into the park's fountain of nations ceremonial containers to mark the opening. Located at the front of the park is a plaque bearing Walker's opening-day dedication.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s-2000s:_Devalued_reputation">1990s-2000s: Devalued reputation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 1990s-2000s: Devalued reputation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Despite its initial success, Epcot constantly met the challenges of evolving with worldwide progress, an issue that caused the park to lose relevance and become outdated in the 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> To maintain attendance levels, Disney introduced seasonal events such as the <a href="/wiki/Epcot_International_Flower_%26_Garden_Festival" title="Epcot International Flower &amp; Garden Festival">International Flower &amp; Garden Festival</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Epcot_International_Food_%26_Wine_Festival" title="Epcot International Food &amp; Wine Festival">International Food &amp; Wine Festival</a> in 1994 and 1995, respectively. In the mid-1990s, Disney also began to gradually phase out the park's <a href="/wiki/Educational_entertainment" title="Educational entertainment">edutainment</a> attractions in favor of more modern and thrilling attractions.<sup id="cite_ref-:02_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:02-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> As a result, many of the attractions within the Future World pavilions, were either rehauled or replaced entirely. <a href="/wiki/Universe_of_Energy" title="Universe of Energy">Universe of Energy</a> was reconfigured as <a href="/wiki/Universe_of_Energy" title="Universe of Energy">Ellen's Energy Adventure</a> in 1996, <a href="/wiki/World_of_Motion" title="World of Motion">World of Motion</a> was rethemed as <a href="/wiki/Test_Track" title="Test Track">Test Track</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Horizons_(Epcot)" title="Horizons (Epcot)">Horizons</a> was demolished in 1999 and replaced with <a href="/wiki/Mission:_Space" title="Mission: Space">Mission: Space</a>. Walt Disney World held the <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_World_Millennium_Celebration" title="Walt Disney World Millennium Celebration">Millennium Celebration</a> with the central focus of the event at Epcot. <a href="/wiki/The_Seas_with_Nemo_%26_Friends" title="The Seas with Nemo &amp; Friends">The Living Seas</a> was closed in 2005, and rethemed with the introduction of characters from <i><a href="/wiki/Finding_Nemo_(franchise)" title="Finding Nemo (franchise)">Finding Nemo</a></i>, as <a href="/wiki/The_Seas_with_Nemo_%26_Friends" title="The Seas with Nemo &amp; Friends">The Seas with Nemo and Friends</a>. That same year, the <a href="/wiki/Soarin%27" title="Soarin&#39;">Soarin'</a> attraction was added to <a href="/wiki/The_Land_(Epcot)" title="The Land (Epcot)">The Land</a>. Wonders of Life closed in 2007, with the pavilon being occasionally used for the park's annual festivals.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010-present:_Transformation_and_redesign">2010-present: Transformation and redesign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2010-present: Transformation and redesign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In November 2016, Disney revealed that Epcot would be receiving “a major transformation” that would help transition the park into being “more Disney, timeless, relevant, family-friendly”, while keeping the original vision alive. No further details were mentioned.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In July 2017, The Walt Disney Company formally announced that Epcot would undergo a multi-year, redesign and expansion plan that would introduce <a href="/wiki/Guardians_of_the_Galaxy:_Cosmic_Rewind" title="Guardians of the Galaxy: Cosmic Rewind"><i>Guardians of the Galaxy</i>: Cosmic Rewind</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Ratatouille:_L%27Aventure_Totalement_Toqu%C3%A9e_de_R%C3%A9my" title="Ratatouille: L&#39;Aventure Totalement Toquée de Rémy">Ratatouille</a></i> attractions to Future World and World Showcase, respectively, as well as maintaining the original vision and spirit for the park.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> As part of the announcement, <a href="/wiki/Universe_of_Energy" title="Universe of Energy">Ellen's Energy Adventure</a> closed the following month, and the interior of the show building was gutted to be reused for <a href="/wiki/Guardians_of_the_Galaxy:_Cosmic_Rewind" title="Guardians of the Galaxy: Cosmic Rewind"><i>Guardians of the Galaxy</i>: Cosmic Rewind</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> That same year, the park reported the first drop in overall attendance ranking among the four Walt Disney World Resort parks, dropping from second to third place, the first in its history.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 25, 2019, at the 2019 D23 Expo, Disney expanded on the plans for the improvements to Epcot.<sup id="cite_ref-Epcot-D23-20192_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Epcot-D23-20192-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> One of the most significant changes announced was the creation of four distinct "neighborhoods", with the subdivision of Future World into three of the aforementioned areas and World Showcase remaining as the fourth one.<sup id="cite_ref-Epcot-D23-20192_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Epcot-D23-20192-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The park's name will change from "Epcot" to "EPCOT".<sup id="cite_ref-:02_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:02-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Celebration">World Celebration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: World Celebration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Disney officials described World Celebration as the home of "new experiences that connect us to one another and the world around us".<sup id="cite_ref-Epcot-D23-20192_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Epcot-D23-20192-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> It will include the park's revised main entrance, a reimagined <a href="/wiki/Spaceship_Earth_(Epcot)" title="Spaceship Earth (Epcot)">Spaceship Earth</a> and the removal of <a href="/wiki/Innoventions_(Epcot)" title="Innoventions (Epcot)">the Innoventions</a> buildings. Just beyond Spaceship Earth will be Dreamer's Point, which will include a new statue of Walt Disney, and a three-story festival pavilion which includes a 360 degree viewing platform on the top level.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Nature">World Nature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: World Nature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The western side of Future World will be renamed to World Nature, which will focus on "understanding and preserving the beauty, awe and balance of the natural world".<sup id="cite_ref-Epcot-D23-20192_17-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Epcot-D23-20192-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/The_Land_(Epcot)" title="The Land (Epcot)">The Land</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Seas_with_Nemo_%26_Friends" title="The Seas with Nemo &amp; Friends">The Seas with Nemo and Friends pavilions</a> will be incorporated into this area, along with a new attraction named "<a href="/wiki/Journey_of_Water" title="Journey of Water">Journey of Water</a>", which draws its inspiration from the 2016 animated feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Moana_(2016_film)" title="Moana (2016 film)">Moana</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Discovery">World Discovery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: World Discovery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The eastern side of Future World will host several major thrill attractions as begin the "stories about spaces, science, technology and intergalactic adventure comes to life", including <a href="/wiki/Test_Track" title="Test Track">Test Track</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mission:_Space" title="Mission: Space">Mission: Space</a>, and the under-construction <a href="/wiki/Guardians_of_the_Galaxy:_Cosmic_Rewind" title="Guardians of the Galaxy: Cosmic Rewind"><i>Guardians of the Galaxy</i>: Cosmic Rewind</a>. Mission: Space will be expanded to include a new restaurant, Space 220, which will simulate dining aboard a space station located 220 miles above Earth.<sup id="cite_ref-Epcot-D23-20192_17-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Epcot-D23-20192-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> It will be operated by Patina Restaurant Group, which also operates the two restaurants in the Italy pavilion, <a href="/wiki/Via_Napoli_Pizzeria_e_Ristorante" class="mw-redirect" title="Via Napoli Pizzeria e Ristorante">Via Napoli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tutto_Italia_Ristorante" class="mw-redirect" title="Tutto Italia Ristorante">Tutto Italia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The dormant Wonders of Life pavilion will be renovated into <a href="/wiki/Play!" class="mw-redirect" title="Play!">Play!</a>, an attraction where guests will interact with popular Disney characters in various activities.<sup id="cite_ref-Epcot-D23-20192_17-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Epcot-D23-20192-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Changes_to_World_Showcase">Changes to World Showcase</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Changes to World Showcase">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The current World Showcase area will maintain its current theme, continuing to be "a celebration of culture, cuisine, architecture, and traditions – infused with new magic."<sup id="cite_ref-Epcot-D23-20192_17-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Epcot-D23-20192-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/France_Pavilion_at_Epcot" title="France Pavilion at Epcot">France</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Pavilion_at_Epcot" title="United Kingdom Pavilion at Epcot">United Kingdom</a> pavilions will receive new attractions inspired by <i><a href="/wiki/Ratatouille:_L%27Aventure_Totalement_Toqu%C3%A9e_de_R%C3%A9my" title="Ratatouille: L&#39;Aventure Totalement Toquée de Rémy">Ratatouille</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Untitled_Mary_Poppins_attraction" title="Untitled Mary Poppins attraction">Mary Poppins</a></i>, respectively. A new nighttime spectacular, <i><a href="/wiki/HarmonioUS" title="HarmonioUS">HarmonioUS</a></i>, will honor Disney music interpreted by various global cultures. The show will replace <i><a href="/wiki/IllumiNations:_Reflections_of_Earth" title="IllumiNations: Reflections of Earth">IllumiNations: Reflections of Earth</a></i> and will feature similar elements including pyrotechnics, choreographed water fountains, searchlights, lasers,  floating set pieces, and LED panels.<sup id="cite_ref-Epcot-D23-20192_17-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Epcot-D23-20192-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Areas">Areas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Areas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Epcot_attractions" title="List of Epcot attractions">List of Epcot attractions</a></div>
<p>Epcot is divided into two main themed areas: Future World and World Showcase. The World Showcase usually opens two hours after park opening and remains open later than the Future World section of the park. Most major attractions in Future World remain open until the park's closing time. A secondary park gate is located between the France and United Kingdom pavilions of World Showcase and is known as the International Gateway. The International Gateway is directly accessible to guests arriving from the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Skyliner" title="Disney Skyliner">Disney Skyliner</a> and the nearby <a href="/wiki/Epcot_Area_Resorts" class="mw-redirect" title="Epcot Area Resorts">Epcot Area Resorts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Disney%27s_Hollywood_Studios" title="Disney&#39;s Hollywood Studios">Disney's Hollywood Studios</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p><b>Future World</b> consists of a variety of <a href="/wiki/Avant-garde" title="Avant-garde">avant-garde</a> <a href="/wiki/Pavilion" title="Pavilion">pavilions</a> that explore innovative aspects and applications including technology and science, with each pavilion featuring self-contained attractions and distinct architecture in its design. Future World also serves as the park's main entrance and features <a href="/wiki/Spaceship_Earth_(Epcot)" title="Spaceship Earth (Epcot)">Spaceship Earth</a>, a large <a href="/wiki/Geodesic_dome" title="Geodesic dome">geodesic sphere</a> structure and the anchor pavilion, which also houses an eponymous <a href="/wiki/Dark_ride" title="Dark ride">dark ride</a> attraction that depicts the history of <a href="/wiki/Communication" title="Communication">communication</a>. At the center of Future World is the former <a href="/wiki/Innoventions_(Epcot)" title="Innoventions (Epcot)">Innoventions</a> plaza. From this position, the remaining six pavilions surround the plaza in clockwise order:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Imagination!_(Epcot)" title="Imagination! (Epcot)">Imagination!</a> is themed to imagination and creativity and features two attractions; <a href="/wiki/Journey_into_Imagination_with_Figment" title="Journey into Imagination with Figment">Journey into Imagination with Figment</a> and the Disney-Pixar Short Film Festival.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/The_Land_(Epcot)" title="The Land (Epcot)">The Land</a> depicts <a href="/wiki/Human_impact_on_the_environment" title="Human impact on the environment">human interaction with the Earth</a>, focusing on <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecology" title="Ecology">ecology</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Travel" title="Travel">travel</a>. The pavilion contains <a href="/wiki/Soarin%27" title="Soarin&#39;">Soarin' Around the World</a>, an attraction that simulates a <a href="/wiki/Hang_gliding" title="Hang gliding">hang gliding</a> flight over various regions of the world; <a href="/wiki/Living_with_the_Land" title="Living with the Land">Living with the Land</a>, a narrated boat tour through <a href="/wiki/Audio-Animatronics" title="Audio-Animatronics">Audio-Animatronics</a> scenes, a <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse" title="Greenhouse">greenhouse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hydroponics" title="Hydroponics">hydroponics</a> lab; and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Land_(Epcot)" title="The Land (Epcot)">Awesome Planet</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/4D_film" title="4D film">4D film</a> about the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>'s biomes and the perils of <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">climate change</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/The_Seas_with_Nemo_%26_Friends" title="The Seas with Nemo &amp; Friends">The Seas with Nemo &amp; Friends</a> is based on <a href="/wiki/Ocean_exploration" title="Ocean exploration">ocean exploration</a> inspired by <i><a href="/wiki/Finding_Nemo" title="Finding Nemo">Finding Nemo</a></i> and features an Omnimover attraction, <a href="/wiki/Turtle_Talk_with_Crush" title="Turtle Talk with Crush">Turtle Talk with Crush</a>, and an aquarium with animal exhibits.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mission:_Space" title="Mission: Space">Mission: Space</a>, centered on <a href="/wiki/Space_exploration" title="Space exploration">space exploration</a>, is a <a href="/wiki/Centrifuge" title="Centrifuge">centrifugal</a> <a href="/wiki/Motion_simulator" title="Motion simulator">motion simulator</a> thrill ride that replicates a space flight experience to <a href="/wiki/Mars" title="Mars">Mars</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Geocentric_orbit" title="Geocentric orbit">low orbit</a> tour over the surface of Earth.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Test_Track" title="Test Track">Test Track</a> is a thrill ride inspired by the rigorous <a href="/wiki/Automotive_engineering" title="Automotive engineering">automobile testing</a> procedures that <a href="/wiki/General_Motors" title="General Motors">General Motors</a> uses to evaluate concept cars.</li>
<li>The Odyssey Events Pavilion currently features a <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Imagineering" title="Walt Disney Imagineering">Walt Disney Imagineering</a> preview exhibit detailing the forthcoming redesign plans for Epcot.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>Originally, Future World debuted with six pavilions: Spaceship Earth, <a href="/wiki/CommuniCore" title="CommuniCore">CommuniCore</a>, Imagination!, The Land, <a href="/wiki/Universe_of_Energy" title="Universe of Energy">Universe of Energy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/World_of_Motion" title="World of Motion">World of Motion</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Horizons_(Epcot)" title="Horizons (Epcot)">Horizons</a> pavilion opened the following year, and <a href="/wiki/The_Seas_with_Nemo_%26_Friends" title="The Seas with Nemo &amp; Friends">The Living Seas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wonders_of_Life" title="Wonders of Life">Wonders of Life</a> pavilions were added in 1986 and 1989, respectively, bringing the lineup to nine. CommuniCore, World of Motion, Horizons, Wonders of Life, Universe of Energy, and Innoventions closed in 1994, 1996, 1999, 2007, 2017, and 2019, respectively. The <a href="/wiki/Fountain_of_Nations" class="mw-redirect" title="Fountain of Nations">Fountain of Nations</a>, a large circular <a href="/wiki/Musical_fountain" title="Musical fountain">musical fountain</a>, was closed in 2019 as well. Each pavilion was initially sponsored by a corporation which helped fund its construction and maintenance in return for the corporation's logos and some marketing elements appearing throughout the pavilion.
</p><p>Additionally, each pavilion of Future World featured a unique circular logo which was featured on <a href="/wiki/Marquee_(structure)" title="Marquee (structure)">park signage</a>, and throughout the attractions themselves. The pavilion logos were gradually phased out in the early 2000s, as the pavilions instead were identified by name and recognized by the main attraction(s) housed inside. Several homages remain scattered throughout the park, including merchandising. However, at D23 Expo 2019, Disney revealed that the concept of the circular pavilion logos would be revived as part of Epcot's transformation, with both classic logos revived and new logos introduced.<sup id="cite_ref-Epcot_pavilion_logos_revival_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Epcot_pavilion_logos_revival-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>There is a small pavilion between China and Germany called the African Outpost, not included as one of the official World Showcase pavilions.
</p><p>Of the 11 pavilions, only <a href="/wiki/Morocco_Pavilion_at_Epcot" title="Morocco Pavilion at Epcot">Morocco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norway_Pavilion_at_Epcot" title="Norway Pavilion at Epcot">Norway</a> were not present at the park's opening, as they were added in 1984 and 1988, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-World_Showcase_theoretical_expansion_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-World_Showcase_theoretical_expansion-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Each pavilion contains themed architecture, landscapes, streetscapes, attractions, shops and restaurants representing the respective country's culture and cuisine. In an effort to maintain the authenticity of the represented countries, the pavilions are primarily staffed by citizens of the respective countries as part of the <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_World_International_Program#Cultural_Representative_Program" title="Walt Disney World International Program">Cultural Representative Program</a> through <a href="/wiki/Q1_visa" class="mw-redirect" title="Q1 visa">Q1 visa</a> agreements. Some pavilions also contain themed <a href="/wiki/List_of_Epcot_attractions#World_Showcase" title="List of Epcot attractions">rides, shows, and live entertainment</a> representative of the respective country. The only pavilion that is directly sponsored by the government of its respective country is Morocco; the remaining pavilions are primarily sponsored by private companies with affiliations to the represented countries.
</p><p>Originally, the showcase was to include partnerships with the governments of the different countries. According to Disney's 1975 Annual Report, the Showcase would:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r886047036"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>...offer participating countries a permanent installation for such features as themed restaurants and shops, product exhibits, industrial displays, cultural presentations, a trade center, and even special facilities for business meetings.
</p><p>Major sponsorships for each participating nation will be asked to provide the capital to cover the cost of designing, developing and constructing its attraction and/or ride and all exhibits, as well as the Pavilion itself. It will also have the responsibility for funding the housing for its employees in the International Village. Its land lease will cover the cost of maintaining the attraction for a minimum of ten years.
</p><p>The Disney organization will be responsible for area development, including the construction of transportation systems and utilities. We will also build and operate the internal people moving system, the Courtyard of Nations and central theater facility.<sup id="cite_ref-World_Showcase_theoretical_expansion_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-World_Showcase_theoretical_expansion-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
</blockquote>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_pavilions_and_unused_locations">Proposed pavilions and unused locations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Proposed pavilions and unused locations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Pavilions for Brazil, the Philippines, Puerto Rico,<sup id="cite_ref-Puerto_Rico_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Puerto_Rico-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Russia, Denmark, Switzerland,<sup id="cite_ref-Abandoned_-_Epcot_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Abandoned_-_Epcot-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Costa Rica, Spain, Venezuela,<sup id="cite_ref-Abandoned_-_Epcot_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Abandoned_-_Epcot-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> the United Arab Emirates, and Israel have occasionally been rumored as potential future pavilions but have never made it past the planning phases to date. However, Disney did go as far as announcing three of the pavilions in the 1980s with signage throughout the park.
</p><p>The Israeli, Spanish, and an Equatorial Africa pavilion (blending elements of the cultures of countries such as Kenya and Zaire) were even announced as coming soon in 1982, and a model of the latter was shown on the opening day telecast, but never took off.<sup id="cite_ref-Abandoned_-_Epcot_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Abandoned_-_Epcot-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Instead, a small African themed refreshment shop known as the Outpost currently resides where Equatorial Africa was to be.<sup id="cite_ref-World_Showcase_theoretical_expansion_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-World_Showcase_theoretical_expansion-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Israel, five African countries (Eritrea, Ethiopia, Kenya, Namibia, and South Africa), as well as eight other countries (Brazil, Chile, India, Indonesia, New Zealand, Saudi Arabia, Scotland, and Sweden), took part in the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Village" title="Millennium Village">Millennium Village</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_World_Millennium_Celebration" title="Walt Disney World Millennium Celebration">Millennium Celebration</a>.
</p><p>There are currently eight undeveloped spots for countries around the World Showcase—including the space occupied by the Outpost—in between the locations of the current countries. Two of the potential locations, on either side of the United Kingdom, are currently occupied by <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Village" title="Millennium Village">World ShowPlace</a>. Two more lie on either side of the American Adventure, though this pavilion's use of reversed <a href="/wiki/Forced_perspective" title="Forced perspective">forced perspective</a> may preclude the construction of additional buildings as they would ruin the illusion.<sup id="cite_ref-World_Showcase_theoretical_expansion_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-World_Showcase_theoretical_expansion-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The new Ratatouille attraction is also taking up one of the undeveloped spots.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Alcohol_policy">Alcohol policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Alcohol policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Unlike Magic Kingdom, which up until 2012 did not serve alcohol and now only serves it in all table service locations,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> most stores and restaurants at Epcot, especially in the World Showcase, serve and/or sell a variety of alcoholic beverages including specialty drinks, craft beers, wines, and spirits reflective of the respective countries. The park also hosts the <a href="/wiki/Epcot_International_Food_%26_Wine_Festival" title="Epcot International Food &amp; Wine Festival">Epcot International Food &amp; Wine Festival</a>, an annual event featuring food and drink samplings from all over the world, along with live entertainment and special exhibits.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_World_Showcase_Adventure">The World Showcase Adventure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: The World Showcase Adventure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Originally based on the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> animated series <i><a href="/wiki/Kim_Possible" title="Kim Possible">Kim Possible</a></i>, the World Showcase Adventure is an interactive mobile attraction taking place in several pavilions throughout the World Showcase. The attraction is an electronic <a href="/wiki/Scavenger_hunt" title="Scavenger hunt">scavenger hunt</a> that has guests using special "Kimmunicators" (in actuality, customized cell phones) to help teenage crime-fighters Kim Possible and Ron Stoppable solve a "crime" or disrupt an evil-doer's "plans for global domination." The "Kimmunicator" is able to trigger specific events within the pavilion grounds that provide clues to completing the adventure. Launched in January 2009 and presented by <a href="/wiki/Verizon_Wireless" title="Verizon Wireless">Verizon Wireless</a>, the Adventure is included in park admission. It was succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Perry_the_Platypus" title="Perry the Platypus">Agent P</a>'s World Showcase Adventure, based on Disney's <i><a href="/wiki/Phineas_and_Ferb" title="Phineas and Ferb">Phineas and Ferb</a></i>, on June 23, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Agent P's World Showcase Adventure was later closed on February 17, 2020 and will be replaced by the DuckTales World Showcase Adventure based on the <a href="/wiki/DuckTales_(2017_TV_series)" title="DuckTales (2017 TV series)">2017 reboot of DuckTales.</a><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Annual_events">Annual events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Annual events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Epcot_FlowerandGarden_2016_11_(25211946484).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Epcot_FlowerandGarden_2016_11_%2825211946484%29.jpg/220px-Epcot_FlowerandGarden_2016_11_%2825211946484%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Epcot_FlowerandGarden_2016_11_%2825211946484%29.jpg/330px-Epcot_FlowerandGarden_2016_11_%2825211946484%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Epcot_FlowerandGarden_2016_11_%2825211946484%29.jpg/440px-Epcot_FlowerandGarden_2016_11_%2825211946484%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2000" data-file-height="1500" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Epcot_FlowerandGarden_2016_11_(25211946484).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Epcot during the annual Flower and Garden Festival.</div></div></div>
<p>Epcot hosts a number of special events during the year:
</p>
<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Epcot_International_Flower_and_Garden_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Epcot International Flower and Garden Festival">Epcot International Flower and Garden Festival</a>, inaugurated in 1994, uses specially-themed floral displays throughout the park, including <a href="/wiki/Topiary" title="Topiary">topiary</a> sculptures of Disney characters. Guests can meet gardening experts and learn new ideas they can use in their own home gardens. The 18th annual event was scheduled for March 2&#160;– May 15. Each event takes more than a full year to plan and more than 20,000 cast member hours.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Epcot_International_Food_%26_Wine_Festival" title="Epcot International Food &amp; Wine Festival">Epcot International Food &amp; Wine Festival</a>, inaugurated in 1995, draws amateur and professional gourmets to sample delicacies from all around the world, including nations that do not have a permanent presence in World Showcase. Celebrity chefs are often on-hand to host the events. In 2008, the festival featured the <a href="/wiki/Bocuse_d%27Or_USA" title="Bocuse d&#39;Or USA">Bocuse d'Or USA</a>, the American semifinal of the biennial <a href="/wiki/Bocuse_d%27Or" title="Bocuse d&#39;Or">Bocuse d'Or</a> cooking competition.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The Epcot International Festival of the Arts inaugurated in 2017, is a festival showcasing visual, culinary, and performing arts.  The first annual event took place on weekends from January 13 through February 20, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The 3rd annual Epcot International Festival of the Arts took place January 18 - February 25, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The Epcot International Festival of the Holidays is Epcot's annual holiday celebration. The World Showcase pavilions feature storytellers describing their nation's holiday traditions, and three nightly performances of the "Candlelight Processional" featuring an auditioned mass choir and a celebrity guest narrating the story of Christmas. During the festival, Illuminations: Reflections of Earth features a special extended ending.</li>
<li>On <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Eve" title="New Year&#39;s Eve">New Year's Eve</a>, the park offers a variety of additional entertainment including live DJ dance areas throughout the park and a special New Year's Eve countdown edition of <a href="/wiki/IllumiNations:_Reflections_of_Earth#New_Year&#39;s_Eve_Countdown_Edition" title="IllumiNations: Reflections of Earth">IllumiNations: Reflections of Earth</a>.</li></ul>
