<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Old_French">Old French</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=tiule&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Old French">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=tiule&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin" class="extiw" title="w:Latin">Latin</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="la"><a href="/wiki/tegula#Latin" title="tegula">tēgula</a></i>, as with <i class="Latn mention" lang="fro"><a href="/w/index.php?title=riule&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="riule (page does not exist)">riule</a></i>, <i class="Latn mention" lang="fro"><a href="/wiki/reule#Old_French" title="reule">reule</a></i> from <i class="Latn mention" lang="la"><a href="/wiki/regula#Latin" title="regula">rēgula</a></i>; either retained through the influence of preservative analogy or reintroduced by scholars<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>. Compare also <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_French" class="extiw" title="w:Middle French">Middle French</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="frm"><a href="/wiki/teille#Middle_French" title="teille">teille</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">flared earthenware vessel</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>, and Champenois <i>teille</i>, from the shortened Vulgar Latin <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">*tegla</i>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pronunciation">Pronunciation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=tiule&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pronunciation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Wiktionary:International_Phonetic_Alphabet" title="Wiktionary:International Phonetic Alphabet">IPA</a><sup>(<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_French_phonology" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Old French phonology">key</a>)</sup>:&#32;<span class="IPA">/ˈtiw.lə/</span>, <span class="IPA">[ˈtiɥ.lə]</span></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=tiule&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Noun">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="fro">tiule</strong>&#160;<span class="gender"><abbr title="feminine gender">f</abbr></span> (<i>oblique plural</i> <b class="Latn" lang="fro"><a href="/w/index.php?title=tiules&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="tiules (page does not exist)">tiules</a></b>, <i>nominative singular</i> <b class="Latn" lang="fro"><strong class="selflink">tiule</strong></b>, <i>nominative plural</i> <b class="Latn" lang="fro"><a href="/w/index.php?title=tiules&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="tiules (page does not exist)">tiules</a></b>)
</p>
