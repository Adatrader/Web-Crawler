<p><b>Larry P Ferguson</b> (March 19, 1940 – May 31, 2015) was a <a href="/wiki/College_football" title="College football">college football</a> player for the <a href="/wiki/Iowa_Hawkeyes_Football" class="mw-redirect" title="Iowa Hawkeyes Football">University of Iowa</a>.  He was named a first team <a href="/wiki/All-American" class="mw-redirect" title="All-American">All-American</a> in 1960 and played one season for the <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Lions" title="Detroit Lions">Detroit Lions</a>. He has six kids Darrick, Lori, Larry Jr., Vicki, Rachella, and James. He also has 8 grandchildren. Laurel, Camille, Jerome Jr., Christian, Brandon, Alyssa, Cameron, and Edward Jr.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="College_career">College career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Larry_Ferguson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: College career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Larry Ferguson only carried the ball nine times for Iowa as a sophomore in 1959, but he gained national attention as a junior in 1960.  He had an 85-yard touchdown run in the season opener against Oregon State, and he had a 70-yard interception return for a touchdown the following week.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  In Iowa’s final conference game that season, Ferguson had a 91-yard touchdown run against Ohio State to help give Iowa a 35-12 victory and a share of the 1960 Big Ten title.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  He was voted first team all-Big Ten and first team All-American as a junior in 1960.
</p><p>In 1961, Ferguson suffered a season ending knee injury in the first quarter of the first game.  He was given a medical redshirt and returned for his senior season in 1962.  As a senior in 1962, Larry Ferguson led the Hawkeyes in rushing with 547 yards and was named all-Big Ten.  As team captain, he helped lead Iowa to victories over Michigan and Ohio State, the first time Iowa had ever defeated both of those schools in the same season.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ferguson played in the 1963 <a href="/wiki/College_All-Star_Game" class="mw-redirect" title="College All-Star Game">College All-Star Game</a>. His season average of 7.39 yards per carry in 1960 still stands as a school record.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  In 1989, Iowa fans selected an all-time <a href="/wiki/University_of_Iowa" title="University of Iowa">University of Iowa</a> football team during the 100th anniversary celebration of Iowa football, and Larry Ferguson was an honorable mention selection at running back.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Larry_Ferguson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ferguson played one season with the <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Lions" title="Detroit Lions">Detroit Lions</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">National Football League</a> (NFL) in 1963.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He then played for the <a href="/wiki/Edmonton_Eskimos" title="Edmonton Eskimos">Edmonton Eskimos</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Football_League" title="Canadian Football League">Canadian Football League</a> (CFL) from 1964 to 1965 and the Toronto Argonauts of the CFL from 1965 to 1967.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
