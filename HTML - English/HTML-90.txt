<p><b>George H. Scithers</b> (May 14, 1929 – April 19, 2010) was an American science fiction <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction_fandom" title="Science fiction fandom">fan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction_author" class="mw-redirect" title="Science fiction author">author</a> and  editor.
</p><p>A long-time member of the <a href="/wiki/World_Science_Fiction_Society" class="mw-redirect" title="World Science Fiction Society">World Science Fiction Society</a>, he published a <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction_fanzine" class="mw-redirect" title="Science fiction fanzine">fanzine</a> starting in the 1950s, wrote short stories, and moved on to edit several prominent science fiction magazines, as well as a number of anthologies. As editor emeritus of <i><a href="/wiki/Weird_Tales" title="Weird Tales">Weird Tales</a></i>, he lectured at the <a href="/wiki/Library_of_Congress" title="Library of Congress">Library of Congress</a> in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Wildside_Press" title="Wildside Press">Wildside Press</a> published his most recent book, <i>Cat Tales: Fantastic Feline Fiction</i>, in 2008.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_H._Scithers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_H._Scithers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Scithers' first published  fiction, the story "Faithful Messenger", appeared in <a href="/wiki/If_magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="If magazine"><i>If</i> magazine</a> in 1969. His involvement in the field, however, dates back to 1957, when he began submitting to the <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction_fanzine" class="mw-redirect" title="Science fiction fanzine">fanzine</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Yandro" title="Yandro">Yandro</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-WindyCon5_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WindyCon5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Two years later, he began publishing the <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Award_for_Best_Fanzine" title="Hugo Award for Best Fanzine">Hugo Award</a>-winning <a href="/wiki/Fanzine" title="Fanzine">fanzine</a> <i>Amra</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-LocusHugos_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LocusHugos-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The term <i><a href="/wiki/Swords_and_sorcery" class="mw-redirect" title="Swords and sorcery">Swords and sorcery</a></i> first appeared there, and <i>Amra</i> became a leading proponent of the subgenre.<sup id="cite_ref-WindyCon5_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WindyCon5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Several of the articles originally published in <i>Amra</i> were later re-printed as part of two volumes about <a href="/wiki/Conan_the_Barbarian" title="Conan the Barbarian">Conan the Barbarian</a> which Scithers co-edited with <a href="/wiki/L._Sprague_de_Camp" title="L. Sprague de Camp">L. Sprague de Camp</a>.
</p><p>In 1963, Scithers chaired <a href="/wiki/21st_World_Science_Fiction_Convention" title="21st World Science Fiction Convention">Discon I</a>, the 21st <a href="/wiki/Worldcon" title="Worldcon">Worldcon</a>, held in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He was a regular <a href="/wiki/Parliamentarian_(consultant)" title="Parliamentarian (consultant)">parliamentarian</a> for business meetings of the <a href="/wiki/World_Science_Fiction_Society" class="mw-redirect" title="World Science Fiction Society">World Science Fiction Society</a> and authored a guide to running <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction_convention" title="Science fiction convention">science fiction conventions</a>, <i>The Con-Committee Chairman's Guide</i> based on his experiences chairing DisCon 1 in 1963.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1973, Scithers founded <a href="/w/index.php?title=Owlswick_Press&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Owlswick Press (page does not exist)">Owlswick Press</a>, a small independent publishing company.  In 1976, Owlswick published Scithers' book (under the pseudonym Karl Würf), <i>To Serve Man: A Cookbook for People</i> (including recipes for "Boiled Leg of Man", "Texas Chili with Cowboy", and "Person Kebabs").
</p><p>In 1977, he was named the first editor for <i><a href="/wiki/Asimov%27s_Science_Fiction" title="Asimov&#39;s Science Fiction">Isaac Asimov's Science Fiction Magazine</a></i> (<i>IASFM</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  He remained in that position until 1982 and won two more <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Award_for_Best_Professional_Editor" title="Hugo Award for Best Professional Editor">Hugo Awards</a> for his work there.<sup id="cite_ref-LocusHugos_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LocusHugos-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> After leaving <i>IASFM</i>, Scithers took the helm at <i><a href="/wiki/Amazing_Stories" title="Amazing Stories">Amazing Stories</a></i> and edited that magazine until 1986.
</p><p>In 1988, he worked with <a href="/wiki/John_Gregory_Betancourt" title="John Gregory Betancourt">John Gregory Betancourt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Darrell_Schweitzer" title="Darrell Schweitzer">Darrell Schweitzer</a> to re-establish <i><a href="/wiki/Weird_Tales" title="Weird Tales">Weird Tales</a></i>, the magazine that had introduced one of his earliest interests, <a href="/wiki/Conan_the_Barbarian" title="Conan the Barbarian">Conan the Barbarian</a>, to the world.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  In 1992, he and Schweitzer won a World Fantasy Award for their work on <i>Weird Tales</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-worldfantasy.org_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-worldfantasy.org-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2001, Scithers was the fan guest of honor at the Worldcon, <a href="/wiki/59th_World_Science_Fiction_Convention" title="59th World Science Fiction Convention">Millennium Philcon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the 2002 World Fantasy Convention in Minneapolis, both Scithers and <a href="/wiki/Forrest_J_Ackerman" title="Forrest J Ackerman">Forrest J Ackerman</a> won the World Fantasy Lifetime Achievement Awards.<sup id="cite_ref-worldfantasy.org_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-worldfantasy.org-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_H._Scithers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Scithers served in the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a> with the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a>. He was a member of the all-male literary banqueting club the <a href="/wiki/Trap_Door_Spiders" title="Trap Door Spiders">Trap Door Spiders</a>, which served as the basis of <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Asimov" title="Isaac Asimov">Isaac Asimov</a>'s fictional group of mystery solvers the <a href="/wiki/Black_Widowers" title="Black Widowers">Black Widowers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Glyer_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Glyer-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He was also very fond of owls and trains.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  He resided in <a href="/wiki/King_of_Prussia,_Pennsylvania" title="King of Prussia, Pennsylvania">King of Prussia, Pennsylvania</a> where <i><a href="/wiki/Weird_Tales" title="Weird Tales">Weird Tales</a></i> was edited in his basement, followed by <a href="/wiki/Rockville,_Maryland" title="Rockville, Maryland">Rockville, Maryland</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_H._Scithers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Scithers died April 19, 2010, two days after suffering a heart attack.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
