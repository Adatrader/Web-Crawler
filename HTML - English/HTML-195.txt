<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=bonus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Noun">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

</p>
<ol><li>Something <a href="/wiki/extra" title="extra">extra</a> that is good; an added benefit.</li>
<li>An extra sum given as a <a href="/wiki/premium" title="premium">premium</a>, e.g. to an employee or to a shareholder.
<ul><li><div class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><b>2013</b> June 22,  “<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.economist.com/news/finance-and-economics/21579879-buy-out-firm-really-does-focus-operational-improvements-engineers">Engineers of a different kind</a>”, in  <cite><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Economist" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The Economist">The Economist</a></cite>, volume 407, number 8841, page 70:</span><dl><dd><div class="h-quotation"><span class="Latn e-quotation" lang="en">Private-equity nabobs bristle at being dubbed mere financiers.<span class="q-hellip-sp">&#32;</span><span class="q-hellip-b">[</span>…<span class="q-hellip-b">]</span><span class="q-hellip-b">&#32;</span>Much of their pleading is public-relations bluster. Clever financial ploys are what have made billionaires of the industry’s veterans. “Operational improvement” in a portfolio company has often meant little more than promising colossal <b>bonuses</b> to sitting chief executives if they meet ambitious growth targets. That model is still prevalent today.</span></div></dd></dl></div></li></ul>
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">The employee of the week receives a <b>bonus</b> for his excellent work.</i></div></dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content"><a href="/wiki/video_game" title="video game">video games</a></span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> An addition to the <a href="/wiki/player" title="player">player</a>'s <a href="/wiki/score" title="score">score</a> based on <a href="/wiki/performance" title="performance">performance</a>, e.g. for time remaining.
<ul><li><b>1988</b>, David Powell, <i>Rygar</i> (video game review) in <i>Your Sinclair</i> issue 25
<dl><dd>Spend the time killing things and there's a <b>bonus</b> for each hit - but only for fatalities notched up since the start of your current life.</dd></dl></li></ul></li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology_6">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=bonus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Latin" class="extiw" title="w:Old Latin">Old Latin</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="itc-ola"><a href="/wiki/duenos#Old_Latin" title="duenos">duenos</a></i>, later <i class="Latn mention" lang="itc-ola"><a href="/wiki/duonus#Old_Latin" title="duonus">duonus</a></i>, from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Italic_language" class="extiw" title="w:Proto-Italic language">Proto-Italic</a></span> <i class="Latinx mention" lang="itc-pro"><a href="/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Italic/dwenos" title="Reconstruction:Proto-Italic/dwenos">*dwenos</a></i>, from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Indo-European_language" class="extiw" title="w:Proto-Indo-European language">Proto-Indo-European</a></span> <i class="Latinx mention" lang="ine-pro"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/dew-&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/dew- (page does not exist)">*dew-</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">to show favor, revere</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>. Some relate it to <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek" class="extiw" title="w:Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a></span> <i class="polytonic mention" lang="grc"><a href="/wiki/%CE%B4%CE%AD%CE%BF%CF%82#Ancient_Greek" title="δέος">δέος</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span lang="grc-Latn" class="mention-tr tr Latn">déos</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, whence <i class="polytonic mention" lang="grc"><a href="/wiki/%CE%B4%CE%B5%CE%B9%CE%BD%CF%8C%CF%82#Ancient_Greek" title="δεινός">δεινός</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span lang="grc-Latn" class="mention-tr tr Latn">deinós</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, <i class="polytonic mention" lang="grc"><a href="/wiki/%CE%B4%CE%B5%CE%B9%CE%BB%CF%8C%CF%82#Ancient_Greek" title="δειλός">δειλός</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span lang="grc-Latn" class="mention-tr tr Latn">deilós</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>.
Compare the change from <i class="Latn mention" lang="la"><a href="/wiki/duellum#Latin" title="duellum"><b>due</b>llum</a></i> to <i class="Latn mention" lang="la"><a href="/wiki/bellum#Latin" title="bellum"><b>be</b>llum</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">war</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>.
</p>
