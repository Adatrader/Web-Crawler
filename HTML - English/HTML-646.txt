<p><b>Daniel Gurewitch</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ɡ/: &#39;g&#39; in &#39;guy&#39;">ɡ</span><span title="/ɜːr/: &#39;ur&#39; in &#39;fur&#39;">ɜːr</span><span title="&#39;w&#39; in &#39;wind&#39;">w</span><span title="/ɪ/: &#39;i&#39; in &#39;kit&#39;">ɪ</span><span title="/tʃ/: &#39;ch&#39; in &#39;China&#39;">tʃ</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born May 20, 1984) is an <a href="/wiki/Americans" title="Americans">American</a> comedian, writer, and actor. He has worked as a  senior writer for American comedy website <a href="/wiki/CollegeHumor" title="CollegeHumor">CollegeHumor</a>, and as a writer for the <a href="/wiki/Late-night_talk_show" title="Late-night talk show">late-night talk</a> and <a href="/wiki/News_satire" title="News satire">news satire</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Last_Week_Tonight_with_John_Oliver" title="Last Week Tonight with John Oliver">Last Week Tonight with John Oliver</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Passalacqua2015_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Passalacqua2015-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dan_Gurewitch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A former pupil of <a href="/wiki/Suffern_High_School" title="Suffern High School">Suffern High School</a>, Gurewitch graduated from <a href="/wiki/S.I._Newhouse_School_of_Public_Communications" class="mw-redirect" title="S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications">Newhouse</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Syracuse_University" title="Syracuse University">Syracuse University</a> with a degree in television, radio, and film.<sup id="cite_ref-Passalacqua2015_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Passalacqua2015-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 2006 to 2013, Gurewitch worked as a senior writer for the comedy website CollegeHumor. His work there included <i>Hardly Working</i> and <i>CollegeHumor Originals</i>, as well as the MTV comedy series <i>The CollegeHumor Show,</i> in all of which he was a principal cast member.
</p><p>He studied <a href="/wiki/Improvisational_theatre" title="Improvisational theatre">improvisation</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Upright_Citizens_Brigade_Theatre" title="Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre">Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre</a> under <a href="/wiki/Anthony_King_(writer)" title="Anthony King (writer)">Anthony King</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Gethard" title="Chris Gethard">Chris Gethard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Mantzoukas" title="Jason Mantzoukas">Jason Mantzoukas</a>, and Jackie Clarke, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As part of the writing staff for <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a>'s <i>Last Week Tonight with John Oliver</i>, he has won four <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Emmy Awards">Emmy Awards</a> for "Outstanding Writing for a Variety Series,” and five <a href="/wiki/Writers_Guild_of_America_Awards" title="Writers Guild of America Awards">Writers Guild of America Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
