<p><b>Steven Barnes</b> (born March 1, 1952) is an American <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fantasy" title="Fantasy">fantasy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mystery_fiction" title="Mystery fiction">mystery</a> writer. He has written novels, short fiction, screen plays for <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a>, scripts for comic books, animation, newspaper copy, and magazine articles.  
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steven_Barnes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Barnes has written several episodes of <a href="/wiki/The_Outer_Limits_(1995_TV_series)" title="The Outer Limits (1995 TV series)"><i>The Outer Limits</i></a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Baywatch" title="Baywatch">Baywatch</a></i>. His "<a href="/wiki/A_Stitch_in_Time_(The_Outer_Limits)" title="A Stitch in Time (The Outer Limits)">A Stitch In Time</a>" episode of The Outer Limits won an <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a>. He has also written the episode <a href="/wiki/Stargate_SG-1_(season_1)" title="Stargate SG-1 (season 1)">"Brief Candle"</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/Stargate_SG-1" title="Stargate SG-1">Stargate SG-1</a></i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Andromeda_(TV_series)" title="Andromeda (TV series)">Andromeda</a></i> episode <a href="/wiki/List_of_Andromeda_episodes#Season_1" title="List of Andromeda episodes">"The Sum of Its Parts"</a>. Barnes's first published piece of fiction, the 1979 <a href="/wiki/Novella" title="Novella">novelette</a> "The Locusts", was written with <a href="/wiki/Larry_Niven" title="Larry Niven">Larry Niven</a>, and was a <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Award" title="Hugo Award">Hugo Award</a> nominee.<sup id="cite_ref-Barnes&#39;_listing_in_&#39;&#39;Locus&#39;&#39;_magazine&#39;s_database_of_award_nominees_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Barnes&#39;_listing_in_&#39;&#39;Locus&#39;&#39;_magazine&#39;s_database_of_award_nominees-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Excerpts_of_a_March_2003_interview_with_Barnes_in_&#39;&#39;Locus&#39;&#39;_magazine_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Excerpts_of_a_March_2003_interview_with_Barnes_in_&#39;&#39;Locus&#39;&#39;_magazine-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  Barnes subsequently collaborated with Niven on several other books, including two books with both Niven and <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Pournelle" title="Jerry Pournelle">Jerry Pournelle</a>.  Barnes said he clashed politically with the two conservative writers but enjoyed working with them, calling it "a tremendous learning opportunity".<sup id="cite_ref-sh-interview_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sh-interview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Barnes' alternate history novel <i>Lion's Blood</i> won the 2003 <a href="/wiki/Endeavour_Award" title="Endeavour Award">Endeavour Award</a>.  His 2004 <a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars">Star Wars</a> tie-in novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cestus_Deception" title="The Cestus Deception">The Cestus Deception</a></i> was a <a href="/wiki/New_York_Times" class="mw-redirect" title="New York Times">New York Times</a> bestseller.
</p><p>Together with his wife, <a href="/wiki/Tananarive_Due" title="Tananarive Due">Tananarive Due</a>, and actor <a href="/wiki/Blair_Underwood" title="Blair Underwood">Blair Underwood</a>, Barnes won the 2009 NAACP Image Award for outstanding Literary Work - Fiction for <i>In the Night of the Heat: A Tennyson Hardwick Novel</i>. <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steven_Barnes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Barnes, was born on March 1, 1952 in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a>. He has had a varied education, including a secondary education at <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_High_School" title="Los Angeles High School">Los Angeles High School</a>. He continued at <a href="/wiki/Pepperdine_University" title="Pepperdine University">Pepperdine University</a>, majoring in Communication Arts.<sup id="cite_ref-Black_&amp;_White_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Black_&amp;_White-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steven_Barnes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Barnes is married to <a href="/wiki/Tananarive_Due" title="Tananarive Due">Tananarive Due</a>, a writer.<sup id="cite_ref-sh-interview_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sh-interview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The couple live in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> and host a video blog together. They also taught a course together at UCLA called <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://hipshot9.clickfunnels.com/sales-page25582446">The Sunken Place: Racism, Survival &amp; Black Horror</a></i> that experienced a surprise visit from <a href="/wiki/Get_Out" title="Get Out">Get Out</a> director <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Peele" title="Jordan Peele">Jordan Peele</a>. The course is now available as a webinar.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Barnes has a daughter from his first marriage and a son from his current marriage.
</p><p>Barnes is also an avid practitioner of <a href="/wiki/Martial_arts" title="Martial arts">martial and physical arts</a>. He began studying in 1969.<sup id="cite_ref-Black_&amp;_White_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Black_&amp;_White-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He is a <a href="/wiki/Black_belt_(martial_arts)" title="Black belt (martial arts)">black belt</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kenpo" class="mw-redirect" title="Kenpo">Kenpo Karate</a> (BKF style), and <a href="/wiki/Judo" title="Judo">Kodokan Judo</a>. He holds an instructor certificate in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wu_Ming_Ta&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wu Ming Ta (page does not exist)">Wu Ming Ta</a>, and has an instructor candidate ranking in Filipino <a href="/wiki/Eskrima" class="mw-redirect" title="Eskrima">Kali</a> stick and knife fighting. He is an advanced student in <a href="/wiki/Jun_Fan" class="mw-redirect" title="Jun Fan">Jun Fan</a> <a href="/wiki/Kickboxing" title="Kickboxing">kickboxing</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bruce_Lee" title="Bruce Lee">Bruce Lee</a> method under <a href="/wiki/Dan_Inosanto" title="Dan Inosanto">Dan Inosanto</a>), and is an instructor in <a href="/wiki/Wu-style_t%27ai_chi_ch%27uan" title="Wu-style t&#39;ai chi ch&#39;uan">Wu-style</a> <a href="/wiki/T%27ai_chi_ch%27uan" class="mw-redirect" title="T&#39;ai chi ch&#39;uan">t'ai chi ch'uan</a> under <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hawkins_Cheung&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hawkins Cheung (page does not exist)">Hawkins Cheung</a>.
</p><p>He is an intermediate student in self-defense pistol shooting (preferring the <a href="/wiki/Weaver_stance" title="Weaver stance">Turnipseed modified Weaver method</a>). He holds a <a href="/wiki/Red_belt_(martial_arts)" title="Red belt (martial arts)">brown belt</a> in <a href="/wiki/The_Jitsu_Foundation" title="The Jitsu Foundation">Shorenji Jiu Jitsu</a>, and intermediate rankings in <a href="/wiki/Tae_Kwon_Do" class="mw-redirect" title="Tae Kwon Do">Tae Kwon Do</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aikido" title="Aikido">Aikido</a>. He completed the Yoga Works basic <a href="/wiki/Hatha_Yoga" class="mw-redirect" title="Hatha Yoga">Hatha Yoga</a> instructor program; is studying <a href="/wiki/Pentjak_Silat" class="mw-redirect" title="Pentjak Silat">Pentjak Silat</a> (an Indonesian fighting system) with Guru <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stevan_Plinck&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Stevan Plinck (page does not exist)">Stevan Plinck</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ashtanga_Vinyasa_Yoga" class="mw-redirect" title="Ashtanga Vinyasa Yoga">Ashtanga Yoga</a>, an aerobic form of yoga.
</p>
