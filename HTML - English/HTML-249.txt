<p><b>Barbara Strang</b> née <b>Barbara Mary Hope Carr</b> later <b>Lady Strang</b> (20 April 1925 – 11 April 1982) was a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> English language scholar.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbara_Strang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Strang was born in <a href="/wiki/Penge" title="Penge">Penge</a> in 1925 in London. Her parents were Frederick and Amy Carr. Her schooling was affected by the war as her convent school in Croydon was moved to Wales and for her first year at <a href="/wiki/King%27s_College,_London" class="mw-redirect" title="King&#39;s College, London">King's College, London</a> she was attending lectures in Bristol.<sup id="cite_ref-ppp_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ppp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She married Colin Strang who was a lecturer and the heir apparent to <a href="/wiki/William_Strang" title="William Strang">his father's</a> baronetcy. She established her name when her first publication <i>Modern English Structure</i> in 1962 became a standard work. Two years later she became the first Professor of English Language and General Linguistics at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Newcastle_upon_Tyne" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Newcastle upon Tyne">University of Newcastle upon Tyne</a> in 1964. This was the first chair of its kind in England, just as she was one of the first women to hold such a post. It was a novel appointment and her new department established an international reputation.<sup id="cite_ref-ppp_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ppp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1970 she published <i>A History of English</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Strang2017_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Strang2017-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_and_legacy">Death and legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbara_Strang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Death and legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Strang died suddenly of a brain hemorrhage at her home in <a href="/wiki/Morpeth,_Northumberland" title="Morpeth, Northumberland">Morpeth</a> in 1982.<sup id="cite_ref-ppp_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ppp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Her university named the <i>Barbara Strang Teaching Centre</i> after her.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
