<p>This page is for collecting feedback from Wiktionary readers.  It should be cleaned out on a three-month basis, as new comments are constantly being added. Feel free to reply to and discuss comments here, though bear in mind that the people who leave the feedback may never come back to read replies. By convention, the feedback is not archived.

<div style="">
<div style="text-align: center;">Do you have general questions about the Wiktionary? See <a href="/wiki/Help:FAQ" title="Help:FAQ">Help:FAQ</a>.</div>
<p><br clear="all" />
</p>
<ul><li><b>Q. Why don't you provide audio files giving the pronunciations of all entries?</b></li>
<li>A. The recording of audio files requires volunteer editors who have the right equipment and software, and who know how to upload these files to the <a class="external text" href="https://commons.wikimedia.org">Wikimedia Commons</a>. All this is somewhat time-consuming, and it seems that at the moment we simply don't have editors who are able to do this for us regularly. We suggest that you learn how to read the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Phonetic_Alphabet" class="extiw" title="w:International Phonetic Alphabet">International Phonetic Alphabet</a> (IPA) transcriptions of pronunciations. For English entries, you can visit <a href="/wiki/Appendix:English_pronunciation" title="Appendix:English pronunciation">Appendix:English pronunciation</a>, which you can also reach by clicking on the "(key)" link next to the word <i>IPA</i> on entry pages.</li></ul>
<ul><li><b>Q. How do I propose that a Wiktionary for another language be started?</b></li>
<li>A. See <a href="https://incubator.wikimedia.org/wiki/Help:Manual" class="extiw" title="incubator:Help:Manual">incubator:Help:Manual</a>.</li></ul>
<p><br clear="all" />
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Schweinehund"><a href="/wiki/Schweinehund" title="Schweinehund">Schweinehund</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wiktionary:Feedback&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Schweinehund">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Schweinehund has fallen out of general usage and is most commonly used in the mentioned phrase "innerer Schweinehund," usually in a self-deprecating sense. As an insult it has been almost fully superseded by "Sauhund" q.v. <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://en.langenscheidt.com/german-english/sauhund">https://en.langenscheidt.com/german-english/sauhund</a> .

<p>I've been wondering if the spelling of the word LIVE, as used in for example: "the TV show is LIVE," couldn't be changed to something less confusing. Such as LYVE, so as not to be confused with live, as in: "I live in Florida." Just a thought. Glynn A.
</p>
<dl><dd>We're a descriptive dictionary- we describe usage, we don't dictate it. Until actual speakers use that spelling, we can't include it. Besides which, any living language that's complex enough to use for everything is going to have parts that aren't logical. It's just something you have to allow for. <a href="/wiki/User:Chuck_Entz" title="User:Chuck Entz">Chuck Entz</a> (<a href="/wiki/User_talk:Chuck_Entz" title="User talk:Chuck Entz">talk</a>) 18:00, 1 February 2020 (UTC)</dd></dl>
