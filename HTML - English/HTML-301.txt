<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Old_French">Old French</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=provoire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Old French">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=provoire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From earlier <i class="Latn mention" lang="fro"><a href="/w/index.php?title=prevoire&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="prevoire (page does not exist)">prevoire</a></i>, from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vulgar_Latin" class="extiw" title="w:Vulgar Latin">Vulgar Latin</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">*previterum</i>, <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">*prebiterum</i>, from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin" class="extiw" title="w:Latin">Latin</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="la"><a href="/wiki/presbyterum#Latin" title="presbyterum">presbyterum</a></i>, accusative of <i class="Latn mention" lang="la"><a href="/wiki/presbyter#Latin" title="presbyter">presbyter</a></i> (cf. the synonym <i class="Latn mention" lang="fro"><a href="/wiki/prestre#Old_French" title="prestre">prestre</a></i> derived from the nominative form). Compare <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occitan_language" class="extiw" title="w:Occitan language">Occitan</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="oc"><a href="/w/index.php?title=preveire&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="preveire (page does not exist)">preveire</a></i>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=provoire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Noun">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="fro">provoire</strong>&#160;<span class="gender"><abbr title="masculine gender">m</abbr></span> (<i>oblique plural</i> <b class="Latn" lang="fro"><a href="/wiki/provoires#Old_French" title="provoires">provoires</a></b>, <i>nominative singular</i> <b class="Latn" lang="fro"><a href="/wiki/provoires#Old_French" title="provoires">provoires</a></b>, <i>nominative plural</i> <b class="Latn" lang="fro"><strong class="selflink">provoire</strong></b>)
</p>
<ol><li><a href="/wiki/priest" title="priest">priest</a></li></ol>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_notes">Usage notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=provoire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Usage notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>According to some sources, <a href="/wiki/prestre" title="prestre">prestre</a> and <a class="mw-selflink selflink">provoire</a> are inflected forms of the same word. This is however unlikely, as the two rarely appear in texts together
</p>
