<p>Wikivoyage uses a <b>copyleft</b> license for all content on the website. <b>Anyone can use</b> Wikivoyage content according to the terms of the <b>Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike</b> license. And <b>anyone can contribute</b> their own or other people's content, as long as the work is their own or available under a compatible license.
</p><p>It is very important that you <b>understand how copyleft works before you contribute to Wikivoyage</b>.  If you find your work reused by someone else in a manner that you didn't consider, or if you stop using Wikivoyage for any reason, it is not possible to change the license for the work you have done. Although you always own the copyright in your work, that does not give you the right to change its license retroactively.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Licensing">Licensing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikivoyage:Copyleft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Licensing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>If this seems confusing, <big><b>skip down to <a href="#Frequently_asked_questions">the FAQ</a>.</b></big></i>
</p><p>All <b>written contributions</b> to Wikivoyage are and must be licensed under Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0 (<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/3.0/legalcode">CC-by-SA 3.0</a>) or a compatible license (e.g., Public Domain, CC-by-SA any, dual CC-by-SA/GFDL any, etc.). Consequently, all redistributed and derivative works of Wikivoyage text must also be licensed as CC-by-SA 3.0.
</p><p><b>Images</b> almost always must be uploaded to <a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Main_page" class="extiw" title="commons:Main page">Wikimedia Commons</a>, and images' individual licensing is described on individual image pages. Any images hosted locally on Wikivoyage comply with our <a href="/wiki/Wikivoyage:Non-free_content" title="Wikivoyage:Non-free content">Wikivoyage:Non-free content</a>, meaning that they do have a license compatible with our copyleft, but are derivative works of something <i>not</i> free, like a work of art. As with text contributions, all files uploaded here must have a license that allows derivative works and commercial re-use (usually CC-by or CC-by-SA, never CC-No Derivatives or CC-Non-Commercial)—see <a href="/wiki/Wikivoyage:Non-free_content#Exemption_Doctrine_Policy" title="Wikivoyage:Non-free content">Wikivoyage:Non-free content#Exemption Doctrine Policy</a> for more information.
</p><p>All redistributed and derivative works including Wikivoyage images and other files must provide file-specific attribution and licensing information, available on the individual file page.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Things_for_users_to_know">Things for users to know</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikivoyage:Copyleft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Things for users to know">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Copyleft</b> means that every single author, editor, illustrator, mapmaker, factchecker and photographer who puts their work into Wikivoyage gives you the right to read, copy, print, save, download, read aloud, project, modify, email, distribute, sell, photocopy and correct their work however you want to.
</p><p>The only restrictions are that:
</p>
<ul><li><i>if you distribute it, or derive new work from it</i>
<ul><li>you must give <b>attribution</b> to the creators</li>
<li>you must retain the work and any derivative works <b>under the same licensing</b>.</li></ul></li></ul>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cc-by_new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Cc-by_new.svg/150px-Cc-by_new.svg.png" decoding="async" width="150" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Cc-by_new.svg/225px-Cc-by_new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Cc-by_new.svg/300px-Cc-by_new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="80" data-file-height="80" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cc-by_new.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Attribution</div></div></div>
<p>In other words, we give you practically every freedom within the boundaries of the law to use the information in Wikivoyage, except the freedom to take that freedom away from others.
</p><p>The license we use is the <b>Attribution-ShareAlike</b> license. It's a piece of legal documentation created by the great people at <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.creativecommons.org/">Creative Commons</a> that basically says the same thing as above: give Wikivoyagers credit for their work, and share with other people.
</p><p>If you want you can read the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/3.0/legalcode">full text of the Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0 license</a>. For the purposes of the License, a <i>Work</i> is the formatted text that makes up a single Wikivoyage article. Each image individually is also a <i>Work</i> for the purposes of the License.
</p><p>In addition, there may be other restrictions on use or distribution based on law in your country or state; in particular, consider <b>privacy rights</b> for <a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Commons:Photographs_of_identifiable_people" class="extiw" title="commons:Commons:Photographs of identifiable people">photographs of identifiable people</a> and derivative works for <a href="/wiki/Wikivoyage:Image_policy#Buildings_and_artworks_in_photos" title="Wikivoyage:Image policy">photographs of buildings and artworks</a>.
</p><p>Lastly, there is <big><i><b>no warranty</b></i></big> on any of the articles or images in Wikivoyage. Hotels close; bars raise prices; train schedules change; earthquakes, mudslides, wars and lightning strikes destroy sights, statues and cities. People make mistakes. Wikivoyagers try their best, but none of the creators of these pages are responsible to you in any way for any injury or discomfort you endure before, after, or during your travels. See the License above for details.
</p><p>If you want to reuse any of our travel guides, whether to build a mirror site, distribute travel information for your city or even build some new gee-whiz travel application, you might want to check out <a href="/wiki/Wikivoyage:How_to_re-use_Wikivoyage_guides" title="Wikivoyage:How to re-use Wikivoyage guides">Wikivoyage:How to re-use Wikivoyage guides</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Things_for_contributors_to_know">Things for contributors to know</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikivoyage:Copyleft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Things for contributors to know">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cc-sa_white.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Cc-sa_white.svg/150px-Cc-sa_white.svg.png" decoding="async" width="150" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Cc-sa_white.svg/225px-Cc-sa_white.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Cc-sa_white.svg/300px-Cc-sa_white.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="64" data-file-height="64" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cc-sa_white.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Share-Alike</div></div></div>
<p><b>Copyleft</b> means that every single yahoo, nutcase, screwjob, charlatan, shyster, weirdo, freakazoid, mouthbreather, goofball, lamer, cheater and jerk will have the right to read, copy, print, save, download, read aloud, project, modify, email, distribute, sell, photocopy and correct your work however they want to.
</p><p><i>In particular</i>, your work can be ruthlessly modified, edited, or cut from Wikivoyage altogether by other <a href="/wiki/Wikivoyage:About#Wikivoyagers" title="Wikivoyage:About">Wikivoyagers</a>. It can be photocopied thousands of times and passed around as flyers by itinerant backpackers. It can be put in Hollywood movies, and it can be projected onto a screen at an outdoor rave. It can be used for commercial ventures, advertisements, or other purposes (with some restrictions) without your direct control.
</p><p><i>In return for your incredible generosity</i>, you will have the knowledge that you are helping travellers all over the world, and that all copies made of your work will be attributed to you, and that everyone who derives new works from yours will have to be just as generous as you are. And, of course, you get to use all the other work in Wikivoyage shared the same way; but you don't need to contribute to do that.
</p><p>You'll also get the pleasure of collaborating with hundreds of other <a href="/wiki/Wikivoyage:About#Wikivoyagers" title="Wikivoyage:About">Wikivoyagers</a> just like yourself. You'll get free spellcheck service from nit-picky folks you've never met. You'll work together with people you don't know to make better pictures, text, and maps than you've ever dreamed of. You still retain the copyright over your own work, and you can use it any other way you want (even if it's not compatible with our license!).  By writing on Wikivoyage, or uploading photos, you're using a license under which all our work is available - making it simpler for everyone.
</p><p>Now, the hard part: because of the Attribution-ShareAlike license, you are legally responsible <b>not to put other people's copyrighted work into Wikivoyage without permission.</b> You are responsible for getting <b>model releases</b> from people you take pictures of, and you are responsible for any <b>libel</b> you include in your articles.
</p><p>Before editing Wikivoyage articles or uploading pictures, you should read very carefully the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/3.0/legalcode">full text of the Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0 license</a>, and make sure that you agree to it. 
</p><p>We want to make the best possible travel guide, with the most brilliant writing, the most up-to-date info, the most awe-inspiring photos, the most detailed and readable maps. But no one item is worth putting yourself and this project in legal jeopardy. If you have any questions about the source of something you want to put in Wikivoyage, <a href="/wiki/Wikivoyage:Using_talk_pages" title="Wikivoyage:Using talk pages">ask about it first</a>. <b>When in doubt, leave it out.</b>
</p>

<h3><span id="What_do_I_need_to_do_to_get_writing.3F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="What_do_I_need_to_do_to_get_writing?">What do I need to do to get writing?</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikivoyage:Copyleft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: What do I need to do to get writing?">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Nothing!</i> Just <a href="/wiki/Wikivoyage:Plunge_forward" title="Wikivoyage:Plunge forward">plunge forward!</a> Whenever you save something you write, you automatically license it for use under CC-by-SA 3.0. So all you need to do is get started writing&#8212;if the text is your own original writing, don't worry about licenses.
</p>
<h3><span id="What_do_I_need_to_do_to_get_uploading_images.3F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="What_do_I_need_to_do_to_get_uploading_images?">What do I need to do to get uploading images?</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikivoyage:Copyleft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: What do I need to do to get uploading images?">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>First, be sure that the image is either one created by you or one that is freely licensed (see "Can I copy text and other content to Wikivoyage from other sites?" below).  Then go ahead and upload it to <a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:UploadWizard" class="extiw" title="commons:Special:UploadWizard">Wikimedia Commons</a>, our central image repository. 
</p><p>If your image is a photo of a work of art or architecture, you <i>may</i> need to upload it locally under our <a href="/wiki/Wikivoyage:Non-free_content#Exemption_Doctrine_Policy" title="Wikivoyage:Non-free content">Exemption Doctrine</a>. Check <a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Freedom_of_Panorama" class="extiw" title="commons:Freedom of Panorama">Commons:Freedom of Panorama</a> to see whether the laws of the country where the photo was taken allow derivative works of publicly displayed art or architecture. If the image cannot be uploaded to Commons because of Freedom of Panorama issues, upload it here (but otherwise never upload anything here!). If you have no idea what this means, feel free to ask <a href="/wiki/Wikivoyage:Administrators#Current_administrators" title="Wikivoyage:Administrators">an administrator</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="How_will_I_be_attributed.3F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="How_will_I_be_attributed?">How will I be attributed?</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikivoyage:Copyleft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: How will I be attributed?">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>You will only be attributed for your work if you register a user account. You will be attributed by your user name, which appears in article history and credits pages. For an example, take a look at the history of this page <a class="external autonumber" href="https://en.wikivoyage.org/w/index.php?title=Wikivoyage:Copyleft&amp;action=history">[1]</a>. Re-users will have to provide attribution as well—online re-users will usually link back to the article being re-used, while offline re-users will simply state the source. 
</p><p>For files you upload, you must provide the attribution information yourself in the upload summary. So, just indicate however you want to be attributed as you upload the file.
</p>
<h3><span id="Can_I_copy_text_and_other_content_to_Wikivoyage_from_other_sites.3F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Can_I_copy_text_and_other_content_to_Wikivoyage_from_other_sites?">Can I copy text and other content to Wikivoyage from other sites?</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikivoyage:Copyleft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Can I copy text and other content to Wikivoyage from other sites?">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Usually No</b>. That is, unless you are the original author of the text, or the other website has been <i>explicitly</i> licensed as compatible with Creative Commons Attribution/Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0, or it is in the public domain.
</p><p>If you are the original author of the text, note that on the article talk page, or else someone will likely remove it as a copyright violation.
</p><p>Wikimedia projects like <b>Wikipedia</b> have a compatible license, so we <i>can</i> copy relevant text <b>as long as proper attribution is provided in accordance with the CC-SA license</b>.  When copying text from another site with a compatible license, you must cite the article from which you are copying in the edit summary and include a link to the license terms.
</p><p>For the content to be public domain, it needs to be created by the U.S. Federal Government, the city government of Washington, D.C., or have a statement on the website saying it is released into the public domain. That great picture you took of the <a href="/wiki/RMS_Titanic" title="RMS Titanic">RMS Titanic</a> leaving port is likely also safe for anyone to use; copyright does eventually expire (depending on date and place of publication) and an author is free to release their own work to the public domain at any time.
</p><p>Lack of a copyright notice does not mean the content is free to re-use—all online content is automatically copyrighted unless the author explicitly states otherwise.
</p>
<h3><span id="If_content_is_available_free-of-charge.2C_can_it_be_used_here.3F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="If_content_is_available_free-of-charge,_can_it_be_used_here?">If content is available free-of-charge, can it be used here?</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikivoyage:Copyleft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: If content is available free-of-charge, can it be used here?">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Probably not. Just because a map, brochure, or other item is given away for free does not mean that it has been freely <i>licensed</i>. For content to be usable on Wikivoyage the content copyright owner <b>must</b> agree to license it under the CC-SA 3.0 or a compatible license.
</p>
<h3><span id="How_should_I_ask_for_permission_to_use_a_copyrighted_work_on_Wikivoyage.3F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="How_should_I_ask_for_permission_to_use_a_copyrighted_work_on_Wikivoyage?">How should I ask for permission to use a copyrighted work on Wikivoyage?</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikivoyage:Copyleft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: How should I ask for permission to use a copyrighted work on Wikivoyage?">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The following template can help. Please quote your original message and reply to it at the respective article's Talk page. You may provide attribution to the original author(s) listing them by a template like <a href="/wiki/Template:Attribution" title="Template:Attribution">Template:Attribution</a>.
</p>
<dl><dd><i>Hello (person name).</i></dd>
<dd><i>Do you mind if I use some of your work at &lt;work name&gt; to contribute to an article on &lt;destination&gt; at Wikivoyage, a free online travel guide written and edited entirely by travelers from around the globe?</i></dd>
<dd><i>The current version of the article I am going to update is here: &lt;article permalink URL&gt;.</i></dd>
<dd><i>Wikivoyage requires all of its content to be licensed under Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0 license or another suitably compatible copyleft license. Basically this means that your content:</i></dd>
<dd><i>- can be distributed by others, even for commercial purposes, as long as you are credited as the author</i></dd>
<dd><i>- may be further modified by other contributors</i></dd>
<dd><i>So do you mind if I use your work for Wikivoyage?</i></dd>
<dd><i>&lt;your signature&gt;</i></dd></dl>
<p>You may also use the following template, which you need the copyright holder to clearly acknowledge:
</p>
<div style="border-style: solid; padding:5px; font-size:100%;">
<p>To: <big><b>info@wikivoyage.org</b></big>
</p>
<hr />
<p>I hereby affirm that <span style="background-color:#b8b8b8"><b>CHOOSE ONE: [</b>I, (name here), am<b>] OR [</b>(copyright holder's name) is<b>]</b></span> the creator and/or sole owner of the exclusive copyright of <span style="background-color:#b8b8b8"><b>[SPECIFY THE WORK HERE - describe the work to be released in detail, attach the work to the email, or give the URL of the work if online]</b></span>
</p><p>I agree to <span style="background-color:#b8b8b8"><b>STANDARD CHOICE; SEE BELOW FOR MORE INFORMATION ON TYPE OF LICENSE: [</b>publish that work under the free license "Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0 Unported" and GNU Free Documentation License (unversioned, with no invariant sections, front-cover texts, or back-cover texts).<b>]</b></span>
</p><p>I acknowledge that by doing so I grant anyone the right to use the work in a commercial product or otherwise, and to modify it according to their needs, provided that they abide by the terms of the license and any other applicable laws.
</p><p>I am aware that this agreement is not limited to Wikivoyage or related sites.
</p><p>I am aware that I always retain copyright of my work, and retain the right to be attributed in accordance with the license chosen. Modifications others make to the work will not be claimed to have been made by me.
</p><p>I acknowledge that I cannot withdraw this agreement, and that the content may or may not be kept permanently on a Wikimedia project. 
</p><p><span style="background-color:#b8b8b8"><b>[SENDER'S NAME AND DETAILS (to allow future verification of authenticity)]</b><br />
<b>[SENDER'S AUTHORITY (Are you the copyright-holder, director, appointed representative of, etc.)]</b><br />
<b>[DATE]</b></span>
</p>
