<p><b>Tim Carvell</b> is an American writer and television producer known for his work on the TV satirical news series <i><a href="/wiki/Last_Week_Tonight_with_John_Oliver" title="Last Week Tonight with John Oliver">Last Week Tonight with John Oliver</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Show_with_Jon_Stewart" class="mw-redirect" title="The Daily Show with Jon Stewart">The Daily Show with Jon Stewart</a></i>, as well as for his print work in publications including <i><a href="/wiki/Mad_(magazine)" title="Mad (magazine)">Mad</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Slate_(magazine)" title="Slate (magazine)">Slate</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tim_Carvell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For the first nine years after he graduated from college, Carvell wrote for a number of New York-based publications, including <i><a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)">Fortune</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sports_Illustrated_for_Women" title="Sports Illustrated for Women">Sports Illustrated for Women</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He also contributed humor pieces to <a href="/wiki/Timothy_McSweeney%27s_Quarterly_Concern" title="Timothy McSweeney&#39;s Quarterly Concern"><i>McSweeney's Quarterly Concern</i></a><sup id="cite_ref-CCToday_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CCToday-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and the op-ed page of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-CCToday_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CCToday-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Carvell stumbled into a comedy career "by accident".<sup id="cite_ref-CCToday_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CCToday-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He heard about an opening on the TV comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Show_with_Jon_Stewart" class="mw-redirect" title="The Daily Show with Jon Stewart">The Daily Show with Jon Stewart</a></i> from Steve Bodow, one of the writers on that show. He submitted a writing sample that consisted of the headlines that <a href="/wiki/Jon_Stewart" title="Jon Stewart">Jon Stewart</a> reads at the desk and dialogue between Stewart and correspondents. Carvell was then interviewed by the show's head writer and the executive producer. He joined the show in March 2004, just as the <a href="/wiki/2004_U.S._presidential_campaign" class="mw-redirect" title="2004 U.S. presidential campaign">2004 U.S. presidential campaign</a> was becoming a common news topic. Describing the subsequent meeting with Stewart as "surreal", Carvell says, "it felt uncannily like I was suddenly a guest on the show, only without a book or movie to promote."<sup id="cite_ref-CCToday_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CCToday-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, Carvell became the series' head writer.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> As a staff writer for the show, he won six <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Emmy Awards">Emmy Awards</a> between 2004–2012.<sup id="cite_ref-CCToday_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CCToday-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since 2005, he has written the "Planet Tad" column for <i><a href="/wiki/Mad_(magazine)" title="Mad (magazine)">Mad</a></i> magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, he wrote a book based on the column, also called <i><a href="/wiki/Planet_Tad_(novel)" class="mw-redirect" title="Planet Tad (novel)">Planet Tad</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, Carvell followed <a href="/wiki/John_Oliver_(comedian)" class="mw-redirect" title="John Oliver (comedian)">John Oliver</a> to his new <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Last_Week_Tonight_with_John_Oliver" title="Last Week Tonight with John Oliver">Last Week Tonight with John Oliver</a></i>, where he serves as showrunner for the series.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tim_Carvell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tim Carvell is a native of <a href="/wiki/Bloomfield_Hills" class="mw-redirect" title="Bloomfield Hills">Bloomfield Hills</a>, a suburb of <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a>. He entered <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a> in 1991, majoring in history. While there, he wrote news and features for <i>Spectator</i> which he described as "as good a journalism education as you could get. It allowed you to learn by making mistakes, without suffering real consequences." He graduated in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-CCToday_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CCToday-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Carvell married his partner, Thomas Keeton, on June 28, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
