<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Phonetic_realization">Phonetic realization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slovak_phonology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Phonetic realization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul><li>The close and mid vowels are typically peripheral, but they can at times be somewhat centralized.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPavlík200493–95_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPavlík200493–95-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/i, iː, u, uː/</span> are typically near-close <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[<a href="/wiki/Near-close_front_unrounded_vowel" title="Near-close front unrounded vowel">i̞</a>, <a href="/wiki/Near-close_front_unrounded_vowel" title="Near-close front unrounded vowel">i̞ː</a>, <a href="/wiki/Near-close_back_rounded_vowel" title="Near-close back rounded vowel">u̞</a>, <a href="/wiki/Near-close_back_rounded_vowel" title="Near-close back rounded vowel">u̞ː</a>]</span>, but sometimes they can be close <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[<a href="/wiki/Close_front_unrounded_vowel" title="Close front unrounded vowel">i</a>, <a href="/wiki/Close_front_unrounded_vowel" title="Close front unrounded vowel">iː</a>, <a href="/wiki/Close_back_rounded_vowel" title="Close back rounded vowel">u</a>, <a href="/wiki/Close_back_rounded_vowel" title="Close back rounded vowel">uː</a>]</span> instead.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPavlík200493,_95_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPavlík200493,_95-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɛ, ɛː, ɔ, ɔː/</span> are typically mid <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[<a href="/wiki/Mid_front_unrounded_vowel" class="mw-redirect" title="Mid front unrounded vowel">ɛ̝</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mid_front_unrounded_vowel" class="mw-redirect" title="Mid front unrounded vowel">ɛ̝ː</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mid_back_rounded_vowel" title="Mid back rounded vowel">ɔ̝</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mid_back_rounded_vowel" title="Mid back rounded vowel">ɔ̝ː</a>]</span>. <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɛ, ɛː/</span> are slightly higher than <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɔ, ɔː/</span> - this can be transcribed in narrow transcription as <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[<a href="/wiki/Mid_front_unrounded_vowel" class="mw-redirect" title="Mid front unrounded vowel">e̞</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mid_front_unrounded_vowel" class="mw-redirect" title="Mid front unrounded vowel">e̞ː</a>]</span> vs. <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[<a href="/wiki/Mid_back_rounded_vowel" title="Mid back rounded vowel">ɔ̝</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mid_back_rounded_vowel" title="Mid back rounded vowel">ɔ̝ː</a>]</span>. At times, <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɛ, ɛː/</span> can be as close as close-mid <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[<a href="/wiki/Close-mid_front_unrounded_vowel" title="Close-mid front unrounded vowel">e</a>, <a href="/wiki/Close-mid_front_unrounded_vowel" title="Close-mid front unrounded vowel">eː</a>]</span> and <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɔ, ɔː/</span> as open as open-mid <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[<a href="/wiki/Open-mid_back_rounded_vowel" title="Open-mid back rounded vowel">ɔ</a>, <a href="/wiki/Open-mid_back_rounded_vowel" title="Open-mid back rounded vowel">ɔː</a>]</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPavlík200494–95_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPavlík200494–95-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Speakers often fail at attempts to pronounce <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/æ/</span>, pronouncing a vowel that is phonetically too close to either <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɛ/</span> or <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/a/</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKráľ198855_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKráľ198855-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/a, aː/</span> are typically open central <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[<a href="/wiki/Open_central_unrounded_vowel" title="Open central unrounded vowel">ä</a>, <a href="/wiki/Open_central_unrounded_vowel" title="Open central unrounded vowel">äː</a>]</span>, but at times they can be somewhat fronted <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[<a href="/wiki/Open_front_unrounded_vowel" title="Open front unrounded vowel">a̠</a>, <a href="/wiki/Open_front_unrounded_vowel" title="Open front unrounded vowel">a̠ː</a>]</span>, retracted <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[<a href="/wiki/Open_back_unrounded_vowel" title="Open back unrounded vowel">ɑ̟</a>, <a href="/wiki/Open_back_unrounded_vowel" title="Open back unrounded vowel">ɑ̟ː</a>]</span> or raised <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[<a href="/wiki/Near-open_central_vowel" title="Near-open central vowel">ɐ</a>, <a href="/wiki/Near-open_central_vowel" title="Near-open central vowel">ɐː</a>]</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPavlík200494–95_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPavlík200494–95-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Under Hungarian influence, some speakers realize <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɛː, ɔː/</span> as close-mid <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[<a href="/wiki/Close-mid_front_unrounded_vowel" title="Close-mid front unrounded vowel">eː</a>, <a href="/wiki/Close-mid_back_rounded_vowel" title="Close-mid back rounded vowel">oː</a>]</span> and <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/a/</span> as open back rounded <span class="IPA nowrap" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">&#91;<a href="/wiki/Open_back_rounded_vowel" title="Open back rounded vowel">ɒ</a>&#93;</span>. The close-mid realizations of <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɛː, ɔː/</span> occur also in southern dialects spoken near the river <a href="/wiki/Ipe%C4%BE" title="Ipeľ">Ipeľ</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKráľ198854,_92_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKráľ198854,_92-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_notes">Other notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slovak_phonology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul><li>Vowel length is not phonemic in Eastern dialects,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHanulíkováHamann2010375_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHanulíkováHamann2010375-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> which have only five vowel phonemes (<span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/i, u, ɛ, ɔ, a/</span>, some speakers also have <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/æ/</span>).</li>
<li>In Western dialects, vowels that are short due to the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_orthography#Rhythmical_rule" title="Slovak orthography">rhythmical rule</a> are often realized as long, and thus violating the rule.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHanulíkováHamann2010376_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHanulíkováHamann2010376-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/y, yː, œ, œː, ɔː/</span> occur only in loanwords.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHanulíkováHamann2010375_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHanulíkováHamann2010375-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKráľ198864–65_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKráľ198864–65-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Just as other mid vowels, <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/œ, œː/</span> are phonetically true-mid <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[<a href="/wiki/Mid_front_rounded_vowel" title="Mid front rounded vowel">œ̝</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mid_front_rounded_vowel" title="Mid front rounded vowel">œ̝ː</a>]</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKráľ198864_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKráľ198864-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Among these vowels, only <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɔː/</span> is consistently realized in the correct manner, whereas the occurrence of the front rounded vowels <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/y, yː, œ, œː/</span> has been reported only by <a href="#CITEREFKráľ1988">Kráľ (1988)</a>, who states that the front rounded vowels appear only in the high register and medium register. However, in the medium register, <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/y, yː/</span> and <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/œ, œː/</span> are often either too back, which results in realizations that are phonetically too close to, respectively, <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/u, uː/</span> and <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɔ, ɔː/</span>, or too weakly rounded, yielding vowels that are phonetically too close to, respectively, <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/i, iː/</span> and <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɛ, ɛː/</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKráľ198857,_64–65,_103_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKráľ198857,_64–65,_103-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/uː, ɛː/</span> do not occur after soft consonants, where they are replaced by the corresponding diphthongs <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɪ̯u, ɪ̯ɛ/</span>. The same is generally true for <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/aː/</span> (<span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɪ̯a/</span> after soft consonants), but the sequence <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/jaː/</span> may occur in some cases.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEShort2002534–535_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEShort2002534–535-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Long <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɛː/</span> occurs only in loanwords, one native word (<i>dcéra</i>) and in adjective endings.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEShort2002535_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEShort2002535-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/æ/</span> can only be short, and occurs only after <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/m, p, b, v/</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHanulíkováHamann2010375_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHanulíkováHamann2010375-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKráľ198855_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKráľ198855-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> There is not a full agreement about its status in the standard language:
<ul><li><a href="#CITEREFKráľ1988">Kráľ (1988)</a> states that the correct pronunciation of <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/æ/</span> is an important part of the high register, but in medium and low registers, <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/æ/</span> merges with <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɛ/</span>, or, in some cases, with <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/a/</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKráľ198855_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKráľ198855-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="#CITEREFShort2002">Short (2002)</a> states that only about 5% of speakers have <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/æ/</span> as a distinct phoneme, and that even when it is used in formal contexts, it is most often a dialect feature.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEShort2002534_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEShort2002534-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="#CITEREFHanulíkováHamann2010">Hanulíková &amp; Hamann (2010)</a> state that the use of <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/æ/</span> is becoming rare, and that it often merges with <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɛ/</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHanulíkováHamann2010375_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHanulíkováHamann2010375-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li></ul></li></ul>

<ul><li>All of the diphthongs are rising, i.e. their second elements have more prominence.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHanulíkováHamann2010376_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHanulíkováHamann2010376-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPavlík200495_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPavlík200495-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The phonetic quality of Slovak diphthongs is as follows:
<ul><li><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɪ̯ɛ/</span> is typically a glide from <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/i/</span> to <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɛ/</span> (<span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[ȋ̞ɛ̝]</span>).<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPavlík200496–97_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPavlík200496–97-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɪ̯a/</span> is typically a glide from the position below <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/i/</span> to <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/æ/</span> (<span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[ȇ̝æ̠]</span>).<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPavlík200495,_97_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPavlík200495,_97-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɪ̯u/</span> is typically a glide from <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/i/</span> to the position more front than <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/u/</span> (<span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[ȋ̞ʊ]</span>).<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPavlík200496–97_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPavlík200496–97-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ʊ̯ɔ/</span> is typically a glide from <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/u/</span> to the position above <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ɔ/</span> (<span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[ȗ̞o̞]</span>).<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPavlík200496–97_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPavlík200496–97-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li></ul></li>
<li>There are many more phonetic diphthongs, such as <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[aʊ̯]</span> in <i>Miroslav</i> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[ˈmirɔslaʊ̯]</span> and <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[ɔʊ̯]</span> in <i>Prešov</i> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[ˈprɛʃɔʊ̯]</span>. Phonemically, these are interpreted as sequences of <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/v/</span> preceded by a vowel. This <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[ʊ̯]</span> is phonetically <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[ȗ̞]</span> and it is very similar to the first element of <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/ʊ̯ɔ/</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPavlík2004105_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPavlík2004105-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHanulíkováHamann2010374_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHanulíkováHamann2010374-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<p>Some additional notes includes the following (transcriptions in IPA unless otherwise stated):
</p>
<ul><li><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/r, l/</span> can be syllabic: <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/r̩, l̩/</span>. <li><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">/n/</span> in front of (post)alveolar fricatives has a postalveolar allophone <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[n̠]</span>.</li>

<p>In the standard language, the <a href="/wiki/Stress_(linguistics)" title="Stress (linguistics)">stress</a> is always on the first syllable of a word (or on the preceding preposition, see below). This is not the case in certain dialects. Eastern dialects have penultimate stress (as in <a href="/wiki/Polish_phonology" title="Polish phonology">Polish</a>), which at times makes them difficult to understand for speakers of standard Slovak. Some of the north-central dialects have a weak stress on the first syllable, which becomes stronger and moves to the penultimate in certain cases. Monosyllabic conjunctions, monosyllabic short personal pronouns and auxiliary verb forms of the verb <i>byť</i> (to be) are usually unstressed.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Preposition" class="mw-redirect" title="Preposition">Prepositions</a> form a single <a href="/wiki/Prosody_(linguistics)" title="Prosody (linguistics)">prosodic</a> unit with the following word, unless the word is long (four syllables or more) or the preposition stands at the beginning of a sentence.

<p>Slovak linguists do not usually use IPA for phonetic transcription of their own language or others, but have their own system based on the Slovak alphabet. Many English language textbooks make use of this  alternative transcription system.
In the following table, pronunciation of each grapheme is given in this system as well as in the IPA.
