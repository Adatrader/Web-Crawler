	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Roger Bennett (journalist)</h1>

<p><b>Roger James Bennett</b> (born 19 September 1970) is a broadcaster, podcaster, and filmmaker, who has, through <a href="/wiki/Men_in_Blazers" title="Men in Blazers">Men in Blazers</a>, become one of the most prominent football broadcasters in the United States.  Along with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Davies_(television_producer)" title="Michael Davies (television producer)">Michael Davies</a>, Men in Blazers has turned a weekly Premier League podcast into a popular television show on NBCSN and digital content brand covering multiple sports including women’s soccer, golf, and the NHL.The duo have written a New York Times Best Selling book, released clothing collaborations with a slew of designers, and travelled the nation on sold-out live tours celebrating soccer, which they call “America’s Sport of the Future… as it has been since 1972.”  
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Bennett_(journalist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Men_in_Blazers">Men in Blazers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Bennett_(journalist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Men in Blazers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Men_in_Blazers" title="Men in Blazers">Men in Blazers</a></div>
<p>The podcast began on the <a href="/wiki/Grantland" title="Grantland">Grantland</a> network in 2011 and quickly established itself as the most popular football podcast in North America.  Regular guests included <a href="/wiki/Billy_Beane" title="Billy Beane">Billy Beane</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Oliver" title="John Oliver">John Oliver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Luck" title="Andrew Luck">Andrew Luck</a>, and bands like <a href="/wiki/The_National_(band)" title="The National (band)">The National (band)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vampire_Weekend" title="Vampire Weekend">Vampire Weekend</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mumford_and_Sons" class="mw-redirect" title="Mumford and Sons">Mumford and Sons</a>, all of whom would come on to discuss their footballing passions.
</p><p>After becoming a cult hit on <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2014_World_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 World Cup">2014 World Cup</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> the duo signed with NBCSN to create a weekly half-hour television show "Men in Blazers" which covers the <a href="/wiki/English_Premier_League" class="mw-redirect" title="English Premier League">English Premier League</a>. It debuted on 15 September 2014, and is filmed in a studio in the SoHo area of New York City, which Bennett refers to as “The Panic Room.”
</p><p>The show celebrates Premier League storylines week to week. Bennett interviews a celebrity in the final third of each episode. His guests have included <a href="/wiki/Will_Ferrell" title="Will Ferrell">Will Ferrell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laura_Linney" title="Laura Linney">Laura Linney</a>, <a href="/wiki/DeAndre_Hopkins" title="DeAndre Hopkins">DeAndre Hopkins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Killer_Mike" title="Killer Mike">Killer Mike</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rory_McIlroy" title="Rory McIlroy">Rory McIlroy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diplo" title="Diplo">Diplo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Alex_Morgan" title="Alex Morgan">Alex Morgan</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_Sports">Other Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Bennett_(journalist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The popularity of the Men In Blazers football coverage has empowered them to move into other sports they love. Along with his production partner, Jonathan Williamson, Bennett and Men In Blazers have created content on NFL,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> golf, where they cover the British Open and The Presidents Cup, as well as The NHL via Men In Blazers on Ice through which Bennett interviewed <a href="/wiki/Alex_Ovechkin" class="mw-redirect" title="Alex Ovechkin">Alex Ovechkin</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/PK_Subban" class="mw-redirect" title="PK Subban">PK Subban</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_Tours">Live Tours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Bennett_(journalist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Live Tours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Every summer, Bennett and Davies tour the nation playing a stream of live tours which bring together footballing loving Americans in a theater setting with guests including <a href="/wiki/Waka_Flocka" class="mw-redirect" title="Waka Flocka">Waka Flocka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sam_Darnold" title="Sam Darnold">Sam Darnold</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Julie_Ertz" title="Julie Ertz">Julie Ertz</a>.  The tours were the subject of a "Real Sports" feature on HBO.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Films">Films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Bennett_(journalist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Thanks to the popularity of Men In Blazers, Bennett has been invited by numerous teams to film with their managers and players.  He has created television specials with <a href="/wiki/Jose_Mourinho" class="mw-redirect" title="Jose Mourinho">Jose Mourinho</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pep_Guardiola" title="Pep Guardiola">Pep Guardiola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jurgen_Klopp" class="mw-redirect" title="Jurgen Klopp">Jurgen Klopp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Kerr" title="Steve Kerr">Steve Kerr</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arsene_Wenger" class="mw-redirect" title="Arsene Wenger">Arsene Wenger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mauricio_Pochettino" title="Mauricio Pochettino">Mauricio Pochettino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_De_Bruyne" title="Kevin De Bruyne">Kevin De Bruyne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Kane" title="Harry Kane">Harry Kane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virgil_van_Dijk" title="Virgil van Dijk">Virgil van Dijk</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Rooney" title="Wayne Rooney">Wayne Rooney</a>.  In 2018, Bennett made a 3-part NBC Sports television story on the history of <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Football_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Liverpool Football Club">Liverpool Football Club</a>, entitled “In the Shadow of the Kop”.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Promoted_Series">The Promoted Series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Bennett_(journalist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The Promoted Series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2017, Bennett launched an annual three-part television series on NBC, entitled ”The Promoted” where he visits the cities of every newly promoted Premier League teams to tell the story of their journey to the Premier League and the culture that surrounds the club. 
2017's episodes were "Promoted: Newcastle United" about <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United F.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>, "Brighton FC" about <a href="/wiki/Brighton_%26_Hove_Albion_F.C." title="Brighton &amp; Hove Albion F.C.">Brighton &amp; Hove Albion F.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and "Huddersfield," about <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town A.F.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>.
2018 featured "Wolves," about <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>, "Cardiff," about <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City F.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>, and "Fulham" about <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham F.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>. 
2019 had "Norwich City," about <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City F.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>,"Sheffield United," about <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_United_F.C." title="Sheffield United F.C.">Sheffield United F.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>, and "Aston Villa," about <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa F.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Podcasts">Podcasts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Bennett_(journalist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Podcasts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="American_Fiasco">American Fiasco</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Bennett_(journalist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: American Fiasco">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2018, Bennett partnered with WNYC to create American Fiasco,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> a 10-part series that followed the rise and fall of US Soccer’s World Cup dreams in 1998, when the American squad believed that after successfully hosting the 1994 tournament, nothing could stop them from going to the next one and winning it all.  The series was described as "part love letter to the American game, and part forensic accounting of the nation’s inability to fulfill its dreams in the global game."  It reached Number One on iTunes in its debut week.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Women_in_Blazers">Women in Blazers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Bennett_(journalist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Women in Blazers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bennett has taped films with US Women World Cup winners including Megan Rapinoe, Alex Morgan, Crystal Dunn, and Lindsey Horan.  In September, 2019, Bennett launched a podcast dedicated to the National Women’s Soccer League,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> which has featured interviews with Sam Kerr<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and Emily Sonnett<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wayne_Rooney_Podcast">Wayne Rooney Podcast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Bennett_(journalist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Wayne Rooney Podcast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After Wayne Rooney filmed a Men in Blazers Special with Rog<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>, the duo went on to tape a regular monthly podcast, "The Wayne Rooney Podcast" whilst Rooney was in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Books">Books</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Bennett_(journalist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Books">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bennett has published books on a slew of eclectic topics including <i>Bar Mitzvah Disco</i>, a book on Bar Mitzvah photographs of the 1980s, which he wrote with <a href="/wiki/Nick_Kroll" title="Nick Kroll">Nick Kroll</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> and <i>Everything You Know is Pong</i>, which he wrote with Eli Horowitz about table tennis.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_projects">Other projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Bennett_(journalist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Other projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, he created the architectural design competition <a href="/wiki/Sukkah_City" title="Sukkah City">Sukkah City</a> with journalist <a href="/wiki/Joshua_Foer" title="Joshua Foer">Joshua Foer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Sukkah_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sukkah-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Bennett is also co-founder of a record label, Idelsohn Society for Musical Preservation, that has tracked down lost music from the 1950s and 60s and reissued it including BAGELS AND BONGOS<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> and BLACK SABBATH.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, he wrote an op-ed in the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Post" class="mw-redirect" title="Washington Post">Washington Post</a> about <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Rooney" title="Wayne Rooney">Wayne Rooney</a><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> A lifelong fan of the Chicago WhiteSox, Bennett threw out the first pitch on August 29, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Bennett_(journalist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bennett married Vanessa Kroll, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Kroll_Inc." title="Kroll Inc.">Kroll Inc.</a> founder <a href="/wiki/Jules_Kroll" title="Jules Kroll">Jules Kroll</a>, in 2000. His brother-in-law is comedian <a href="/wiki/Nick_Kroll" title="Nick Kroll">Nick Kroll</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Bennett is Jewish.<sup id="cite_ref-Sukkah_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sukkah-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, he announced he would be becoming a naturalized US citizen based upon the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States men&#39;s national soccer team">United States men's national soccer team</a>'s progress in the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>. Bennett became a US citizen on 1 June 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>  Bennett is a longtime supporter of <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton F.C.</a>, which plays in the English <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>. Bennett is also a fan of the Chicago Bears and the Chicago White Sox.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> He is fond of Tracy Chapman and tweed.
</p>
