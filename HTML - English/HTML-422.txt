<p><b>Gatorland</b> is a 110-acre (45&#160;ha) <a href="/wiki/Theme_park" class="mw-redirect" title="Theme park">theme park</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wildlife_preserve" class="mw-redirect" title="Wildlife preserve">wildlife preserve</a> in <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, located along South <a href="/wiki/Orange_Blossom_Trail" class="mw-redirect" title="Orange Blossom Trail">Orange Blossom Trail</a> south of <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando</a>. It was founded 71 years ago by Owen Godwin on former cattle land in 1949,<sup id="cite_ref-gsbitrst_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gsbitrst-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and is privately owned by his family.
</p><p>Billed as the "Alligator Capital of the World," Gatorland features thousands of <a href="/wiki/Alligator" title="Alligator">alligators</a> (including rare <a href="/wiki/Leucism" title="Leucism">leucistic</a> alligators) and <a href="/wiki/Crocodile" title="Crocodile">crocodiles</a>, and many other animals. Attractions in the park include a breeding marsh with a boardwalk and observation tower, <a href="/wiki/Zip_line" title="Zip line">zip lines</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Off-roading" title="Off-roading">off-road</a> swamp vehicle tour, a <a href="/wiki/Ridable_miniature_railway" title="Ridable miniature railway">ridable miniature railroad</a>, alligator feeding shows, alligator wrestling shows, an <a href="/wiki/Aviary" title="Aviary">aviary</a>, a petting zoo, and educational programs. The breeding marsh area of the park was used in the filming of the <a href="/wiki/1984_in_film" title="1984 in film">1984</a> movie <i><a href="/wiki/Indiana_Jones_and_the_Temple_of_Doom" title="Indiana Jones and the Temple of Doom">Indiana Jones and the Temple of Doom</a></i>.
</p><p>The park is known for buying and rescuing nuisance alligators from trappers that would otherwise be killed for their meat and skin. Gatorland also manages the live alligator display at the <a href="/wiki/Gaylord_Palms" class="mw-redirect" title="Gaylord Palms">Gaylord Palms</a> resort in <a href="/wiki/Kissimmee,_Florida" title="Kissimmee, Florida">Kissimmee</a>.
</p>

<p>The Gatorland Express, known as <i>Ol' Iron Horse Express</i> prior to 2001,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/2_ft_and_600_mm_gauge_railways" title="2 ft and 600 mm gauge railways"><span class="nowrap">2&#160;ft</span></a> (<span class="nowrap">610&#160;mm</span>) <a href="/wiki/Narrow_gauge_railway" class="mw-redirect" title="Narrow gauge railway">narrow gauge</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> railroad attraction inside the park, which first opened in 1961 and was originally built by the <a href="/wiki/Allan_Herschell_Company" title="Allan Herschell Company">Allan Herschell Company</a>. The park claims that the Gatorland Express is the first amusement attraction in <a href="/wiki/Central_Florida" title="Central Florida">Central Florida</a>. The original locomotive was retired in 2000 and put on static display, while a brand-new locomotive built by Train Rides Unlimited<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> was purchased and put into operation the following year. The new locomotive is the same model as the locomotive used at the nearby <a href="/wiki/Green_Meadows_Petting_Farm" title="Green Meadows Petting Farm">Green Meadows Petting Farm</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> An additional fee is required to ride the railroad and prior to the 2011 opening of the zip line, it was the only non-animal-related attraction in Gatorland.
</p>
<div style="clear:left;"></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2006_fire">2006 fire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gatorland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2006 fire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shortly before sunrise on Monday, November 6, 2006, a three-alarm fire broke out at Gatorland,<sup id="cite_ref-gsunuiflm_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gsunuiflm-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> apparently started when a heating pad in one of the displays in the gift shop shorted out; <a href="/wiki/Arson" title="Arson">arson</a> was not suspected.<sup id="cite_ref-hbigfre_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbigfre-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The fire was brought under control within several hours, but the gift shop was completely destroyed, and several walkways also burned.<sup id="cite_ref-gbnsapll_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gbnsapll-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The fire killed a four-foot-long crocodile and two six-foot-long pythons, but spared the other animals.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gcriwks_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gcriwks-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> During the day, the birds that are displayed in and around the shop were moved to the aviary at the back of the park and were not injured.
</p><p>After inspectors confirmed that there was no structural damage to the various walkways and displays at the park, Gatorland reopened less than three weeks later, on the day after <a href="/wiki/Thanksgiving" title="Thanksgiving">Thanksgiving</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-greobcl_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-greobcl-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The gift shop and main offices were rebuilt as a two-story concrete block building, incorporating the repainted historic concrete alligator's mouth, and opened on May 22, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Zip_line_ride">Zip line ride</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gatorland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Zip line ride">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the summer of 2011, Gatorland added a zip line that travels across a pool of alligators and past several of the existing attractions. The ride is approximately 1,200 feet (370&#160;m) long and several stories high. It is available for riders 37 inches (94&#160;cm) tall or more. In January 2016, Gatorland made the zip line <a href="/wiki/Accessibility" title="Accessibility">wheelchair-accessible</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gator_Spot">Gator Spot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gatorland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Gator Spot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fun_Spot_America_Orlando_Gator_Spot.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Fun_Spot_America_Orlando_Gator_Spot.JPG/220px-Fun_Spot_America_Orlando_Gator_Spot.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Fun_Spot_America_Orlando_Gator_Spot.JPG/330px-Fun_Spot_America_Orlando_Gator_Spot.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Fun_Spot_America_Orlando_Gator_Spot.JPG/440px-Fun_Spot_America_Orlando_Gator_Spot.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3670" data-file-height="2399" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fun_Spot_America_Orlando_Gator_Spot.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Gator Spot entrance at Fun Spot America Orlando</div></div></div>
<p>Gatorland opened Gator Spot at <a href="/wiki/Fun_Spot_America_Theme_Parks" title="Fun Spot America Theme Parks">Fun Spot America Theme Parks</a>' Orlando park in the <a href="/wiki/International_Drive" title="International Drive">International Drive</a> tourist area on May 11, 2015. The $1 million attraction allows visitors to hold, take photos with, and feed alligators. The main attraction is a leucistic alligator named Bouya, a white gator with blue eyes.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
